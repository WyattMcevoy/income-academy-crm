## FAQ

**How much do I need to start?**
Most members start with $100-$300 for their first month of inventory. You need a smartphone for photos, a free eBay account, and a corner to stage items. That's it.

**What if I live in a rural area?**
Rural markets can actually be stronger than urban ones — less competition for the same goods. The course teaches route-planning for 30-60 minute radius markets and remote-sourcing strategies.

**What if my spouse hates clutter?**
We cover the "90-day inventory rule" that keeps things moving out as fast as they come in. If you can't dedicate at least one dedicated staging area, this might not be the right fit.

**Does the course cover taxes?**
Module 7 covers the basics: sales tax, Schedule C, the 1099-K threshold. We're not accountants — for your specific situation, talk to a tax pro. (Bookkeeping is one of the other 4 courses in the Foundation Pass, so you can learn both sides of the equation.)

**Is there an online version? Like selling on eBay without ever leaving the house?**
Arbitrage (buying online, selling on eBay) is a different skill — we touch on it in Module 2 but the core of this course is in-person sourcing. That's where the real margins are.

**When should I apply to the eBay Done-With-You program?**
When you're sourcing $2,000+/month in inventory and spending more time on photography/listing/shipping than on sourcing. Module 6 covers this in detail. Applications route through the Members Area — no separate signup.

**Does this work for someone who's never sold anything online?**
Yes. The course assumes zero eBay experience. Module 5 walks you through your first listing step-by-step. If you can upload a photo and type a description, you can do this.

### Reselling legal notes
Consult your state and local laws about secondhand dealers and resale licensing. Most states do not regulate casual reselling, but a few cities/counties require permits for recurring garage/yard sales held at your own address. This course does not address your specific legal situation — confirm with local authorities before scaling to a storefront or warehouse model.
