# Vintage Glassware — Quick Reference Cheatsheet

Pyrex, Fire King, Pyrexfire, and the glassware you'll see most at estate sales.

---

## Pyrex Corning USA (1915-1998 USA production)

The #1 category at American estate sales. Patterns vary wildly in value.

### High-value patterns (aim for these)
- **Snowflake Blue** (1958-1963): $50-300 per piece depending on size/piece
- **Butterprint** (1957-1968, amish print): $40-200
- **Gooseberry** (1957): $50-300
- **Pink Daisy** (1957-1962): $75-400
- **Eyes / Atomic Eyes** (promotional, 1950s): $100-500
- **Lucky in Love** (1959 promotional, rare): $500-3000+
- **Turquoise dinnerware** (1956-1959): $40-150 per piece

### Medium-value patterns
- **Primary Colors bowls** (red/blue/green/yellow, 1949-1970): $40-200 for complete set
- **Friendship** (orange print, 1971): $30-100
- **Spring Blossom / Crazy Daisy** (green, 1972-1979): $20-80
- **Old Orchard** (brown on beige, 1974-1976): $25-75

### Lower-value (skip unless dirt cheap)
- Clear Pyrex without color/pattern: $5-30 each
- Corning Ware (different product, ceramic not glass, 1958-present): $5-50

### Pattern identification
- Bottom stamp shows pattern name + size + "Made in USA" typically
- If no stamp, examine pattern + colors against the [Corelle pattern guide](https://corellecorner.com/pyrex-patterns-guide/)
- Reproductions exist: most modern Pyrex is made in China; older USA Pyrex shows age (slight utensil marks, pattern fade)

### Condition matters a lot
- **Excellent**: no utensil marks, full color, no chips → full value
- **Utensil marks**: reduce 20-40%
- **Dishwasher dulling** (faded pattern from years of cycles): reduce 40-60%
- **Chips**: reduce 50-80% depending on location
- **Cracks**: essentially unsellable except to crafters

### Complete sets are worth more than sum of parts
- Casserole with matching lid: +30-50% vs. casserole alone
- Nesting mixing bowl sets (4 sizes): +40% vs. individual

## Fire King (Anchor Hocking, 1942-1976)

Heat-proof glassware, often in white/milk glass or jadeite green.

### High-value items
- **Jadeite mixing bowls** (1940s-1950s): $75-400 per piece
- **Jadeite restaurant ware** (plates, cups for diners): $30-200
- **Peach Luster glassware** (iridescent, 1950s): $25-150
- **Jane Ray jadeite** dinnerware (full pattern): $30-150 per piece

### Medium-value
- **Milk glass mixing bowls** (1950s-60s Colonial series): $25-100
- **Turquoise blue** dinnerware (1956-1958): $20-80

### Identification
- Bottom stamp: "Fire-King" / "Fire King" / "Anchor Hocking"
- Jadeite is milky pale green — not bright green (that's uranium glass)
- USA-made stamp

## Other glassware worth knowing

### Depression Glass (1929-1939)
- Mostly pink, green, yellow, blue
- Makers: Jeannette, Hocking, Federal, Indian, Imperial
- Patterns matter: Cameo, Cherry Blossom, Madrid, Sharon
- Values: $10-200+ depending on pattern + piece + condition
- Beware reproductions: many common patterns have been reissued

### Carnival Glass (1907-1930)
- Iridescent finish, came in bowls, cups, vases
- Makers: Northwood (highest value), Fenton, Imperial, Dugan
- Identification: look for moulded maker's marks
- Values: $30-300 for typical pieces

### McCoy Pottery (1910-1990, Ohio)
- Not technically glass — art pottery — but common at estate sales
- High-value: Cookie jars ($50-400), vases with distinctive glazes
- Red flag: many reproductions exist, check maker's mark carefully

## What to do at the sale

1. Flip anything that looks like Pyrex over. Check bottom stamp.
2. Pattern name = immediate Google check
3. Is it in complete sets? Lids included?
4. Any chips, cracks, or severe fade?
5. Apply 40% rule: is asking price ≤ 40% of sold range?

## Quick value rules

- If stamped "Made in China": skip (modern reproduction)
- If no pattern name and clear glass: likely low value ($5-15 range)
- If colorful pattern on milky white: check pattern name — could be $50-300
- If jadeite green: check maker's mark — Anchor Hocking fire-king is real, unmarked jadeite is often Chinese repro
- If it looks TOO perfect for claimed age: likely repro
