# Estate Sale Sourcing Academy — Curriculum

8 modules. ~4-6 hours total runtime. Designed to be completed in 1-2 weeks with field-practice embedded.

---

## Module 0 — Welcome + Reality Check

**Runtime**: 15 min
**Objective**: Set honest expectations. Filter out wrong-fit buyers early.

- What this course is and isn't (reselling skill, not "passive income")
- Realistic numbers: $300-$1,500/month part-time is typical for year 1; some do more, most don't. Inventory sourcing is the ceiling.
- Who succeeds: patient, willing to drive, enjoys the hunt, has garage/spare room for inventory
- Who fails: needs fast money, hates clutter, won't haggle
- The bundle upgrade path: "when your sourcing gets good, the eBay DWY program scales it"

## Module 1 — How the Estate Sale Ecosystem Works

**Runtime**: 30 min

- Estate sales vs. garage sales vs. auctions vs. moving sales — pros and cons of each
- The people you'll meet: estate sale companies, downsizing services, surviving family, professional resellers, other "pickers"
- The calendar: Thursday-Saturday is the primary window; Sunday-Monday is the "half-off" day
- Pricing psychology at estate sales: what goes fast, what lingers, what the company prices too high
- **Assignment**: attend 1 estate sale this weekend, buy nothing, just observe and take notes

## Module 2 — Finding Sales Near You (The Weekly Rhythm)

**Runtime**: 40 min

- **EstateSales.net walkthrough** — the primary aggregator. Searches, saved alerts, photo previews
- Craigslist "garage sales" category — still viable in many metros
- Facebook: local buy/sell + estate sale groups
- Newspaper classifieds (still underrated in smaller markets)
- Route optimization: Google Maps + sale lists = Saturday morning efficiency
- The "preview night" — some companies offer Thursday preview for serious buyers
- **Deliverable**: your Saturday route spreadsheet (template provided)

## Module 3 — What to Look For: The 7 Categories

**Runtime**: 60 min (the biggest module)

- **Vintage tools** (Stanley, Starrett, Lie-Nielsen planes, Craftsman USA) — high ROI, easy to authenticate
- **Jewelry** (gold, silver, costume vs. fine, acid-test basics, WHEN to walk away)
- **Collectibles** (vintage toys, sports cards pre-1990, Disney animation, Pyrex, Fire King)
- **Electronics** (vintage audio gear, film cameras, vintage computers, video game consoles 1985-2005)
- **Glassware & pottery** (Pyrex patterns, Fiestaware, Depression glass, McCoy, Roseville)
- **Books** (first editions, signed copies, rare technical books, cookbooks)
- **Furniture** (mid-century modern, Eames, Knoll, Danish teak — and the long tail of boring stuff to avoid)
- **Category cheat sheets** with visual identification guides (template provided)

## Module 4 — Pricing, Negotiation, and the Exit Rule

**Runtime**: 40 min

- The 30-second phone check — Terapeak + eBay sold listings + Google Lens
- The 50/50 rule: if you can't get it for 50% of what it sells for, walk away (accounting for fees + shipping)
- The "last hour" strategy — companies cut prices hard in the final hour to avoid hauling
- Negotiation scripts (3 scripts: respectful bundle, polite decline, walk-away-return)
- When NOT to negotiate (rule: if priced right, pay it — relationships with estate companies matter)
- Emotional pricing traps: "I drove an hour so I have to buy something"

## Module 5 — The Pipeline: From Sale to Profit

**Runtime**: 50 min

- Staging area workflow (dedicate a room or garage zone)
- The photograph station: cheap lightbox, phone camera, consistent backdrop
- Listing workflow: eBay first (best pricing), then FB Marketplace, then Mercari
- Shipping 101: USPS Priority Flat Rate boxes, eBay label savings, fragile-packaging basics
- Title-writing conventions for eBay search visibility
- **AI Writing Assistant** — included Claude Project drafts listings, titles, and descriptions from photos
- Deliverable: list your first 10 items this week

## Module 6 — Scaling Up: When to Graduate to the eBay Done-With-You Program

**Runtime**: 30 min

- The ceiling of solo sourcing: ~$1,500-$2,500/month part-time, 25-40 hours/week full-time
- Signs you're ready for scale:
  - You're averaging 8+ sourced items/week
  - You have $2,000+ in ROI inventory
  - You're losing time to photography/listing/shipping
  - You want to expand beyond your local sourcing radius
- What the eBay DWY program does: warehouses your inventory, photographs professionally, lists on your behalf with expert titling, handles shipping + returns
- How to apply (internal funnel — leads to phone consultation, not shown publicly)
- Realistic ceiling with the DWY program: $5k-$15k/month (honest range, with disclaimer)

## Module 7 — Taxes, Tracking, and Not Becoming a Hoarder

**Runtime**: 30 min

- Sales tax: when you're a reseller, when you're not (state variations, resale certificates)
- Income tax: self-employment, Schedule C basics, when to talk to a CPA
- The 1099-K threshold (IRS rules as of 2026)
- Inventory tracking: simple spreadsheet template
- Mileage tracking: deductible if itemized; apps that do this automatically
- The psychological trap of "I might sell this someday" — the 90-day inventory rule
- Disposing of what doesn't sell (donations, lot-listings, estate sale RE-sale)

## Module 8 — Your 90-Day Plan

**Runtime**: 20 min

- Week 1-2: observe 3 sales, buy nothing, build your route spreadsheet
- Week 3-4: first sourcing trips, target $100 in inventory, list first 10 items
- Month 2: refine categories, track ROI by source, hit $500 in inventory
- Month 3: choose your specialization (the category you're best at), $1,000+ in inventory, evaluate readiness for eBay DWY
- The "when to quit" checklist — if you're 90 days in and hate it, refund is still available on the bundle (per the 7-day money-back guarantee you already used OR by canceling the $19/mo membership)
