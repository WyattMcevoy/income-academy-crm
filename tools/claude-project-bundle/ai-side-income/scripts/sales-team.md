# Sales Team Script — AI Side Income Starter → High-Ticket Launchpad

For phone/Zoom sales reps closing $47 buyers into the $2-5k "AI-Augmented Business Launchpad" back-end.

**Goal**: 15-20% close rate on qualified conversations, $3k average order value.

---

## Before the call (5 min prep)

- Pull the buyer's CRM record from Income Academy CRM
- Review: when they bought the $47 course, how far they've gotten in modules, any notes
- Check MailerLite engagement (are they opening emails? clicking?)
- Have your calendar open to book follow-ups
- Have the Launchpad offer sheet ready
- Have your Stripe high-ticket Payment Link ready

---

## Call structure (30-45 min total)

### 1. Opening (3 min)

```
"Hi [FIRST NAME] — this is [YOUR NAME] from Income Academy. We don't have a hard meeting scheduled, I just wanted to personally check in since you picked up the Starter Kit [N DAYS] ago. Got a few minutes?"

[If yes, continue. If no, book a specific time.]

"Great. Quick ground rule — this isn't a sales call. I'm going to ask you some questions about what you're trying to do, see if we're a fit to work more closely together. If we are, I'll tell you about that. If not, I'll give you some resources and let you get on with your day. Cool with that?"
```

### 2. Discovery (10-15 min)

The #1 rule: they talk 70%, you talk 30%.

```
"First — what made you jump on the Starter Kit? What's the real thing you're hoping to solve for yourself here?"

[LISTEN. Don't interrupt. Take notes.]

"What have you tried before? What worked, what didn't?"

"How far are you in the material?"
[If they've done most of it, they're more qualified. If not, softer close — offer support to finish first.]

"If you could wave a magic wand and have this side income thing really working in 6 months — what would that look like for you? How many clients? What kind of work? How much per month?"

[They're telling you their actual goal. Write it down verbatim.]

"What's in the way, as you see it?"

[Listen for: lack of confidence, lack of time, no clients, no portfolio, fear of sales, tech issues.]

"Anything on your plate right now — family, health, work — that would make the next 90 days hard to commit to something?"

[This is a qualifier. If they say yes, don't push the high-ticket.]
```

### 3. Teaching moment (5 min)

Based on what they told you, share ONE genuinely useful insight. Make them smarter, not just sold.

Examples based on common answers:

**If they said "I don't know where to find clients":**
> "Here's what I see most often. The Starter Kit covers the 3 paths to clients — Upwork, referrals, local outreach. The fastest win for most 50+ members is actually local outreach. Specifically: going down the list of chiropractors, dentists, or realtors in your zip code and sending a genuine, helpful email. Not scalable, but high conversion. It works because your demographic is known and trusted in your community — you can use that. Have you tried that yet? Why not?"

**If they said "I'm worried I'm not technical enough":**
> "Real talk — the tech isn't the problem. I have members who are 68, 72, never built anything online, who are now earning $2k/month doing this. The thing that gets people stuck isn't the tech — it's not having a specific person to ask 'is this good enough to send?' for their first few weeks. Once you've delivered 2-3 real projects, the technical confidence is cemented. The first few weeks are where people quit."

### 4. Transition to the offer (3 min)

Only do this if the fit is clear. If it's not, close warm and don't pitch.

```
"Based on what you've told me, I think you might be a good fit for our deeper program. Let me tell you about it and you can decide.

It's called the AI-Augmented Business Launchpad. It's a 90-day, done-with-you program. That means: you bring the effort, we bring a coach who's been through this exact journey.

Specifically you get:
- Weekly 1-on-1 calls for 12 weeks with a dedicated coach
- A custom AI assistant (Claude Project) built for YOUR specific niche and clients — so when you're doing client work, the AI already knows your voice, your client types, your scope
- Private feedback on your first 10 pieces of client work before they go out
- Direct introduction to 2 of our alumni for networking
- Access to our private member Slack for 6 months beyond the program
- All future Starter Kit updates forever

The investment is $3,997. Or payment plan: $1,500 down, $1,000/month for 3 months.

People do it when:
- They've gotten through the Starter Kit and want a coach, not just content
- They've tried to implement solo and hit a wall
- They want accountability more than information

Does that sound like what you need right now?"
```

### 5. Handling objections

**"That's more than I expected."**
```
"Totally fair. Let me put it this way: your goal was [REPEAT THEIR GOAL — from discovery]. 
Getting there by yourself, based on what we see with members who try, takes 8-12 months of stops and starts. With coaching, it's 2-3 months on average. 
The investment pays itself back when you close your 2nd or 3rd client. If you're comfortable with the 90-day work but the price is the blocker, the payment plan makes it $1,500 this month and $1,000 for 3 months after. Would that help?"
```

**"I need to think about it."**
```
"Totally. What specifically do you need to think through? Usually when folks say that, it's one of two things — either it's the money, or they're not sure this is the right moment. Which one is it for you?"

[Let them answer. Address the real thing. Don't keep pushing if they're genuinely not ready.]
```

**"I need to talk to my spouse / partner."**
```
"Of course. When are you going to have that conversation? What's your spouse's biggest concern going to be — money, time, or something else?

Here's what I'd do: I'll send you a 1-page summary of the program and a note specifically for your partner that covers the common questions. Schedule 30 minutes with them tonight or tomorrow. Then let's set up a 15-min call Thursday to make the decision — sound good?"
```

**"Can I pay in more installments?"**
```
"We can do 5 installments — $800 up front, then $800/month for 4 months, totaling $4,000 instead of $3,997 to cover the extra billing. It's the most stretched-out version we do. Would that fit?"
```

**"I want to try the Starter Kit more first."**
```
"Totally fair. How far are you right now? [LISTEN]

Here's my honest opinion: if you're under Module 4, yes, finish those first. If you're past Module 5, you've got the foundation — the Launchpad accelerates the application, which is usually where people get stuck.

Let's do this: can you send me a note when you hit Module 7? I'll hold one Launchpad spot open for 30 days — no pressure, you can ignore it. But I want to make sure you don't miss the next cohort."

[This is both genuine AND a soft scarcity close.]
```

**"I don't have time right now."**
```
"That's the most honest answer of any of these. The 90-day program is about 5-7 hours a week — 2 hours of coaching calls + 3-5 of practice work. 
If you genuinely don't have 5 hours a week for the next 12 weeks, this isn't the right moment. Will it be in 2-3 months?"

[If no, close warm. If yes, book a future call.]
```

### 6. Close (2 min)

If they're in:

```
"Great. Let me walk you through the next steps:
1. I'm going to send you a link to pay [amount] right now
2. Once that goes through, I'll introduce you to your coach — probably within 24-48 hours
3. You and your coach will schedule your first call within a week
4. You'll get Slack access today
5. Everything else, your coach will guide you through

Any questions before I send the link?"
```

If they're not ready:

```
"Totally fine. Here's what I'd do: finish the Starter Kit. When you hit Module 8 and start sending cold outreach, email me. I'll check in — no pressure — and we can see where you are then.

In the meantime, what's ONE thing you're going to do this week?"

[Get a commitment to a specific action. Follow up in 1 week.]
```

### 7. Book the follow-up (always)

Whether they bought or not:

```
"Let me put one more follow-up on the calendar. [DATE, 1-2 weeks out.] I'll send you a calendar invite."
```

## Scripts for common scenarios

### "I bought the Starter Kit but haven't opened it"

```
"Totally normal — about 30% of buyers take 2-3 weeks to dive in. No judgment. What I'm curious about: what was going on in your life that made you pull the trigger when you did? Something triggered it — what was it?"

[Get to the emotional reason behind the purchase. That's the lever for everything else.]
```

### "I finished the Starter Kit and I have 2 clients already"

```
"That's fast. What'd you do differently than most people?"

[Let them talk. They want to.]

"Here's the thing — at your current pace, you're going to hit the ceiling of solo freelancing within 6 months. The Launchpad is built for exactly what you're in — scaling past 3 clients without burning out or lowering quality. Want to talk about it?"
```

### "I'm interested but my spouse isn't supportive"

```
"That's a real issue — harder than a money objection. Let me ask: what specifically doesn't your spouse support?"

[Listen for real concern: money, time, "another shiny object syndrome," fear of failure.]

"I can give you specific language to address that. And here's the honest truth — if your spouse is against it, you shouldn't do this. A program that costs $4k and 90 days of your time isn't worth putting your relationship under stress. Start with the hardest conversation first. What's at the root of their concern?"

[Some genuine coaching on the relationship issue. You may end up losing the sale. That's fine — you'll sleep better.]
```

## Disqualifiers — when to NOT sell

**Never sell the Launchpad to someone who:**
- Can't afford it (they said directly or the math is clear — $4k would put them in debt)
- Is in a current life crisis (divorce, illness, job loss) — unless they say "this is my lifeline"
- Haven't finished even Module 1 of the Starter Kit after 30+ days — they don't take action
- Is doing this for someone else ("my husband thinks I should...") — not their choice
- Is chasing get-rich-quick expectations even after all our reality-checking

In these cases, close warm, offer the free resources (community, MailerLite continuity), and set a follow-up.

## Metrics to track (per rep)

- Calls scheduled
- Calls conducted (no-show rate)
- Qualified conversations (met qualification criteria)
- Offers made
- Closes
- Average close value (track payment plan vs pay-in-full)
- Follow-up conversions (second-call closes)

### Healthy baselines
- 40-60% of scheduled calls actually happen
- 60-80% of those are "qualified" (meaning they should hear the offer)
- 15-25% of qualified conversations close on the first call
- Another 10-15% close on follow-up
- Total cold-to-close rate: 20-30% of qualified calls

If you're below 15% cold-to-close, something's off. Usually: offer positioning, discovery questions, or qualification.

---

## Ethics and compliance

- **Never overpromise.** Your credibility is your asset.
- **Never pressure a "no."** If they're not ready, don't push.
- **Never imply the price is going up tomorrow if it isn't.**
- **Never imply seats are limited if they aren't.**
- **Always disclose** that results vary and many members don't reach income goals.
- **Always honor the refund policy** without argument.

Reps who get accounts in trouble are the ones who cut these corners. Your job is to help the right people buy, not to sell everyone.
