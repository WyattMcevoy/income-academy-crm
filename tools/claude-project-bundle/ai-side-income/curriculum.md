# AI Side Income Starter Kit — Full Curriculum

**Total runtime target**: 4-6 hours of video, split across 8 modules + intro + conclusion.
**Format**: talking head for context/teaching, screen recordings for tool demos.
**Tone**: honest, grounded, no hype. Speak to a 60-year-old who's smart but not technical.

---

## Module 0 — Welcome + Setup (15 min)

**Goal**: set expectations, establish honesty, get them to open ChatGPT and Claude for the first time.

**Cover**:
- Who this is for (and who it isn't)
- What you will and won't learn
- The honest earnings disclaimer (say it early, get it out of the way)
- Set up ChatGPT (free) + Claude (free tier)
- Brief tour of both interfaces

**Key talking points**:
- "This isn't a shortcut. AI is a tool. Tools require practice."
- "If you're looking for 'make $5k in your first week,' this isn't that course. Refund now — I won't take it personally."
- "If you're looking for real skills you can get paid for, stick around."

**Homework**: Create free accounts on ChatGPT and Claude. Have a conversation with each for 10 minutes about anything.

## Module 1 — Your AI Assistants, Explained (30 min)

**Goal**: demystify what AI tools actually are, so the demo stops being scared of them.

**Cover**:
- What Large Language Models actually do (predict the next word, explained like you're talking to your parent)
- Why they hallucinate (and how to spot it)
- The 3 tools everyone should know: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — strengths of each
- What they're good at (writing, summarizing, brainstorming, organizing)
- What they're bad at (current events, math, citing sources without making them up)

**Screen recording demo**:
- Same prompt to ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini side by side
- Show how answers differ
- Show the "I don't know" test — ask about something obscure, watch the hallucination

**Key talking points**:
- "Think of it as a really confident intern. Useful, not infallible."
- "Always verify facts. Never copy-paste without reading."
- "You're going to make mistakes using it. That's normal."

## Module 2 — Talking to AI (The Skill That Makes Everything Work) (45 min)

**Goal**: teach prompting fundamentals. This is the skill — everything else is application.

**Cover**:
- The 4-part prompt structure: **Role + Context + Task + Format**
- Why "write a blog post" gets garbage but "You're a gardening writer with 10 years experience. Write a 500-word beginner's guide to hydrangeas in Zone 7. Use a warm, neighborly tone with one story from personal experience." gets usable output
- The "give it examples" technique (few-shot prompting)
- The "ask it to ask you questions first" trick
- When to start over vs. when to refine

**Screen recording demo**:
- Bad prompt → bad output (show the disappointment)
- Same task with good prompt → good output
- Iterative refinement of a real client email

**Exercises**:
- Rewrite 3 vague prompts from Exercise Sheet 1 using the 4-part structure
- Generate three versions of a cold outreach email and pick the best one

**Key talking points**:
- "Your output is only as good as your input. If you're getting garbage, the problem is on your side."
- "AI won't read your mind. Tell it everything."

## Module 3 — Five AI-Ready Side Hustles That Work for Beginners (60 min)

**Goal**: give them 5 real service offerings they can sell, with realistic rates.

**Cover each in depth** (8-10 min per):

### 3.1 Writing & Editing Assistant
- What it is: helping small businesses with blog posts, newsletters, product descriptions
- Realistic rate: $150-500 per article for a 50+ writer with AI assistance
- Ideal client: a 3-10 person local service business or a solopreneur
- AI tools used: Claude for drafting, Grammarly for polish
- Time per gig: 2-3 hours

### 3.2 Bookkeeping Support (not full bookkeeping)
- What it is: receipt categorization, expense report drafting, invoice generation for small operators
- Realistic rate: $25-40/hour or $200-400/month retainer
- Ideal client: Etsy seller, freelancer, small contractor
- AI tools used: ChatGPT for categorization, Claude for memo writing, Google Sheets
- Time per gig: 4-8 hours/month per client
- Compliance note: **not CPA work. Not giving tax advice.**

### 3.3 Social Media Ghostwriting
- What it is: drafting LinkedIn/Facebook posts for busy professionals
- Realistic rate: $300-800/month retainer for 12 posts
- Ideal client: realtor, consultant, local attorney
- AI tools used: Claude for voice-matching drafts
- Time per gig: 3-5 hours/week per client

### 3.4 Customer Email Triage
- What it is: answering repetitive customer service emails for a small business owner
- Realistic rate: $15-25/hour
- Ideal client: Etsy/eBay seller with 20+ emails/day, local service owner
- AI tools used: Claude for draft responses, Gmail, spreadsheet tracker
- Time per gig: 1-2 hours/day per client

### 3.5 Transcription + Note Organization
- What it is: turning meeting recordings into organized notes, action items, summaries
- Realistic rate: $40-80 per hour of audio
- Ideal client: realtor, therapist, consultant, course creator
- AI tools used: Otter.ai or Whisper for transcription, Claude for summarization
- Time per gig: 30-45 min per audio hour

**Key talking points**:
- "These are all real services real people pay for. You're not inventing anything."
- "Pick one. Don't try to do all five at once."
- "Quality > Volume. Three great clients beats fifteen mediocre ones."

## Module 4 — Landing Your First 3 Clients (60 min)

**Goal**: remove "but I don't know where to find clients" as an excuse.

**Cover**:

### 4.1 The 5 proven places to find your first clients

1. **Upwork** — set up a profile (walk them through), bid on 2-3 jobs/day
2. **Referrals from your existing network** — email template to let your friends/contacts know what you now offer
3. **Local small business outreach** — pick a niche (chiropractors, plumbers, realtors), find 20 local ones, send a personal email
4. **Facebook groups for small business owners** — helpful participation → relationships → offers
5. **Existing clients of accountants, lawyers, designers** — these professionals often know their clients need help, refer if you build a relationship

### 4.2 The anatomy of a good outreach email

Template in `templates/cold-outreach-sequence.md`. Walk through why each line works.

### 4.3 The first discovery call

- How to show up (calm, curious, not salesy)
- 5 questions to ask to understand their real problem
- When to quote a price vs when to hold off
- Script: `templates/discovery-call-script.md`

### 4.4 Handling "how much do you charge?"

- Why you should almost never be the one to name the first price
- When you do have to, use the range technique

**Screen recording demo**:

- Rewrite a cold email from a student in the demo audience

## Module 5 — Doing the Work (With AI, Not Replaced by AI) (45 min)

**Goal**: show the actual workflow for delivering a paid gig using AI tools.

**Cover**:

### 5.1 The "AI as co-pilot, human as pilot" principle
Never submit raw AI output. Always edit. The client is paying for your judgment, not the model's.

### 5.2 Full workflow example: "A blog post for a local chiropractor"
- Client brief intake (template in `templates/client-intake.md`)
- Research with Claude + fact-check
- Outline with Claude
- Draft with Claude
- Human edit pass
- Polish with Grammarly
- Client delivery (template in `templates/delivery-email.md`)

### 5.3 Full workflow example: "A month of LinkedIn posts for a realtor"
- Voice interview recording (30 min)
- Transcript + pattern extraction with Claude
- Topic brainstorm with Claude
- Draft 12 posts with Claude matching their voice
- Schedule in Buffer or ship as draft list

### 5.4 Quality-control checklist
- Fact-check every specific claim
- Read aloud — does it sound like the client?
- Did I add genuine value beyond what Claude would have said generically?
- Is there anything a client could object to? (tone, accuracy, style)

**Screen recording demo**:
- Full blog post workflow in real time, ~20 min

**Key talking points**:
- "If your client could have just used ChatGPT themselves, they will eventually. Your job is to add what ChatGPT can't."
- "The human editing pass is 30% of the time and 70% of the quality."

## Module 6 — Pricing Your Time (30 min)

**Goal**: stop them from undercharging, which is the #1 killer of beginner freelance attempts.

**Cover**:

### 6.1 Three pricing models
1. **Hourly** — simple, but caps your income. Good for first month only.
2. **Project** — most common, most flexible
3. **Retainer** — the goal. Stable income, lower sales effort.

### 6.2 The beginner pricing mistake
Charging $15/hour "because I'm new." Resulting in:
- Clients assume low quality
- You can't afford to deliver quality
- You burn out in 2 months

### 6.3 The confidence ladder
- Month 1: $30-50/hour project-equivalent
- Month 3: $50-75/hour
- Month 6: $75-100/hour
- Month 12+: $100-150/hour OR retainer $1500-3000/client/month

### 6.4 How to raise rates with existing clients
Template in `templates/rate-increase-email.md`. Walk through the 60-day notice approach.

### 6.5 Value-based pricing primer
For advanced use — skip in module 6 unless they ask.

## Module 8 — Your 90-Day Plan (30 min)

**Goal**: end with a concrete week-by-week roadmap.

**Cover — week by week**:

### Days 1-7
- Complete Modules 1-3
- Pick ONE service (from module 3) to lead with
- Set up Upwork profile
- List 20 local businesses in your niche

### Days 8-21
- Complete Modules 4-5
- Send 3 cold outreach emails per day
- Bid on 2-3 Upwork jobs per day
- Run 2-3 discovery calls per week

### Days 22-45
- Land your first client (goal)
- Deliver excellent work (use Module 5 workflow)
- Get a testimonial
- Raise rates for client #2

### Days 46-90
- Target: 3 active clients
- Raise rates
- Start saying no
- Consider: add a second service OR double down on first

**Key talking points**:
- "Most people quit at Day 30 because they haven't landed a client yet. The ones who land a client in month 3 are the ones who sent 100 cold emails by then."
- "Reply to the week-12 email. Tell me what happened. I read every one."
