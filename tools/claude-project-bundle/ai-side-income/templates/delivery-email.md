# Delivery Email — Sending Completed Work

Template for handing off finished work. Strong delivery emails are a huge part of why clients renew. Bad delivery emails (or no email — just a file link) make clients wonder if they got their money's worth.

---

## Standard delivery (single deliverable)

```
Subject: Delivered: [PROJECT / ASSET NAME]

Hi [NAME],

Here's [WHAT YOU'RE DELIVERING — specific]:

📎 [LINK or ATTACHMENT] — [FILE DESCRIPTION, e.g., "Final draft, 1,200 words"]

What I did:
- [KEY ACTION 1 — specific, not generic]
- [KEY ACTION 2]
- [KEY ACTION 3]

What to look at first:
- [SPECIFIC THING — e.g., "The intro on page 1 is where I leaned into the 'plain spoken' voice we talked about. Tell me if it lands."]

What I'm not sure about:
- [ANY DECISIONS YOU WANT FEEDBACK ON — e.g., "The callout box on page 3 — I was torn between two approaches. Happy to swap if this one doesn't feel right."]

Next step: [BE CLEAR — e.g., "Reply with feedback by [DATE] and I'll do a revision pass," or "Reply 'approved' if you're happy and I'll invoice."]

As always, let me know if you have questions.

[YOUR FIRST NAME]
```

## Monthly retainer recap (multiple deliverables)

```
Subject: [MONTH] recap — everything shipped + what's ahead

Hi [NAME],

Wrapping up [MONTH]. Here's what we did and what's coming:

### What shipped this month
- ✅ [DELIVERABLE 1 — specific, with link]
- ✅ [DELIVERABLE 2 — specific, with link]
- ✅ [DELIVERABLE 3 — specific, with link]
- ✅ [KEY MEETING or DECISION]

### Notable wins or changes
- [ONE THING WORTH CELEBRATING — engagement numbers, client feedback, a problem solved]
- [ANOTHER]

### Heads-up for next month
- [UPCOMING MILESTONE or SHIFT]
- [SEASONAL / EVENT CONSIDERATION]

### Your input needed
- [SPECIFIC THING YOU NEED FROM THEM — e.g., "Approve the Feb editorial calendar by Friday so I can get a head start"]
- [ANOTHER ASK — or "nothing, we're in good shape"]

### One question
[SOMETHING INTENTIONAL — e.g., "Would it be useful to add [NEW THING] next month? Or stay focused on [CURRENT SCOPE]?"]

Hope [MONTH] went well on your end too. Happy to hop on a call if any of this is easier voice-to-voice.

[YOUR FIRST NAME]
```

## Big milestone delivery (launch / major project)

```
Subject: [PROJECT NAME] is live / shipped / in your hands

Hi [NAME],

We did it. [PROJECT] is [LIVE / DELIVERED / APPROVED BY LEGAL — whatever "done" means].

### What's final:
- [LINK / FILE]
- [LINK / FILE]
- [LINK / FILE]

### Key decisions we made along the way
- We chose [X] over [Y] because [REASON]
- We cut [THING] for scope reasons and parked it for phase 2
- We added [NEW THING] because [REASON]

### What I'm proud of
[ONE OR TWO SPECIFIC THINGS — be honest, don't humble-brag. This is a project-pride moment.]

### What I'd do differently
[ONE THING — shows self-awareness, builds trust for future work]

### What's next — options
- [OPTION A — e.g., "Maintenance mode: monthly updates at $X/month"]
- [OPTION B — e.g., "Phase 2: the features we parked for scope. Fresh quote whenever you're ready."]
- [OPTION C — e.g., "Just enjoy the launch — no next step required"]

Thanks for trusting me with this. Genuinely enjoyed the work.

[YOUR FIRST NAME]
```

## Why these work

### 1. They preview what to look at first
Clients have 17 other things on their plate. Telling them "look at page 3 first" saves them 20 minutes and shows you thought about their time.

### 2. They invite feedback, not just validation
"What I'm not sure about" invites real feedback. "Let me know what you think" invites "looks great!" which isn't helpful.

### 3. They state next steps clearly
"Reply approved" or "by Friday" removes ambiguity. Ambiguous emails sit in inboxes.

### 4. They include context, not just files
Dropping a file link with no commentary feels transactional. Showing your thinking feels like partnership.

### 5. They end with personality
One sentence of warmth at the end ("genuinely enjoyed the work") builds the relationship. Clients buy your work AND your sanity to work with.

## What NOT to do

- **Don't over-apologize** ("sorry for the delay"). Once is enough if it happened.
- **Don't list every small thing you did.** Summarize; don't resume-dump.
- **Don't ask for a testimonial in the same email.** Wait 2-3 days after they've reviewed and said something positive.
- **Don't send at 11pm.** Unless you've agreed otherwise, mornings read better. Schedule if you work nights.

## Timing

- **Same day** as completion if possible. Momentum matters.
- **Not Friday afternoon** (weekend kills momentum). Monday morning is better if you finished Friday.
- **Before invoicing** — deliver first, invoice same day or next. Clients pay faster when the work is fresh.
