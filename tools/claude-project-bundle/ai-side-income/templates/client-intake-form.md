# Client Intake Form

Send within 24 hours of a signed contract. Collects everything you need to actually start the work. Use a Google Form, Typeform, or simple doc — whichever they'll actually fill out.

---

## Intake form questions

### Part 1: About you

1. **Full name + title**
2. **Business name**
3. **Website URL**
4. **Best email for working communication**
5. **Phone number (for urgent only)**
6. **Preferred communication channel** (email / Slack / SMS / WhatsApp)
7. **Time zone + typical working hours**
8. **Is there anyone else who will need to review / approve work?** (name + email)

### Part 2: About your business

9. **Describe your business in 2-3 sentences (as if I'm a smart stranger)**
10. **Who is your target customer?** (age, situation, what they want, what frustrates them)
11. **What do you sell and at what price points?**
12. **How long have you been in business?**
13. **What makes you different from competitors?**
14. **What's the one thing you want people to feel when they interact with your brand?**

### Part 3: About this project specifically

15. **In your own words: what are we actually accomplishing in this engagement?**
16. **What does "done well" look like to you?** (be specific)
17. **What would make this engagement a disaster?**
18. **Have you worked with someone in my role before? What worked/didn't?**
19. **Any deadlines I should know about?** (launches, events, seasonal windows)
20. **Is there anyone on your team I'll work with directly?**

### Part 4: Access + materials

21. **What tools / logins will I need access to?** (list each + who gives access)
22. **Do you have a brand guide or style guide?** (upload if yes)
23. **Do you have samples of work you like (from any source)?** (links)
24. **Do you have samples of work you DON'T like?** (links — this is as useful as the "likes")
25. **What's your logo file / color palette / fonts?** (upload)
26. **Prior content, case studies, or past work I should read?** (links)

### Part 5: Voice + tone (for content work specifically)

27. **5-10 words that describe your brand voice** (e.g., warm, direct, playful, expert, neighborly)
28. **What should we NEVER say or do in your voice?**
29. **Are there phrases or words specific to your niche I should use?**
30. **Are there phrases or words that would immediately sound wrong?**

### Part 6: Working rhythm

31. **How often do you want check-ins?** (weekly / bi-weekly / as-needed)
32. **What's the best day/time for our recurring call?**
33. **How do you like to receive drafts?** (inline email / Google Doc / etc.)
34. **How quickly do you typically give feedback?** (1 day / 3 days / 1 week)
35. **Is there any time of year I should know you'll be slow / fast / traveling?**

### Part 7: The honest stuff

36. **What's a concern or fear you have going into this engagement?**
37. **What would make you want to renew or refer me to others?**
38. **Anything else I should know that I haven't asked about?**

---

## How to use

1. Send within 24 hours of signed contract
2. Give them 3-5 days to complete
3. Read it thoroughly before the kickoff call
4. Save a copy — refer back anytime you're unsure of their preferences
5. Re-run (questions 15-38 only) annually for long-term clients — their priorities change

## Tips

### Don't skip "the honest stuff" section
Questions 36-38 are the most valuable. Nine times out of ten, clients tell you their real concern here — something they wouldn't have volunteered in the sales call. Read these twice before starting.

### Expect some questions to come back sparse
Clients won't always know the answers. That's fine. "Not sure yet" tells you what you need to figure out together.

### For simpler engagements, cut sections
- Content work only: sections 1-5 are essential. Section 7 is gold.
- Admin / bookkeeping: sections 1-3 and 6, skip voice-focused.
- One-off projects: sections 1-4 only.

### Store responses systematically
Create a client folder in Google Drive / Notion / your file system with:
- Signed contract
- This completed intake
- Voice profile doc (if content work)
- Running log of decisions
- Asset files

Makes handoff, renewal, and anniversary reviews much easier.
