# Discovery Call Script (45 min)

Use this the first time you talk to a prospective client. Goal: understand their situation deeply enough to decide if it's a fit, and quote a realistic price.

## Before the call (10 min prep)

- [ ] Research their business (website, LinkedIn, one quick Google)
- [ ] Write down 3 things you noticed that might matter
- [ ] Have a blank doc open for notes
- [ ] Close all tabs except Zoom/Meet + note doc
- [ ] Get water

## The call structure

### Opening (3 min)

```
"Hi [NAME], thanks for taking the time today. Just so we use this well — my plan is to spend about 30 minutes understanding what you're working on, and then maybe 10 minutes talking about whether and how I could help. Does that work?"

[Wait for confirmation]

"If at any point you realize this isn't a fit, say so — no hurt feelings. And I'll be direct if I don't think I'm the right person for what you need."
```

### Context questions (10 min)

These get them talking. You mostly listen.

```
- "Tell me about your business. What do you do and who for?"
- "How did you end up doing this? What's the short origin story?"
- "Who else is on the team, if anyone?"
- "What's going well right now? What's working?"
```

**Listening for:**
- Size of business
- Stage (scrappy, growing, mature)
- Their personality / how they communicate
- Whether they're the actual decision maker

### Problem questions (15 min)

The heart of the call.

```
- "What made you reach out / respond to my email?"
- "What's the thing you're trying to solve? Walk me through it."
- "How long has this been an issue?"
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "Why didn't those things work?"
- "What does it cost you — time, money, stress — to leave this unsolved?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would this look like 6 months from now?"
```

**Listening for:**
- Actual pain (not theoretical "would be nice")
- Budget signals (are they losing $X/month? paying someone $X currently?)
- Timeline urgency
- Decision criteria

### Fit questions (5 min)

```
- "Who else would be involved in this decision?"
- "What's your timeline for getting started — if you decide to?"
- "Do you have a budget range in mind? Or would it help if I shared typical ranges?"
- "Have you worked with someone like me before? What was that like?"
```

### Offer (5 min — only if clear fit)

If they're a clear fit and you have enough info:

```
"Based on what you've described, here's how I'd approach this:
[BRIEF — 2-3 sentences describing your plan]

For something like this, the typical investment is $[RANGE — low to high].

To be specific about what it'd be for you, I'd want to put together a proper proposal. I can have that over by [DATE]. Would that work?"
```

If they push for a specific number immediately, it's OK to say:

```
"I'll give you a real number in the proposal. What I don't want to do is shoot from the hip and either underquote and resent it, or overquote and scare you off. Give me 48 hours and I'll send something I stand behind."
```

### Close (2 min)

```
"Great — so to recap:
- Here's what I heard as the main issue: [SUMMARIZE]
- Here's my next step: [PROPOSAL BY DATE]
- Here's what I'd want from you: [ANYTHING — access, samples, a doc, etc.]

Does that all match?"
```

## Things to listen for (red flags)

**Don't take the project if:**
- They repeatedly talk about how someone else screwed up (victim framing)
- They want to negotiate your rate in the first 5 minutes
- They're evasive about budget or decision-making authority
- The scope keeps expanding mid-call
- They want you to start before signing / paying
- You get a "vibe" — trust it. Bad clients announce themselves.

**Don't take the project (yet) if:**
- They haven't clearly decided to solve this problem — they're still exploring
- Budget is genuinely not there for the quality they want
- Timeline is impossible

**Do take the project if:**
- They can articulate the problem clearly
- They've tried things and learned from them
- They understand this is an investment, not a cost
- They respect your time on the call
- Budget fits your target rate

## Common objections + quick responses

**"That's more than I expected."**
"Help me understand — what did you have in mind?" (Listen. Don't defend your price.)

**"Can you do it cheaper if I give you more work?"**
"Volume discounts on retainers, yes. Reducing the per-piece price, no — the time per piece is what it is."

**"I need to think about it."**
"Of course. What's on your mind? I might be able to help you think through it."

**"I want to compare quotes."**
"Makes sense. If you'd like, I can point you to 2-3 other people I respect in this space."

**"Can you start now and I'll send the paperwork later?"**
"I can't start without the paperwork signed and the first payment in — that's been my policy since a client stiffed me 3 years ago. I can turn it around same-day."

## What you're really selling

Not the deliverable. You're selling:
- The relief of them not having to think about this anymore
- The confidence that it'll be done right
- The taste/judgment they're paying for, not just the time

Price accordingly.
