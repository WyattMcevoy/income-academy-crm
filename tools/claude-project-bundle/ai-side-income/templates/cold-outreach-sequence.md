# Cold Outreach — 3-Email Sequence

Use this when reaching out to local businesses, small business owners, or prospects you don't know yet. Full sequence typically spans 10-14 days.

## Email 1 — Initial reach (day 0)

```
Subject: Quick question about [BUSINESS NAME]

Hi [FIRST NAME],

I came across [SPECIFIC SOMETHING YOU NOTICED — their website, a LinkedIn post, a mention, etc.]. [ONE-SENTENCE GENUINE OBSERVATION — not flattery, not generic. Something you actually noticed.]

I help [YOUR TYPE OF CLIENT] with [YOUR SERVICE]. I'm wondering if [SPECIFIC PROBLEM YOU SUSPECT THEY HAVE] is something on your radar right now.

For example, when I worked with [RELEVANT REFERENCE — past client or hypothetical], we [SPECIFIC THING YOU DID] which led to [SPECIFIC OUTCOME].

Open to a 15-minute call this week to see if there's a fit?

[YOUR FIRST NAME]
[PHONE]
[WEBSITE OR LINKEDIN]
```

### Rules for Email 1
- Max 120 words
- First sentence references something specific to them
- No "I hope this email finds you well"
- Don't attach anything
- Sign off with first name only

### Template variables to fill
- `[BUSINESS NAME]` — their company
- `[FIRST NAME]` — decision-maker's first name
- `[SPECIFIC SOMETHING YOU NOTICED]` — prove you did 2 minutes of research
- `[YOUR SERVICE]` — one specific offering, not a menu
- `[SPECIFIC PROBLEM]` — narrow enough to sound informed
- `[RELEVANT REFERENCE]` — real past client (anonymized if needed) or honestly-framed hypothetical

---

## Email 2 — Follow-up (day 4-5)

Only send if email 1 got no response.

```
Subject: re: [SAME SUBJECT AS EMAIL 1]

Hi [FIRST NAME],

Quick follow-up in case it got buried.

Since I wrote, I was thinking about [SPECIFIC THING] — [ONE NEW INSIGHT, RESOURCE, OR IDEA YOU DIDN'T MENTION FIRST TIME].

Still happy to have a 15-minute call if useful. If not the right time, no worries.

[YOUR FIRST NAME]
```

### Rules for Email 2
- Max 60 words
- Add one NEW thing — a thought, a resource, an insight
- Don't apologize for re-reaching out
- Don't say "just bumping this up" or "circling back"

---

## Email 3 — Polite closure (day 10-14)

Only send if emails 1 and 2 got no response.

```
Subject: One last note, [FIRST NAME]

I'll stop cluttering your inbox.

If [YOUR SERVICE / WHAT YOU DO] ever becomes relevant, feel free to reach out. I'll leave the door open.

Either way — [SPECIFIC WISH FOR THEM — e.g., "wishing you a strong Q3," or "hope the [SPECIFIC PROJECT OF THEIRS] lands well"].

[YOUR FIRST NAME]
[WEBSITE OR LINKEDIN]
```

### Rules for Email 3
- Max 50 words
- Sincere, not passive-aggressive
- No guilt-trip ("I guess you're not interested")
- Leaves the door open

---

## Sequence guidance

### Timing
- Email 1: Day 0, Tuesday-Thursday, 7-10am local time for the prospect
- Email 2: Day 4 or 5
- Email 3: Day 10-14

### Subject line tips
- Keep it lowercase and informal ("quick question about [business]")
- Avoid "[Service] for [Business]?" — looks pitchy
- Personalize with business name if possible

### Volume rules
- Send 5-10 cold outreach emails per day maximum (quality over quantity)
- Batch research — research 10 businesses at once, write emails for all 10 at once
- Track in a simple spreadsheet: Name, Business, Date sent, Date followed up, Response

### Reply rate expectations
- 3-5% reply rate is normal for cold outreach (assuming good targeting + personalization)
- Of those replies, about 30-50% turn into calls
- Of those calls, about 20-40% convert to paying clients
- Math: 100 cold emails → 4 replies → 2 calls → 0.5 clients

### When to stop
- If 50 cold emails in a niche produce zero calls, the niche or pitch is off
- Pause. Re-evaluate. Don't double down on a broken approach.

## Compliance reminders (US, as of 2026)

- CAN-SPAM: include your mailing address in the footer if you're cold-emailing at volume
- Unsubscribe: if running a campaign through a CRM, include an opt-out mechanism
- GDPR (if targeting EU): you need legitimate interest or explicit consent — consult an attorney if relevant
- State privacy laws (CCPA, etc.): be careful targeting specific demographic/protected groups
