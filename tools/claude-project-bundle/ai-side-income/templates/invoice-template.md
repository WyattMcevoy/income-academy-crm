# Invoice Template

For freelance invoicing. Keep it simple and professional.

---

```

From:
[YOUR BUSINESS NAME]
[YOUR ADDRESS]
[YOUR EMAIL]
[YOUR PHONE — optional]

[CLIENT BUSINESS NAME]
[CLIENT CONTACT]
[CLIENT ADDRESS]

Invoice #: [001 — increment sequentially]
```

## Recommended tools

Don't hand-generate invoices. Use one of these:

| Tool | Cost | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Wave** | Free | Solo freelancers, super simple |
| **Stripe Invoicing** | 0.4% per invoice | If you already use Stripe |
| **FreshBooks** | $19+/mo | Multi-client management, time tracking |
| **QuickBooks Self-Employed** | $15/mo | If you also track business expenses |

All of these auto-assign invoice numbers, track paid vs. unpaid, send reminders, and integrate with payment processors.

## Numbering convention

- Start at 001 and increment sequentially (not reset each year — some accountants prefer this)
- If you want to distinguish clients: use prefixes like `ACME-001`, `ACME-002` (optional; most freelancers just number sequentially)
- Never skip numbers. Auditors notice.

## What to include on every invoice

- [x] Your name / business name + address
- [x] Client name + address
- [x] Unique invoice number
- [x] Invoice date + due date
- [x] Itemized list of services (specific enough the client remembers what they're paying for)
- [x] Subtotal, any tax, total due
- [x] Payment method instructions
- [x] Your contact info for questions

## Optional but professional

- [x] Purchase order number (if client provided one)
- [x] Project name or reference
- [x] Brief note of appreciation
- [x] Statement about late fees (matches your services agreement)

## Timing

- Send invoices promptly — within 24-48 hours of completion (for one-time) or on the agreed monthly date (for retainers)
- Late invoices signal disorganization. Clients pay faster when they feel they're dealing with a pro.

## Follow-up schedule for unpaid invoices

- **Day 1 (due date)**: auto-friendly reminder via invoicing tool
- **Day 7 overdue**: polite personal email ("just checking in — any issues with this one?")
- **Day 15 overdue**: firmer email, mention late fee per services agreement
- **Day 30+ overdue**: pause any ongoing work, call the client, discuss
- **Day 60+ overdue**: formal collections letter, or consult an attorney

Don't let invoices sit for months without action. The longer they age, the harder they are to collect.

## Tax notes

- Keep copies of every sent invoice (digital + backup)
- Note the date paid, not just the date sent (this matters for cash-basis accounting)
- Year-end: reconcile invoices sent vs. paid for your 1099 / Schedule C
- Consult a CPA for your specific situation — especially if crossing state lines or international clients
