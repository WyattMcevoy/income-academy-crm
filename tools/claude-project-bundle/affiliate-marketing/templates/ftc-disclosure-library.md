# FTC Disclosure Library

Ready-to-use disclosure language for every common affiliate marketing scenario. Copy, adapt, use.

**⚠️ Reminder**: Not legal advice. For your specific jurisdiction and situation, have an attorney review. FTC guidelines change; review annually.

---

## Core requirements (FTC)

All disclosures must be:
1. **Clear and conspicuous** — not hidden, not in tiny gray footer text
2. **Before the affiliate link** — at top of article, not buried below
3. **In the same language as the content** — disclose where the reader will see it
4. **Unambiguous** — plain English, not legalese that obscures meaning

---

## Disclosures by placement

### Top-of-article disclosure (most common)

**Short version (for articles with 1-3 affiliate links)**:
```
Disclosure: This post contains affiliate links. If you purchase through these links, I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you.
```

**Medium version (for product reviews)**:
```
Disclosure: This post contains affiliate links. If you purchase through these links, I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. I've personally used [PRODUCT] for [TIMEFRAME] before writing this review. Commissions don't affect my opinions or ranking.
```

**Full version (for major roundups or situations where multiple types of affiliate relationships are involved)**:
```
Disclosure: This post contains affiliate links to multiple retailers. If you purchase through these links, I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. I've personally tested [N] of these products; the remainder are included based on thorough research. Products are ranked by fit for [AUDIENCE], not commission structure. See my full [disclosure policy] for details.
```

### Product review disclosure (specific variations)

**When you purchased the product yourself**:
```
I purchased [PRODUCT] with my own money and have used it for [TIMEFRAME]. This review includes affiliate links — if you buy through them, I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you.
```

**When you received a free sample**:
```
Disclosure: [COMPANY] sent me [PRODUCT] to review at no cost. I was not paid to publish this review and retained full editorial control. This post contains affiliate links — if you buy through them, I may earn a commission. My honest opinions are below.
```

**When you received a discount or loaner**:
```
Disclosure: [COMPANY] provided [PRODUCT] at a discount for review purposes. I was not paid additional compensation, and I retain full editorial control. This post contains affiliate links; I may earn a commission on purchases. All opinions are mine.
```

**When paid sponsorship**:
```
Disclosure: This post is sponsored by [COMPANY]. I was paid to write this article. I've personally used [PRODUCT] and retain editorial control over the content, but the sponsorship relationship should be understood when reading my review.
```

### Social media disclosures

**Instagram / Facebook post**:
Add to the beginning of the caption (not just hashtags):
```
[ad] [or] [sponsored] [or] [affiliate] — I earn a commission on purchases through these links.
```

OR, use the platform's built-in "paid partnership" label for Instagram Reels and posts.

**YouTube video description**:
```
Some of the links below are affiliate links. If you purchase through them, I may earn a commission at no additional cost to you. Thanks for supporting the channel!
```

Add a verbal disclosure within the first 30 seconds of video if the video is substantially about products.

**YouTube Shorts**:
Add "#ad" or "#affiliate" in caption AND a pinned comment with the same.

**TikTok**:
Add "#ad" or "#affiliate" in caption AND verbally state at beginning of video.

**X (Twitter) post**:
```
[ad] Just tested [PRODUCT]. Here's what I found: [CONTENT] [LINK]
```

---

### Email newsletter disclosures

**At top of email** (preferred):
```
Disclosure: This email contains affiliate links. If you purchase through them, I may earn a commission. Details at the footer of each email.
```

**At footer of email**:
```
Some links in this email are affiliate links. I earn a commission on purchases made through them. I only link to products I've used or researched carefully. Your support keeps this newsletter free.
```

---

### Podcast disclosures

**Spoken at the start of an episode featuring affiliate products**:
> "Just a quick note before we dive in — today's episode mentions [PRODUCT]. I'm an affiliate, which means if you buy through the link in my show notes, I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. I only recommend products I genuinely use and believe in."

**In show notes**:
```
This episode contains affiliate links. Links in the show notes to [PRODUCT] are affiliate links — if you purchase, I may earn a commission.
```

### Ad creative disclosures

For Meta ads, Google ads, or native ad placements promoting affiliate offers:

**Small text at bottom of creative**:
```
Results not typical. [PROGRAM NAME] affiliate. Earnings vary. Full disclaimer at [YOUR URL].
```

## Situational disclosures

### Disclosing when you have equity in a product

```
Disclosure: I'm an early investor / have an equity position in [COMPANY]. This is not a typical affiliate relationship — my financial incentive is higher and longer-term than standard affiliate commissions. I'm being upfront about this conflict so you can weigh my opinion accordingly.
```

### Disclosing when you work or worked for a company mentioned

```
Disclosure: I worked at [COMPANY] from [DATE] to [DATE] as a [ROLE]. I still know people there. This may influence my coverage — make your own judgments with this context.
```

### Disclosing close personal relationships

```
Disclosure: [FOUNDER NAME] and I have been friends for [TIME]. While I genuinely believe in their product, you should know this relationship exists.
```

### Disclosing prior free services received

```
Disclosure: I've received [COMPANY]'s premium plan free for several years as part of our industry relationship. This may not reflect what you pay or experience. My review is based on ongoing use.
```

## Platform-specific rules

### Amazon Associates
Amazon requires specific disclosure language:

**Standard Amazon disclosure**:
```
As an Amazon Associate, I earn from qualifying purchases. [POST OR SITE]
```

Required to appear on any page with Amazon links. They enforce this in audits.

### Other networks (ShareASale, Impact, CJ, etc.)
Each network has its own terms. Most accept the standard disclosure templates above, but verify in each program's agreement.

## Disclosure for "unbiased review"

Reviews claiming to be "unbiased" while containing affiliate links create risk. Options:

**Option A — don't call it "unbiased"**:
Use "honest review" or "detailed review" instead. Aligns language with your actual relationship.

**Option B — explain what unbiased means**:
```
Disclosure: This review contains affiliate links. While I earn a commission on purchases, I was not paid to write this review. My opinions reflect my honest experience with the product. I ranked alternatives based on fit for my readers, not commission structure.
```

## Children / protected audiences

### COPPA considerations (US minors)
If any part of your audience is under 13, additional rules apply:
- Don't collect personal information from minors without parental consent
- Separate privacy policy for child-directed content
- Extra disclosure clarity (kids shouldn't buy adult products via your links)

### International (GDPR — EU minors)
If EU traffic includes minors:
- Additional consent requirements
- Different data handling obligations
- Consult counsel

## Penalty for non-compliance

FTC has escalated enforcement over the past 5 years. Common outcomes:
- Warning letters (public, bad for reputation)
- Fines ($10,000-$50,000 per violation is common)
- Cease-and-desist orders
- Required corrective disclosures for months/years

A clean disclosure practice takes 10 minutes per article. Not doing it is a 5-7 figure risk.

---

## Final principle

When in doubt, disclose. The penalty for over-disclosing is zero (except perhaps mild annoyance for readers). The penalty for under-disclosing can be your business.
