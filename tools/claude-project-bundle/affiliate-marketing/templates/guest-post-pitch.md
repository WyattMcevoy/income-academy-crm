# Guest Post Pitch Template

For building authoritative backlinks and audience exposure by writing articles for other sites in your niche.

---

## The pitch (standard)

```
Subject: Guest post idea for [SITE NAME]: [WORKING TITLE]

Hi [EDITOR/OWNER NAME],

I've been reading [SITE NAME] for [TIMEFRAME], and your recent piece on [SPECIFIC ARTICLE] hit a nerve — I [SHARED IT / BOOKMARKED IT / PASSED IT TO SPECIFIC PERSON / APPLIED THE ADVICE].

I write about [MY NICHE] at [YOUR SITE URL], and I'd love to contribute a guest post.

Here are 3 ideas, each tailored to your readership:

**1. [WORKING TITLE FOR OPTION 1]**
Angle: [ONE-PARAGRAPH HOOK — why this matters to their readers]
Estimated: ~[WORD COUNT] words
Hook: [CAPTIVATING OPENING LINE OR KEY INSIGHT]

**2. [WORKING TITLE FOR OPTION 2]**
Angle: [HOOK]
Estimated: ~[WORD COUNT] words
Hook: [OPENING LINE/INSIGHT]

**3. [WORKING TITLE FOR OPTION 3]**
Angle: [HOOK]
Estimated: ~[WORD COUNT] words
Hook: [OPENING LINE/INSIGHT]

A few samples of my writing:
- [LINK 1]: [BRIEF NOTE ABOUT THIS PIECE]
- [LINK 2]: [BRIEF NOTE]
- [LINK 3]: [BRIEF NOTE]

If any of these fit your content plans, I can have a first draft to you in [TIMEFRAME]. I'll follow your style guide and include any specific sections/sources you want.

If none hit the mark but you're open to other topics from me, let me know what you need and I'll pitch fresh angles.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR SITE]
[LINKEDIN OR SOCIAL]
```

## The pitch (shorter, more experienced writer)

Once you've published a few guest posts, this shorter version works:

```
Subject: Guest post — [TOPIC]

Hi [NAME],

I've been a reader for a while. Would you consider a guest post on [SPECIFIC TOPIC]?

Angle: [ONE-PARAGRAPH HOOK]
Working title: "[TITLE]"
Length: [WORD COUNT]

My writing samples: [LINK 1], [LINK 2], [LINK 3]

Happy to draft on spec if it's a fit.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

---

## Follow-up (7-10 days after initial pitch)

```
Subject: re: [ORIGINAL SUBJECT]

Hi [NAME],

Quick follow-up in case this got buried.

Since I first wrote, I also thought of: [ONE NEW ANGLE RELATED TO RECENT NEWS OR THEIR CONTENT]. Happy to pitch that instead or in addition.

If now isn't right, no worries. I'll keep reading either way.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

---

## Final "closing the loop" (14-21 days after, no response)

```
Subject: One last note, [NAME]

Last note from me. If guest posts aren't a fit right now, totally understand. I'll keep enjoying your site.

If the door opens in the future, feel free to reach back.

Either way, thanks for the work you put out.

[YOUR NAME]
```

## Tips for higher acceptance rates

### Research is everything
Before pitching, spend 30+ minutes on their site:
- What have they covered recently?
- What topics do they seem to avoid?
- What's their angle or editorial voice?
- Who's their audience specifically?

Your pitch should demonstrate you understand this.

### Three ideas, not one
Offering multiple options gives the editor choice and shows you thought carefully.

### Match their length/style
Some sites prefer 800-word actionable posts. Others prefer 3,000-word deep dives. Pitch what fits them.

### Offer unique angles, not rehashes
"5 Tips for [COMMON TOPIC]" is usually declined. "How [SPECIFIC AUDIENCE] Can [SPECIFIC OUTCOME] Using [UNEXPECTED METHOD]" gets reads.

### Your samples should be STRONG
- Choose 3 of your best pieces, not 5 random ones
- Link to content on YOUR site (proves writing quality)
- Avoid linking to social posts or unpublished drafts

### Write for their audience, not yours
The post is for their readers. If their audience needs more explanation than yours, adjust.

### Respect "no" gracefully
Many editors say no to 90% of pitches. Stay polite. You might pitch again in 6 months successfully.

## What NOT to do

### Don't pitch without reading the site
Editors can tell immediately. "I love your recent post on..." when they haven't posted in 6 months = instant reject.

### Don't pitch the same article to 5 sites simultaneously
Exclusive pitches. If rejected, then move to the next site.

### Don't pitch and then go silent
Follow through on timeline, respond to feedback, deliver the promised piece.

### Don't pitch a post that's secretly a self-promotional ad
Good guest posts serve the host's audience. Your backlink in the byline is the reward — not the body of the piece.

### Don't complain when rejected
- Don't argue
- Don't ask why
- Don't burn bridges

### Don't skip the author bio
When accepted, you'll likely write a short author bio at the end. This is where your subtle self-promotion (and backlink) goes. Draft a good one:

**Author bio template**:
```
[YOUR NAME] writes about [NICHE] at [SITE NAME], focusing on [SPECIFIC AUDIENCE OR ANGLE]. When not writing, [ONE HUMAN DETAIL]. Follow [HIM/HER/THEM] on [PLATFORM] for [SPECIFIC VALUE].
```

Example:
```
Jane McKinney writes about retirement-era side income at GraySideHustle.com, focusing on AI-assisted freelancing for people over 50. When not writing, she's negotiating with her rescue greyhound for couch space. Follow Jane on LinkedIn for weekly tips.
```

Short. Specific. Human. Ends with a soft CTA.

## Scaling guest posts

Once you've published 5-10 successful guest posts:
- Editors start coming to you with asks
- You can negotiate paid guest posts
- You build genuine relationships with other creators
- Your own site's authority grows from quality backlinks

Sustainable guest posting = 1-2 per month, chosen carefully, executed well. Not a pitch-storm.

---

## Backlink value note

The backlink value of a guest post depends on:
- **Domain authority of the hosting site** (higher = more valuable)
- **Relevance to your niche** (tangentially related gives less value)
- **Placement** (in body text = gold; in bio only = okay; in comments/footer = near-zero)
- **Do-follow vs no-follow** (do-follow passes link juice; no-follow is just reader traffic)

Aim for do-follow, in-body links on niche-relevant sites with established authority. 3 of those beat 30 random links on no-authority sites.
