# Honest Affiliate Marketing Starter

**Target audience**: US adults 45–75. Semi-retired or near-retirement. Non-technical. Want extra income from home, are skeptical of hype.

**Compliance posture**: Frame affiliate marketing as building a real content business — not "push button commissions." Always disclose FTC-style. No promises of passive income.

## Offer stack

| Tier | Price | What they get |
|---|---|---|
| **Front-end tripwire** | $47 one-time | 8-module starter course, niche research toolkit, 30 email templates, affiliate tracking spreadsheet, Income Academy AI Writing Assistant |
| **Continuity** | $19/mo (starts after 7-day trial) | Private community, monthly affiliate program roundup, quarterly niche opportunity reports, monthly Q&A |
| **High-ticket back-end** | $2,000-$5,000 | "Content Site Accelerator" — 90-day done-with-you program where we help you build your first affiliate content site, write the first 20 posts together, and launch traffic |

## Positioning

> "Affiliate marketing isn't a get-rich-quick scheme — and it's not dead. It's just misrepresented. Real affiliate marketers build real small businesses: a niche site, a newsletter, or a YouTube channel, with time and care. This course teaches that. Nothing else."

## Why this works alongside Income Academy's Ebay + AI offerings

- **Different learner profile**: AI Side Income = service business (exchange time for $). Affiliate Marketing = content business (build assets). Appeals to a different personality type — the patient, research-loving, long-game thinker.
- **Same email list can cross-sell**: MailerLite survey can ask "service or assets?" — route to right offer.
- **Same phone team, different script**: high-ticket is similar structure ("done-with-you 90-day program") so sales team reuses workflow.
- **Warehouse leverage**: minimal here. But content sites can promote physical goods via Amazon Associates — not competing with Ebay program, complementary.
- **Compliance story**: affiliate marketing taught well is 100% legit. Taught hype-y it's FTC bait. We teach it well.

## Risks to manage

1. **Category reputation** — affiliate marketing has been dragged by decades of get-rich-quick offers. Our differentiation is honesty. Lean into it heavily in all copy.
2. **Declining SEO** — Google's 2023 Helpful Content Update punished thin affiliate sites. Our training reflects post-HCU reality: depth over keyword stuffing, trust over tricks.
3. **Stripe risk** — affiliate marketing courses have been used by scammers, so Stripe reviews them closely. Keep our offer description boring and educational. No "make $X" language anywhere on Stripe side.
4. **Program approval denials** — Amazon Associates, for example, rejects applicants without active content sites. Module 4 addresses this honestly.
