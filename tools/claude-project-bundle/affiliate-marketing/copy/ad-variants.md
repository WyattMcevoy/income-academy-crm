# Honest Affiliate Marketing — Meta Ad Variants

Ready-to-test Meta ad creative. FTC-compliant. No hype. Sober positioning — which is itself the differentiator in this category.

## Compliance rules (always)

**NEVER use:**
- "Passive income," "automated income," "while you sleep"
- "Make $X/month" or specific income claims
- "Get rich quick" or anything implying fast results
- "Guaranteed," "can't fail," "proven method"
- Lifestyle imagery (Lamborghinis, mansions, beach)

**Always use or imply:**
- "Learn," "training," "skills"
- Realistic framing ("6-12 months," "slow-build," "content business")
- Earnings disclaimer small text

## Common approval issues for this niche

### Pitfall: Historical bad actors
Affiliate marketing courses have been used by scammers for 20+ years. Meta reviewers may flag even legitimate offers.

**Mitigation**: Lead with sober language in every ad. No dollar amounts. No "quit your job." No aspirational lifestyle.

### Pitfall: Income Opportunity targeting
Meta restricts some "employment" and "income opportunity" targeting (Special Ad Categories).

**Mitigation**: Frame as "education" consistently. Target interests like "blogging" and "SEO" rather than "make money online."

### Pitfall: Landing page mismatch
Meta reviews the landing page. Hypey landing page = rejected ad.

**Mitigation**: Use the compliant landing page copy from `landing-page.md`. Keep the earnings disclaimer visible.

## Winning formula summary

For the 45-75 audience in a skeptical category:

1. Lead with honesty, not hype — contrast yourself against the category's bad reputation
2. Set realistic timelines in the ad itself (makes you more trustworthy)
3. Use plain, readable copy — not marketing-speak
4. Show the actual math (year 1 vs year 2 earnings, not cherry-picked screenshots)
5. Make the refund prominent — risk reversal is the close
