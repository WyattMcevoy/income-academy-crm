# Prompts — Compliance & Disclosures

The boring stuff that keeps your business alive. Affiliate marketing has been a regulator target for 30 years. Don't wing this.

## FTC disclosure writing

### CD-01: Standard FTC disclosure
```
Write a standard FTC disclosure for my affiliate site. Requirements:
- "Clear and conspicuous" (not hidden in footer)
- Names the relationship (affiliate compensation)
- States the relationship applies to specific content
- Under 60 words
- Readable (no dense legalese)

Three versions to choose from:
- Short (above-the-fold): 1-2 sentences
- Medium (top of blog post): 2-3 sentences
- Long (footer of article): 3-4 sentences with details
```

### CD-02: Disclosure for specific article
```
Article: [TITLE/URL]
Affiliate relationships in this article:
- Amazon Associates links (multiple products)
- Direct partnership with [COMPANY]
- Free sample received from [OTHER COMPANY]

Write an article-specific disclosure that:
- Explicitly names any company where I received something free
- States the Amazon affiliate relationship
- Clarifies that affiliate links don't change price
- Is at TOP of article (before first affiliate link)
- Under 100 words

Template I can adapt for other articles: ___
```

### CD-03: Social media disclosure
```
Platform: [INSTAGRAM / YOUTUBE / X / TIKTOK / FACEBOOK]
Content type: [PRODUCT REVIEW / COMPARISON / ROUNDUP]

Write FTC-compliant disclosure for this platform:
- Appropriate hashtag (#ad #affiliate #sponsored per FTC)
- Placement (in-caption for Instagram/X; in-video for YouTube; in-description)
- Required if: I'm affiliated, received product free, or paid sponsorship
- Character count efficient (platform limits)

Provide 3 variants per platform.
```

## Privacy policy

### CD-04: Simple privacy policy for affiliate site
```
My site: [URL]
My location: [STATE, US]
Data I collect:
- Email addresses (via MailerLite for newsletter)
- Google Analytics (anonymized)
- Affiliate tracking cookies (from affiliate networks)
- Any user-submitted comments or form responses

My privacy respected approach:
- I don't sell data
- I don't share with anyone except platform partners (Google Analytics, MailerLite, hosting)

Write a privacy policy that:
- Clear, readable language (not generic template)
- Covers required disclosures (GDPR, CCPA, COPPA for minors)
- Mentions cookies and affiliate tracking
- Includes how readers can opt-out / delete data

Length: 800-1500 words. Note: get attorney review for your jurisdiction.
```

### CD-05: GDPR + CCPA compliance check
```
Traffic sources for my site include some EU and California visitors.

Review my current privacy practices:
[PASTE OR DESCRIBE]

Check compliance:
- Do I have a cookie consent banner? (required for EU)
- Do I disclose data categories collected?
- Do I offer a "do not sell my data" option? (required for CA)
- Do I have a data deletion process?
- Are my email opt-ins double-opt-in or single? (single is fine for US; double is safer for EU)

Flag specific gaps. Suggest minimum viable fixes.

Not legal advice — but common issues for small affiliate sites.
```

- Geographic restrictions

Flag any program with unusually restrictive terms. Flag any where my current practices might be at risk.

(I'll provide program names; you'll tell me common patterns to check.)
```

```
I'm an Amazon affiliate. Run through the most common compliance mistakes:

## Tax compliance

### CD-08: US self-employment tax quick guide
```
I'm a US-based affiliate marketer (sole proprietor, no LLC yet).

Summarize:
- Which forms do I file for affiliate income? (Schedule C, SE, 1040)
- How do I report affiliate income (Amazon, ShareASale, etc.)?
- Quarterly estimated taxes: who needs to pay them? When?
- Deductible business expenses for affiliate sites (hosting, tools, subscriptions, home office pro-rated)
- When should I consider an LLC or S-corp?

Not tax advice. Tell me the 80/20 — what most affiliate marketers need to know, with a strong recommendation to consult a CPA for specifics.
```

## Legal protection

### CD-10: Terms of service for affiliate site
```
My site: [URL]
My services:
- Content (articles, reviews)
- Email newsletter
- (Optional) paid products: [LIST]

Write a basic ToS covering:
- Visitor agreements (use at own risk, no professional advice)
- Intellectual property (my content can't be copied without permission)
- Comment and user-submitted content policies
- Affiliate relationship acknowledgment
- Limitation of liability
- Jurisdiction for disputes

Length: 800-1200 words. Get attorney review.
```

### CD-11: Affiliate income disclaimer
```
Write an earnings disclaimer specifically for affiliate marketing:

- Results mentioned are individual, not typical
- Success depends on effort, skill, market conditions, luck
- No guarantee of income
- Average affiliate marketer earns: reference what's published
- Readers should not quit their job based on my content

Length: 300-500 words. Place: site footer + within posts that reference earnings.
```

## Quality / brand safety

### CD-12: Content moderation policy
```
For my blog's comments or my community (if I have one):

Write a content moderation policy:
- What's welcome (on-topic discussion, questions, sharing experiences)
- What's not welcome (spam, ad hominem, affiliate link dumping, racist/sexist/harassment)
- What I'll do about violations (delete, warn, ban)
- Appeal process
- My commitment to fair moderation

Length: 300-500 words. Make it clear but human — not corporate legalese.
```

### CD-13: Conflict of interest disclosure
```
Situations where I should disclose extra:
- I'm on the board/advisory of a company I review
- I have equity in a startup I mention
- I used to work for a company I compare
- I have a close personal relationship with a product's founder

Write a "conflicts of interest" policy for my site covering when and how I disclose these.
```

## Compliance principles

### Clear > clever
Disclosures should be obvious, not hidden in legalese or light-gray 8-point footer text. Assume your reader is smart but busy.

### Over-disclose, not under
If you're unsure whether to disclose: disclose. The penalty for over-disclosing is zero. The penalty for under-disclosing is regulatory action.

### Each post, not just site-wide
"There's a disclosure in my footer" is not sufficient for FTC. Each post with affiliate links needs its own disclosure at top.

### Get attorney review once
Spend $500-1500 once to have an attorney customize your ToS, privacy policy, and disclosures for your state and business. Then use the reviewed versions for years.

### Keep records
Save screenshots of your site's disclosure language at various points in time. If a regulator asks "what did your site look like in 2024?", you want to be able to show them.

### Affiliate programs watch too
Amazon, for example, audits affiliates. Violating their operating agreement can get you banned and your commissions clawed back. Comply with both FTC rules AND your programs' specific rules.
