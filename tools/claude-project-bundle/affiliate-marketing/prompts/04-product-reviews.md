# Prompts — Product Reviews & Comparisons

For writing the product content that directly converts readers into buyers. The single most important skill in affiliate marketing.

## Review planning

### PR-01: Decide if a product is worth reviewing
```
Product: [NAME + BRIEF]
My genuine opinion: [WOULD I BUY THIS? USE THIS? RECOMMEND TO A FAMILY MEMBER?]

Evaluate whether I should write about it:
- Is there search demand? (rough estimate)
- Are there already strong reviews from trustworthy sites?
- Can I add something genuinely new (personal use, unique angle, underserved audience)?
- Is the affiliate program worth it (commission + cookie)?
- Would I defend this recommendation in public?

Give a go/no-go. Don't let me recommend something I don't believe in.
```

### PR-02: Review outline from real use
```
Product: [NAME]
How long I've used it: [TIME]
What I actually use it for: [SPECIFIC USE CASES]
My honest feelings: [SHORT DUMP — likes, dislikes, surprises]

Generate a review outline that maximizes my authentic perspective:
- Opening: a specific moment that captures my feeling about it
- Who this review is for (specific scenario)
- At-a-glance verdict (2-3 sentence summary)
- Context: how I came to use it, for how long
- What it does well (3-5 points, each anchored in my specific use)
- Where it falls short (2-3 honest flaws — no perfect products exist)
- Who should buy it (specific scenarios)
- Who should NOT buy it (this builds trust, keeps bad-fit buyers from refunding)
- Alternatives if this isn't right
- FAQ
- Final take + affiliate link with clear disclosure

Flag anything I need to verify before writing (specs, prices, availability).
```

## First-draft generation

### PR-03: Write the review draft
```
Using this outline:
[PASTE OUTLINE]

Write a [WORD COUNT] review draft. Rules:

- Open with the specific moment from outline
- Weave in personal experience throughout (don't silo it to one section)
- Every "pro" must reference a specific use case
- Every "con" must be genuine (not "the packaging could be better")
- Mention competing products by name (helps SEO, shows I did research)
- Include a comparison table if 3+ alternatives are worth noting
- Keep paragraphs short (2-4 sentences)
- End with a clear recommendation + who shouldn't buy

FTC disclosure placeholder: "Disclosure: I received this product [at regular retail / as a review sample / as an affiliate]. See my [disclosure page] for details. Commissions don't affect what I recommend."

Voice profile:
[PASTE VOICE]
```

### PR-04: Add emotional texture to a technical review
```
This review is factually accurate but emotionally flat:
[PASTE DRAFT]

Add 4-6 moments where my actual feelings about the product come through:
- Frustration (when it didn't work as expected)
- Delight (small thing that surprised me)
- Realization (moment I understood its best use)
- Comparison (to what I was using before)
- Honesty (what I initially got wrong about it)

Keep each addition under 50 words. Don't rewrite the whole piece.
```

## Comparison reviews

### PR-05: Head-to-head comparison structure
```
Compare [PRODUCT A] vs [PRODUCT B]

My direct experience:
- A: [MY USE]
- B: [MY USE OR RESEARCH]

Generate outline:
- Opening: who this comparison is for
- At-a-glance winner per category:
  - Best overall
  - Best for budget
  - Best for [SPECIFIC USER TYPE]
- Feature-by-feature comparison table (build it)
- Price difference + what justifies it
- Where A wins (specific)
- Where B wins (specific)
- Tiebreaker categories (durability, support, reputation)
- Winner for 3 different reader types
- My pick + why
- FAQ (5 questions)

Balance the piece. If I genuinely prefer one, say so clearly but don't bury the other's strengths.
```

### PR-06: Comparison table content
```
I'm building a comparison table for: [PRODUCT A] vs [PRODUCT B] vs [PRODUCT C].

Key comparison criteria:
[LIST THE 8-10 MOST IMPORTANT ATTRIBUTES]

For each product and criterion, fill in the cell with:
- Specific value (not "good" or "excellent")
- Brief context if helpful (in parentheses)
- Use — for "doesn't have this feature"
- Use "limited" or "full" for feature presence

Return as a markdown table. Flag anything I need to verify before publishing.
```

## Roundup reviews

### PR-07: "Best X for Y" roundup outline
```
Roundup topic: "Best [PRODUCT TYPE] for [SPECIFIC AUDIENCE/USE]"
Products I'm considering: [LIST 8-12 CANDIDATES]

For each, assess inclusion:
- Does it genuinely fit the "for Y" audience?
- Have I used it or can I verify claims?
- Are there real use scenarios where this is the best choice?
- Is there a better alternative in its sub-category?

Recommend final 5-8 picks with:
- Sub-category (Best overall, Best budget, Best for X, etc.)
- Reasoning for inclusion
- What makes it win its sub-category

Kill weak picks. A tight 5-pick list beats a padded 10-pick list.
```

### PR-08: Roundup writeup — per product
```
Write the writeup for this product within a roundup:

Product: [NAME]
Sub-category: [BEST FOR WHAT — e.g., "Best budget option"]
My notes: [EXPERIENCE, OBSERVATIONS]
Price: $[AMOUNT]
Key specs: [2-3 MOST RELEVANT]

Format:
- Sub-category badge (e.g., "BEST BUDGET")
- Product name (linked with affiliate)
- 200-300 words
- Quick verdict in first 2 sentences
- 3 specific strengths (anchored in why it wins its category)
- 1-2 honest weaknesses
- Who should get this specifically
- Link to full review if I've written one

Maintain consistency with other writeups in tone and length.
```

### PR-09: Buying guide for end of roundup
```
For a roundup of [PRODUCT TYPE]:

Write a "how to choose" buying guide section (500-800 words):
- What to prioritize based on your situation (3-5 scenarios)
- Common mistakes first-time buyers make
- Specs that matter vs specs that don't
- Budget considerations (what $X gets you vs $Y)
- Red flags to watch for (e.g., "too cheap usually means X")

Make it actually useful. Not filler. Goal: reader feels equipped to choose even beyond my recommendations.
```

## Updates + refreshes

### PR-10: Review refresh (article updates)
```
This review was published [DATE]. Key changes since:
- Product updates: [ANY]
- Pricing changes: [CURRENT $]
- Alternatives that have emerged: [IF ANY]
- Things I've learned from longer use: [NOTES]

Generate updates to the existing review:
[PASTE ORIGINAL]

Specifically:
- Update outdated info
- Add a "Update: [DATE]" note with changes
- Adjust verdict if my opinion has shifted
- Flag deprecated sections

Minimal rewrite — preserve what's still accurate. Goal: keep the article helpful 2 years after I first wrote it.
```

### PR-11: Performance check + improvement
```
This review has been live 6+ months. Current performance:
- Traffic: [MONTHLY VISITORS]
- Rankings: [POSITION FOR MAIN KEYWORD]
- Affiliate clicks: [MONTHLY]
- Conversions: [MONTHLY]

Article:
[PASTE]

Identify what's hurting performance:
- Missing sections that similar articles have
- Weak hook or intro
- Unclear verdict
- Missing comparison
- Outdated info
- Weak internal linking
- Missing FAQ

Suggest 5-10 specific improvements, prioritized.
```

## Photo + media prompts

### PR-12: Photo caption ideas
```
I'm taking photos of [PRODUCT] to include in my review. 

Typical photo types I'll take:
- Product in its box (unboxing shot)
- Product in use (me using it)
- Close-up of a specific feature
- Size comparison (with common object)
- Before/after (if applicable)

For each photo type, suggest:
- The specific angle/composition
- What to include in the frame for context
- A 15-20 word caption that adds value (not "product photo")

Goal: photos that make the review feel real, not stock.
```

## Trust-building rules

### Always disclose
- FTC disclosure at top of every post with affiliate links
- State clearly if you received the product free, at discount, or bought it yourself
- Link to your policies/disclosure page

### Always be specific
- "I've used this daily for 6 months" > "This product is great"
- "After 2 weeks, I noticed [X]" > "Highly effective"
- "The battery lasts exactly [N] hours" > "Good battery life"

### Always include real cons
- No product is perfect. Saying so hurts your credibility.
- The best review writers point out exactly what they don't love.
- Readers trust balanced reviews 10× more than "10/10 amazing" ones.

### Don't recommend what you haven't used
- If you're writing a roundup with products you haven't personally used, say so: "I've personally used X and Y. Z is included based on research from [sources]."
- Never pretend.

### Price honesty
- List the actual price at time of writing
- Update prices when they change
- Mention if price swings (e.g., "this product goes on sale often around [time]")
