# Prompts — Niche Research

For validating a niche BEFORE you commit 6-12 months to it.

## Initial niche brainstorming

### NR-01: Generate niches from my interests
```
I'm considering starting an affiliate content business. Here are things I'm genuinely interested in, know about, or have experience with:
[LIST 5-10 TOPICS/HOBBIES/AREAS OF EXPERIENCE]

Generate 15 specific niche angles — not broad topics but specific sub-niches that could support a content business. For each:
- Specific niche
- Example content ideas (2-3)
- Who the audience would be (specific)
- Why this sub-niche specifically (vs the broader topic)

Example: Not "fitness" — "strength training for men over 50." Not "cooking" — "air fryer cooking for empty nesters."
```

### NR-02: Narrow down to winners
```
Here are 5 niche candidates I'm considering:
[LIST]

For each, evaluate on these 5 criteria (score 1-5):
- Audience size (is there a real market?)
- Commercial intent (do people buy?)
- Product variety (are there diverse affiliate programs?)
- Competition level (can a small site compete?)
- My staying power (will I care in 2 years?)

Don't invent numbers. Use general market knowledge. Flag anything where data is unclear.

Return the total score and a 1-paragraph recommendation for each.
```

## Market validation

### NR-03: Commercial viability check
```
For the niche "[NICHE]", assess commercial viability:

1. What types of products/services do people in this niche buy?
2. What's the typical price range?
3. Which affiliate networks or direct programs serve this space?
4. Estimated commission rates typical for this niche?
5. Are purchases repeat or one-time? (Recurring software = much better LTV)
6. Seasonality? (Every December spike is fine; a niche that only earns Nov-Dec is risky)

Be honest about what you don't know vs. what you can reasonably estimate.
```

### NR-04: Existing content audit
```
I'm researching the niche "[NICHE]". 

Search Google for "[MAIN KEYWORD]" and related terms. Based on what you know about the top-ranking sites:

- Who are the 5 biggest players?
- What are they doing well?
- Where do they have gaps (topics they don't cover, formats they don't use)?
- Is this an open lane (few strong sites) or crowded (dominated by giant sites)?

Flag clearly: if 8+ of the top 10 are sites like Forbes, Healthline, or Wirecutter, small-site SEO in this niche is extremely hard.
```

### NR-05: Keyword difficulty quick read
```
For the niche "[NICHE]", here are 10 potential target keywords:
[LIST]

For each, estimate keyword difficulty (1-5 scale):
- 1 = small sites can rank
- 3 = moderate competition, ranks in 12-18 months
- 5 = dominated by huge sites, don't bother

Without specific tools, use judgment based on the expected competitors. Suggest 3 alternative long-tail keywords I should consider instead of any that score 4-5.
```

## Audience research

### NR-06: Ideal reader profile
```
For the niche "[NICHE]", build a reader persona:
- Demographics (age range, life stage, income, education)
- Daily life pain points (specific to this niche)
- Where they currently go for info (forums, YouTube, books, etc.)
- What they're trying to solve or achieve
- Their skepticism level (have they been burned by bad content?)
- Their purchasing patterns (impulse buyers? researchers?)

Be specific. Don't default to a generic reader.
```

### NR-07: Content format preferences
```
For readers in the niche "[NICHE]", which content format works best?
- Long-form written (1500+ words)
- Listicles
- Video (YouTube)
- Podcasts
- Newsletters
- Social (short-form)

Base this on:
- What the demographic prefers
- What the topic lends itself to (tutorials work as video; buying guides work as long-form)
- What's saturated vs open

Recommend the top 1-2 formats and explain why.
```

## Competition intelligence

### NR-08: Specific competitor deep dive
```
Competitor: [SITE URL OR NAME]
Niche: [THEIR FOCUS]

Analyze (based on public info):
- Their apparent monetization model (affiliate? courses? ads?)
- Content production rate (frequent posts? old site with evergreen?)
- Apparent authority signals (mentions, links, social following)
- Their best-performing content (if discoverable via rank data or social shares)
- Weaknesses or gaps in their coverage
- Are they a threat or a model to learn from?

If I can't determine something, flag it instead of guessing.
```

### NR-09: Competitive "what I'd do differently" brainstorm
```
Given the competitive landscape in "[NICHE]":
[PASTE RESEARCH FROM NR-04 OR NR-08]

What's a defensible positioning for a new entrant?
- Specific audience underserved by current players
- Specific content format or angle not covered
- A "contrarian but defensible" take
- A deeper vertical within the niche

Give me 3 distinct positioning options, with pros/cons.
```

## Seasonality & trends

### NR-10: Trend check
```
For the niche "[NICHE]":
- Is search interest growing, flat, or declining?
- Are there seasonal spikes I should be aware of?
- Has any major event or regulation changed the landscape recently?
- Are there rising adjacent niches worth monitoring?

Use Google Trends logic (you don't need to browse — just apply general knowledge of the category).
```

### NR-11: Evergreen vs trend check
```
For the niche "[NICHE]":
- What aspects are evergreen? (durable, will still matter in 5 years)
- What aspects are trend-based? (big now, might not be in 2 years)

Rule for this business: 80% of content should be evergreen. Trend content is fine for traffic spikes but don't build the business on it.

Flag any niche that's MOSTLY trend-based — those aren't good choices for a patient affiliate site.
```

## Final niche decision

### NR-12: Go / no-go framework
```
I've researched the niche "[NICHE]" and gathered:
[PASTE FINDINGS FROM PREVIOUS PROMPTS]

Give me a go/no-go recommendation with:
- Score total out of 25 (using the 5 criteria: audience, intent, products, competition, staying power)
- Top 3 reasons to proceed
- Top 3 reasons to reconsider
- My biggest risk if I proceed
- My biggest missed opportunity if I don't

If the score is under 15, push back hard. Don't let me commit to a bad niche.
```
