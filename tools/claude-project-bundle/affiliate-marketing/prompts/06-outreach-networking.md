# Prompts — Outreach & Networking

For guest posts, collaborations, podcast pitches, and building the relationships that 10× your affiliate business.

## Guest post pitches

### ON-01: Guest post pitch email
```
Target site: [WEBSITE]
Editor/owner: [NAME]
Target audience: [THEIR READERSHIP]

My info:
- My site: [MINE]
- My niche: [NICHE]
- Sample articles they might know from me: [LINKS]

Write a guest post pitch:
- Show I actually read their site (reference a specific article they published)
- Propose 3 specific headline ideas that fit THEIR voice/style
- For each, include: working title, 2-sentence angle, who the reader would be
- Close with ONE clear next step
- Under 250 words

No "I hope this email finds you well." No generic opening. Treat them like a peer.
```

### ON-02: Follow-up on guest post pitch
```
I pitched [SITE/NAME] on [DATE] and haven't heard back. 
My original pitch: [PASTE]

Write a follow-up:
- Under 50 words
- Add ONE new piece of value (a recent specific observation, article I wrote since, resource)
- Doesn't apologize or "circle back"
- Leaves it easy to say yes or ignore
```

## Podcast pitches

### ON-03: Podcast pitch from my expertise
```
Target podcast: [NAME]
Host: [NAME]
Average guest topics: [BRIEF — what they usually cover]

My expertise: [WHAT I KNOW DEEPLY, SPECIFICALLY]
My unique angle: [WHY A PODCAST AUDIENCE WOULD FIND ME INTERESTING]

Write a pitch:
- Reference a specific recent episode I've listened to (and why it resonated)
- Propose 2-3 topics I could discuss
- For each topic: what the host/audience would take away
- Brief credibility note (not a resume — one line max)
- Ask for a 15-minute pre-interview chat
- Under 200 words
```

### ON-04: Response if they say yes to an interview
```
The podcast host agreed to have me. 

Write a confirmation response:
- Enthusiastic but professional
- Confirm logistics (date, time zone, platform)
- Ask what prep they'd like (bio format, photo, topics they want to focus on)
- Send any assets they might need (headshot link, LinkedIn, my prior interviews if relevant)
- Under 150 words
```

## Collaboration pitches

### ON-05: Co-creation pitch to another niche creator
```
Creator: [NAME + WHAT THEY DO]
Our audiences overlap because: [REASON]
What I'd propose: [SPECIFIC COLLAB — joint post, newsletter swap, webinar, product bundle, etc.]

Write a pitch:
- Open with genuine recognition of their work
- Explain the specific collab idea with mutual benefit
- Outline what I'd bring to the table (not just what I want)
- Suggest a 20-min call to discuss
- Leave the door open if they're not interested
- Under 250 words
```

### ON-06: Newsletter swap proposal
```
Another newsletter writer in my niche: [NAME + NEWSLETTER]
Their size: [SUBSCRIBER COUNT, IF KNOWN]
My size: [MY SUBS]

Write a swap proposal:
- Explain: they mention my newsletter in one of their sends; I do the same for them
- Suggest the format (blurb, full email, dedicated send, etc.)
- Propose specific swap dates
- Note: mutual benefit clear, honest about audience sizes (shouldn't swap 10,000 for 200 subs)
- Under 200 words
```

## Link-building outreach

### ON-07: Resource page link request
```
Resource page: [URL]
Site owner/editor: [NAME IF FINDABLE]
My relevant article: [URL + TITLE]

Write a polite link request:
- Specifically mention their resource page (not "I enjoy your site")
- Explain which article I think fits their list + why
- Make it easy to say yes (give them the exact anchor text I'd suggest + placement on their page)
- Not pushy — assume they might not do it
- Under 150 words

Key principle: I'm adding value to THEIR page, not asking a favor.
```

### ON-08: Broken link building outreach
```
Site: [URL]
Page with broken link: [URL]
The broken link they have: [POINTS TO WHAT — 404? deleted page?]
My replacement content: [MY URL + WHY IT FILLS THE GAP]

Write outreach:
- Lead with the actual problem (broken link = bad for them)
- Subtly note I have content that could fill the gap
- Make it easy to swap — include my anchor text suggestion
- Frame as a favor TO THEM, not an ask
- Under 150 words
- Never be salesy about my content

This works 15-30% of the time. High quality, high success rate outreach.
```

## Journalism / PR

### ON-09: HARO / reporter outreach response
```
I saw a HARO query looking for expert quotes on: [TOPIC]
The reporter is from: [PUBLICATION]
They need: [WHAT THEY NEED — quote, data, example, etc.]

My angle on this: [WHAT I KNOW/WOULD SAY]

Write my response:
- Specific, quotable sound bite (2-3 sentences they could actually use)
- Credentials in one line (not my whole resume)
- Offer to elaborate if needed
- Under 200 words

Goal: give them a quote that makes their article better. They remember helpful experts.
```

### ON-10: Podcast/press follow-up after being featured
```
I was just quoted/featured in: [PUBLICATION/PODCAST]

Write:
- Thank-you note to the reporter/host (genuine, specific)
- Social media announcement (LinkedIn + X)
- Newsletter note to my list about the feature
- Request for them to keep me in mind for future coverage

Total length across all four: under 500 words combined. Tone: grateful, confident, not bragging.
```

## Community / forum engagement

### ON-11: Niche forum value-first posts
```
Forum: [NAME OF FORUM/SUBREDDIT/FACEBOOK GROUP]
Vibe of the community: [BRIEF]
A question I saw: [PASTE]
My honest answer: [WHAT I'D SAY]

Write my response:
- 300-500 words, thoroughly helpful
- No links to my site (yet)
- Mention my experience only if genuinely relevant
- Ask a follow-up question to keep engagement going

Goal: establish as a helpful regular in the community. Links come later, after reputation is built.
```

### ON-12: Answering questions with helpful depth
```
Question from community: [PASTE]

Write an answer that:
- Takes the question seriously
- Provides 2-3 specific actionable steps
- Acknowledges when my answer depends on their situation
- Includes a small personal story if it adds credibility
- Ends with "happy to explain more if helpful"
- No self-promotion. No links. Just genuine help.

Length: 200-400 words.
```

## Influencer outreach

### ON-13: Creator collaboration request
```
Creator: [NAME + PLATFORM]
Their specialty: [NICHE + CONTENT TYPE]
What I want to propose: [SPECIFIC IDEA]

Write a DM or email:
- Show familiarity with their recent work (specific reference)
- Propose the collab with mutual benefit clear
- Make the "ask" easy — first step is a 15-min call, not a full project commitment
- Keep it conversational, not corporate
- Under 150 words
```

### ON-14: Follow-up after a creator engagement
```
[CREATOR] interacted with my content (commented / shared / mentioned).

Write a follow-up:
- Thank them genuinely (specific to what they said/did)
- Don't immediately pitch anything
- Ask a question about THEIR work that I'm genuinely curious about
- Leave the door open for future conversation

Under 100 words. This builds real relationships.
```

## Event networking

### ON-15: Conference attendee intro
```
I'm attending: [CONFERENCE]
Prospect I want to meet: [NAME + ROLE]
What I want from the connection: [CLEAR BUT REASONABLE]

Write a pre-conference email:
- Reference the conference
- Explain why I want to meet specifically
- Propose a specific time/format (coffee at [SESSION BREAK], not open-ended)
- Make it easy to say yes
- Under 150 words

Don't pitch anything yet. The meeting is the ask.
```

### ON-16: Post-event follow-up
```
Met someone at [EVENT]. 

What we discussed: [BRIEF]
Something they said I can reference: [SPECIFIC]
What I'd want from the ongoing relationship: [HONEST]

Write a follow-up:
- Reference the specific conversation point
- Follow through on anything I promised at the event (article I'd send, intro I'd make)
- Suggest a specific next step (another call, an exchange)
- Under 200 words
```

## Relationship principles

### Give first, ask later
Before asking for a guest post, backlink, or intro — add value to their world first. Share their content. Comment thoughtfully. Recommend them. THEN, when you have a reason to ask, they remember you.

### Specificity wins
"I love your work" is spam. "Your piece on [SPECIFIC TOPIC] — especially the part about [DETAIL] — is why I'm reaching out" is a real opener.

### The 10-to-1 ratio
For every 1 promotional message you send (pitch, ask, request), you should have engaged with or helped that person 10 times. Networking without the 10:1 feels like sales to them.

### It's a long game
Relationships that lead to the best outcomes in affiliate marketing take 2-5 years to develop. Don't measure outreach ROI in 90-day windows.

### Follow up with what you promised
If you said you'd send them your spreadsheet / intro them to someone / write that guest post — DO IT. Failing here is how you become forgettable.
