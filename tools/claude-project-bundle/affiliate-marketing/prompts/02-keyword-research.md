# Prompts — Keyword Research

For finding topics people are actually searching for. Skip this and you waste months writing content nobody wants.

## Seed keywords

### KR-01: Seed keyword generator
```
My niche: [NICHE]
Audience: [READER]

Generate 30-50 seed keywords — short phrases someone in this niche might type into Google:
- Informational: "how to X", "what is X"
- Commercial: "best X", "X review", "X vs Y"
- Navigational: brand-specific searches
- Local: if relevant, location-based

Group by intent type. Don't invent search volumes (I'll check separately). Focus on variety.
```

### KR-02: Long-tail expansion
```
Seed keyword: [PHRASE]

Generate 20 long-tail variations (4-8 words each):
- Questions: "how to [X] without [Y]", "what [X] works best for [SPECIFIC AUDIENCE]"
- Specific scenarios: "[X] for [small yard / beginners / over 60 / etc.]"
- Problem-based: "why does [X] [COMMON ISSUE]"
- Comparison: "[X] vs [Y] for [USE CASE]"
- Budget-aware: "best cheap [X]", "affordable [X]"

Long-tails are where small sites win. Rank easier, converts higher.
```

## Intent analysis

### KR-03: What does the searcher actually want?
```
Keyword: [KEYWORD]

Analyze search intent:
- Informational (they want to learn)
- Commercial (researching a purchase)
- Transactional (ready to buy)
- Navigational (looking for specific site)
- Local (location-based)

Also:
- What's the emotional state? (frustrated? curious? urgent?)
- What stage of buying journey? (just exploring, comparing, deciding)
- What content format fits? (listicle? deep-dive? review?)
- What's the "perfect result" for them?

This determines how you write the article — not just what you write.
```

### KR-04: Reverse-engineer top-ranking content
```
For the keyword "[KEYWORD]", here are the top 10 Google results:
[PASTE URLS AND TITLES]

Analyze patterns:
- Common content structure (listicle, guide, review, comparison)
- Average word count (rough estimate)
- Common angles covered
- Common gaps (topics NOT covered — opportunity)
- Dominant brands (are there small sites in top 10 or only giants?)

What would I need to do to realistically rank here? Be honest — some keywords aren't worth pursuing.
```

## Competition assessment

### KR-05: Keyword difficulty estimate
```
Keyword: [KEYWORD]

Estimate difficulty (1-5 scale):
- 1 = Easy (small sites can rank in 3-6 months)
- 2 = Moderate-easy
- 3 = Moderate (12 months of effort)
- 4 = Hard (dominated by mid-sized sites; 18+ months)
- 5 = Impossible for new sites (giant sites + topic ownership)

Factors to consider:
- SERP composition (how many big sites)
- Content depth of top results
- Backlink authority visible in snippets
- Whether Google has "own answer" features (featured snippet, People Also Ask)
- Is there still a "knowledge gap" worth filling?

Suggest 3 alternative keywords if this one is too hard.
```

### KR-06: The "open lane" test
```
For my niche [NICHE], I'm looking for keywords where small sites still rank.

Criteria for an "open lane" keyword:
- Fewer than 5 giant sites in top 10
- Top results have visible gaps (thin content, outdated, missing angle)
- Search volume under 10,000/month (giants don't bother) but over 100/month (worth writing about)
- Commercial intent is at least moderate (can monetize)

From the list below, identify the top 5 "open lane" opportunities:
[LIST CANDIDATE KEYWORDS]

Rank them by likelihood I could rank #1-3 within 12 months with a great article.
```

## Question + topic discovery

### KR-07: Question mining
```
Topic: [TOPIC]
Audience: [READER]

Generate 30 questions real people ask about this topic:
- Beginner questions
- Intermediate questions
- Advanced questions
- Frustrated questions (troubleshooting, problems)
- Skeptical questions ("is X really worth it")
- Comparison questions ("is X better than Y")
- Specific-situation questions ("X for small apartment")

Each question should be a potential article title or FAQ entry.
```

### KR-08: Content gap finder
```
Niche: [NICHE]

What are 10 topics in this niche that:
- Real audiences ask about repeatedly
- But aren't well-covered by existing sites
- Or where existing coverage is outdated/shallow/wrong

For each:
- The topic
- Evidence it's asked about (forum threads, Reddit, YouTube comments you know of)
- Why existing content doesn't cover it well
- Rough angle I could take

Be specific. "The best tools for X" is too generic. "The best tools for X in apartments under 600 sq ft" is a gap.
```

## Content calendar

### KR-09: 90-day content roadmap
```
My niche: [NICHE]
My target keywords (from research):
[LIST 20-30]

Build a 90-day content calendar:
- Week 1-4: publish 1-2 "pillar" posts (2500+ word cornerstone content on highest-priority keywords)
- Week 5-12: publish 2-3 posts/week of "supporting content" (1000-2000 word articles on long-tail keywords that link to pillars)
- Throughout: 1-2 "quick win" posts on low-competition, long-tail keywords

For each week, list 3-5 specific article titles.

Balance:
- Commercial-intent posts (best/review/comparison): 40-50%
- Informational posts (how-to, guides, definitions): 40-50%
- Listicle / round-up posts: 10-20%
```

## Refresh existing content

### KR-10: Existing article audit
```
Article I published: [TITLE + URL]
Target keyword: [KEYWORD]
Current ranking: [POSITION]
Published: [DATE]

Analyze why it's not ranking better and suggest fixes:
- Is the article depth sufficient vs top-ranking competitors?
- Are there missing sections that top results include?
- Is the information outdated?
- Is keyword placement natural and adequate?
- Are there internal linking opportunities?
- Is the search intent match correct?

Return 5-10 specific fixes, prioritized by likely impact.
```

### KR-11: Keyword cannibalization check
```
My site has multiple articles targeting similar keywords:
[LIST ARTICLE TITLES WITH THEIR KEYWORDS]

Check for cannibalization (multiple articles competing for same search):
- Which articles are actually competing with each other?
- Should any be consolidated?
- Which is the "winner" and should be kept?
- What's the plan (merge, redirect, differentiate)?

Cannibalization hurts all articles involved. Better to have one great article than three mediocre ones.
```

## Tools + workflow

### KR-12: Free keyword tool workflow
```
I want to do keyword research using only free tools.

Step by step, describe how to:
1. Use Google Keyword Planner (free with Google Ads account, no ad spend required)
2. Use Google autocomplete + "People also ask" + "Related searches"
3. Use AnswerThePublic (free tier, limited queries)
4. Use Google Trends for trend validation
5. Use Ubersuggest free tier for difficulty estimation
6. Use Reddit / niche forums for question mining

For each tool, note:
- What to look for
- What data to extract
- How to organize findings

Workflow should take 1-2 hours per major keyword set.
```

## Keyword research principles

### Start specific, get broader
Don't begin with "cooking" — begin with "air fryer cooking for empty nesters." You can always expand later. Starting narrow gives you a chance to rank.

### Intent > volume
A keyword with 500 searches/month and clear buying intent beats one with 50,000 searches/month that's just curious browsing.

### Commercial keywords have "teeth"
"Best [X] review" and "[X] vs [Y]" convert 10-50× higher than informational content. Balance your content calendar toward commercial intent if affiliate income is the goal.

### Rankings take time
Don't judge keyword strategy in 30-60 days. Give each new piece 4-6 months to find its place in the SERPs before assessing.
