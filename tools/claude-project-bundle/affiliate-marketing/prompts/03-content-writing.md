# Prompts — Content Writing

For the articles that will actually drive your affiliate commissions. Post-HCU reality: Google rewards genuinely helpful content with demonstrated expertise.

## Article planning

### CW-01: Article outline from keyword
```
Target keyword: [KEYWORD]
Niche: [NICHE]
Audience: [READER PROFILE]

Generate a detailed article outline for a post targeting this keyword:

- Working title (3 options)
- Search intent analysis (what does the searcher actually want?)
- Suggested word count (based on top-ranking content for similar keywords)
- H2 sections (4-8 subsections)
- H3 sections under each H2
- Suggested products to mention (with affiliate relationship)
- Personal experience hooks I should add (1-2 places where I bring specific expertise)
- Internal links to my existing content (note placeholders)
- FAQ section at end (5 questions)

Return as structured markdown.
```

### CW-02: Article from existing content idea
```
I want to write about [TOPIC] for [AUDIENCE].

My angle: [WHAT'S UNIQUE ABOUT MY TAKE]
My expertise: [WHAT I BRING — personal experience, background, etc.]

Generate:
- 3 article title options that balance SEO and clickworthy
- Opening paragraph (hook the reader in 2-3 sentences)
- Full outline (as in CW-01)
- Recommended affiliate products to feature naturally
- 3 potential pull-quote moments

Don't write the full article yet — outline first so I can refine.
```

## Draft writing

### CW-03: First draft from outline
```
Write a complete article based on this outline:
[PASTE OUTLINE]

Rules:
- [TARGET WORD COUNT] words
- Write in my voice (see voice profile below)
- Include personal experience where the outline flags it
- Mention products naturally, don't force affiliate links
- Include FTC-compliant disclosure at top (placeholder)
- Use H2/H3 structure
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Occasional pull quotes for scannability

Voice profile:
[PASTE YOUR VOICE PROFILE]

After the first draft, do NOT polish. I'll edit.
```

### CW-04: Personal experience insert
```
This section of the article is generic:
[PASTE SECTION]

Add a specific personal anecdote or experience that illustrates the point. The anecdote should:
- Be grounded in a specific moment (not general)
- Include a small detail only someone who experienced it would know
- Connect explicitly to the point being made
- Be under 100 words

Context about me: [YOUR BACKGROUND / EXPERIENCE]
Product or context: [RELEVANT DETAIL]
```

### CW-05: Convert "here's the info" to "here's how I learned this"
```
This paragraph is factually correct but sounds like it could be AI-generated because it lacks perspective:
[PASTE PARAGRAPH]

Rewrite it to include:
- A first-person moment where I learned or discovered this
- Specific sensory/situational detail
- The lesson framed as hard-won, not as textbook knowledge

Context about me: [BACKGROUND]
```

## Reviews + comparisons

### CW-06: Honest product review structure
```
Product: [NAME + BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
My experience: [DID I OWN IT? FOR HOW LONG? WHAT WAS IT LIKE?]
Audience: [WHO'S READING THIS REVIEW]

Generate a detailed review outline:
- Hook: specific moment of using the product
- At-a-glance verdict (one paragraph)
- What it is (brief factual description)
- Real use cases (who's this actually for?)
- What it does well (3-5 specific points — no generic "great quality")
- Where it falls short (2-3 honest flaws — no review without flaws)
- Comparison to top 2-3 alternatives
- Who should buy it + who shouldn't
- FAQ (4-5 questions)

No fake pros. Every "pro" needs to be specific and backed by my experience. Flag anything I can't personally vouch for.
```

### CW-07: Comparison post outline
```
Compare: [PRODUCT A] vs [PRODUCT B] (and optionally [PRODUCT C])
Use case: [WHAT PEOPLE ARE DECIDING BETWEEN FOR]
Audience: [READER]

Structure:
- Opening: who this comparison is really for
- At-a-glance winner per category
- Feature-by-feature comparison table
- Deep dives: price, key features, ease of use, support, niche fit
- Winner for different reader types (specific scenarios)
- My recommendation with honest reasoning

Include a comparison table. Include nuance — not just "A is better." Real buyers have different needs.
```

## Roundup posts

### CW-08: "Best X for Y" roundup
```
Topic: "Best [PRODUCTS] for [SPECIFIC USE CASE/AUDIENCE]"
Number to include: 5-10
My criteria for inclusion: [STATE YOUR CRITERIA]

Generate:
- Introduction: who this list is for, how I chose
- Brief "at-a-glance" recommendations by sub-need:
  - Best overall: [X]
  - Best budget: [X]
  - Best for beginners: [X]
  - Best for [SPECIFIC SCENARIO]: [X]
- Detailed writeup for each product (200-300 words each):
  - What it is
  - Best for
  - Key features (specific, not "high-quality")
  - Pros
  - Cons (each product needs real cons)
  - Price range
  - Alternative to consider if this isn't right
- Buying guide section at the end (what to look for when choosing)
- FAQ

Do NOT rank products based on commission alone — rank based on genuine fit for reader.
```

## Editing passes

### CW-09: Structural edit
```
Review this draft and give me 5-10 specific structural suggestions:
[PASTE DRAFT]

Look for:
- Does the flow make sense? Are there logic jumps?
- Is the opening strong enough?
- Are there sections that should be shorter or cut?
- Are there topics that need deeper coverage?
- Does the conclusion land?

Don't rewrite — just suggest edits I can implement.
```

### CW-10: SEO optimization pass
```
Article draft:
[PASTE]

Target keyword: [PRIMARY KEYWORD]
Secondary keywords: [LIST]

Check:
- Is the primary keyword in: title, H1, first 100 words, at least one H2, meta description, URL slug? (report what's missing)
- Are secondary keywords distributed naturally?
- Is keyword density natural (not stuffed)?
- Are there related/LSI terms I should add?
- What's the Flesch reading ease (estimate)?
- Are there link opportunities to my other content?

Suggest specific fixes. Don't rewrite the full article.
```

### CW-11: Humanize (anti-AI detection)
```
This draft reads too "AI generic." Make it sound human.

[PASTE DRAFT]

Apply these fixes:
- Add specific details only a human writer would include
- Vary sentence length more dramatically
- Break up overly clean logical flow with tangential observations
- Add one small self-deprecating or personal moment
- Replace generic words with specific ones
- Remove any corporate-speak

Keep the structure and facts. Change the feel.
```

### CW-12: Add voice across the piece
```
This article is informational but flat. My voice profile:
[PASTE VOICE PROFILE]

Go through the article and add 4-6 voice moments where my perspective, personality, or experience would show through:

[PASTE DRAFT]

Identify each moment and suggest 1-2 sentences to add there. Don't rewrite — suggest insertions.
```

## FAQ + supporting content

### CW-13: FAQ section from article
```
Based on this article about [TOPIC]:
[PASTE]

Generate 6-8 FAQ questions that:
- Are what real readers would ask after reading
- Aren't already answered directly in the body
- Include at least 2 "searchable" questions that might rank independently
- Include at least 1 buying-intent question that could drive an affiliate click

For each, write a clear answer under 100 words.
```

### CW-14: Internal link suggestions
```
Article I just wrote:
[PASTE]

My existing content (other articles on my site):
[LIST OF EXISTING ARTICLE TITLES/URLS]

Suggest 5-10 places in my new article where I could link to existing content:
- The sentence/phrase to link on (anchor text)
- Which existing article to link to
- Why this link makes sense (topical relevance + reader value)
```

---

## Writing rules

- Never publish AI output raw. Human editing adds 40% of the value.
- Every claim needs to be either widely known or personally sourced.
- "As a seasoned expert" and similar phrases are AI tells. Don't use.
- Show, don't tell. "I use this every morning for coffee" beats "I highly recommend it."
- Depth beats breadth. One great 3,000-word article beats five 800-word articles.
