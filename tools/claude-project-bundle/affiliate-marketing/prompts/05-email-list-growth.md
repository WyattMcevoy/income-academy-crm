# Prompts — Email List Growth & Monetization

For building the email list that becomes your most valuable asset — independent of Google, algorithms, and platforms.

## List building

### EL-01: Lead magnet idea brainstorm
```
My niche: [NICHE]
My audience: [READER]
My goal: get them on my email list

Generate 10 lead magnet ideas that would be genuinely valuable:
- Cheat sheets (specific, actionable)
- Templates (immediately usable)
- Checklists (step-by-step)
- Calculators (interactive)
- Starter guides (condensed how-to)
- Case studies (specific example)
- Free mini-course (3-5 email sequence)
- Challenge (7-day email series)

For each:
- Concept name
- What it solves
- Production effort (low/medium/high)
- Estimated opt-in rate for this niche
- How to justify it vs just giving info away

Rank by value-for-effort ratio.
```

### EL-02: Lead magnet content outline
```
Lead magnet concept: [CONCEPT]
Format: [PDF / video series / email course / etc.]
Audience: [READER]
Promise to reader: [WHAT THEY'LL LEARN/GET]

Generate the full outline:
- Intro (why this matters, what they'll get)
- Section 1-5 (specific content, detailed)
- Quick-start action items
- Final call-to-action (subtle — they're on the list now)

Goal: something they actually use, not something they download and forget. Useful > comprehensive.
```

### EL-03: Opt-in form copy
```
For my lead magnet: [CONCEPT]

Write opt-in form copy for these placements:
- Inline (within an article): 30-50 words
- Exit-intent popup: 40-60 words
- Footer CTA: 20-30 words
- Blog sidebar: 40-60 words
- Dedicated landing page: 200-300 words

Each should:
- Lead with the outcome (not the feature)
- Overcome the "will my email get spammed?" objection
- Have a clear CTA button text
- Include social proof if relevant

Write 3 variants of each for A/B testing.
```

## Welcome sequence

### EL-04: Welcome email #1 (delivery + intro)
```
New subscriber just signed up for: [LEAD MAGNET]
My voice: [VOICE PROFILE]
My niche: [NICHE]

Write welcome email #1:
- Delivers the promised lead magnet (link or attachment)
- Introduces me briefly (not my whole life story)
- Sets expectations: how often will they hear from me?
- Soft ask: reply with their #1 challenge (builds engagement + reveals what they actually want)
- Length: under 300 words
- Subject line options: 3 variants
- No affiliate links yet
```

### EL-05: Welcome email #2 (trust builder)
```
Subscriber is now 2-3 days in. They got the lead magnet.

Write email #2:
- Share one specific tip/insight not in the lead magnet
- Tell one short personal story that relates
- Introduce one tool/resource (NOT an affiliate link yet)
- Ask one specific engagement question
- Length: 400-600 words
- Subject line options: 3

Goal: prove I'm consistently valuable. No selling yet.
```

### EL-06: Welcome email #3 (first recommendation)
```
Subscriber is 5-7 days in. They've received value twice.

Now write email #3:
- Tell a problem-solving story (specific, hands-on)
- Show how [PRODUCT I'M AFFILIATED WITH] helped (or would help)
- Include FTC disclosure inline
- Affiliate link feels natural, not forced
- Give them a clear next step (read full review? try it? reply with question?)
- Length: 500-700 words
- Subject line options: 3

First sale attempt — don't push too hard. Still building trust.
```

### EL-07: Welcome email #4 (segmentation + path)
```
Subscriber is 10-14 days in. Time to understand who they are and what they want.

Write email #4:
- Offer a "choose your path" structure
- Give 3 different segments/interests and what content fits each
- Link to clicks that tag them in MailerLite (so I can segment automation)
- Explain what they can expect going forward

Length: 300-500 words. This email drives most of my future segmentation.
```

## Weekly/regular newsletter

### EL-08: Weekly newsletter template
```
My niche: [NICHE]
Audience: [READER]
Newsletter day/time: [WHEN]

Create a standard template for my weekly newsletter:

Structure:
- Personal intro (opening — 2-4 sentences, warm)
- Main section: one specific lesson/tip/insight (300-500 words)
- "This week I liked" (short — 1-3 items: an article, tool, product, book)
- "Your move" (one specific action reader can take this week)
- Affiliate recommendation (if relevant) with disclosure
- Sign-off

Keep it scannable. Each section should stand alone.
```

### EL-09: Weekly email from blog post
```
I just published this article:
[PASTE]

Turn it into a weekly newsletter email:
- Not a summary — give readers a REASON the article matters to them
- Tease the key insight (don't give everything away)
- Personal story/angle that connects
- Link to read more
- One related recommendation (affiliate link if relevant)

Length: 300-500 words.
Subject line options: 3 (clickworthy but not clickbait)
```

### EL-10: Reader question spotlight
```
A reader replied with this question:
[PASTE]

Write a newsletter email that:
- Acknowledges the question publicly (with permission)
- Gives a thorough, generous answer (400-600 words)
- Opens up related topic for broader discussion
- Invites other readers to reply with their questions

Goal: encourage engagement. Readers feel seen. I get more questions that can become content.
```

## Promotional emails

### EL-11: Affiliate product promo
```
Product: [NAME]
Why I believe in it: [GENUINE REASON]
My experience: [HOW I'VE USED IT]
Target audience within my list: [WHO THIS IS FOR]

Write a promo email:
- Don't lead with the product — lead with the problem it solves
- Tell a story about why I care about this problem
- Naturally arrive at the product as my solution
- Transparent about affiliate relationship
- One clear CTA (link to my review, or direct to product)
- Length: 400-600 words

Send this ONLY to subscribers who fit the audience (use segments).
Subject line options: 3
```

### EL-12: "Last chance" email (if product has a legit deadline)
```
Product: [NAME]
Deadline: [REAL LAUNCH WINDOW, PRICE INCREASE, or LIMITED AVAILABILITY]
What makes the deadline real: [EXPLAIN — no fake urgency]

Write a final push email:
- Acknowledge I've mentioned this before
- Remind of the specific deadline and why it's real (not manufactured)
- Brief reminder of WHY this product
- Clear CTA
- Short email (200-400 words)

Do NOT use fake scarcity. Only send this if there's a real reason to mention a deadline.
```

### EL-13: Multi-email sequence for major promotion
```
Promoting: [PRODUCT / LAUNCH]
Send window: [DATES — typically 5-7 days for a product promo]
Audience: [SEGMENT]

Generate a 4-email sequence:

Email 1 (Day 1): The setup — share a story that frames the problem this product solves
Email 2 (Day 3): The product introduction — what it is, why me, who it's for
Email 3 (Day 5): Handling objections — common concerns I'd have addressed directly
Email 4 (Day 7): Last call — final benefit + real deadline

Each email: 400-600 words, one clear CTA, FTC disclosure inline when affiliate.
Subject lines: 3 variants per email.
```

## Re-engagement

### EL-14: Win-back email for inactive subscribers
```
Target: subscribers who haven't opened an email in 90+ days.

Write a win-back email:
- Acknowledge they've been quiet
- Ask if they still want to hear from me
- Make the unsubscribe easy (not hidden)
- Share one thing I've made since we last connected
- No pressure

Length: under 200 words. Tone: genuine, not guilt-trippy.
```

### EL-15: List clean-up email
```
Sending a "are you still with us?" to dormant subscribers before I remove them.

Write:
- Subject line: gentle, not alarming
- Body: clear that inaction will unsubscribe them in [N] days
- Give them a one-click "yes I want to stay" button
- Brief reminder of what they get by staying

This protects my deliverability (sending to engaged subs = better inbox placement).
```

## Analytics + optimization

### EL-16: Newsletter performance analysis
```
Recent newsletter sends:
[PASTE DATA — subject lines, open rate, click rate, unsubs]

Analyze patterns:
- Subject lines that outperformed (what's the pattern?)
- Content types that got most clicks
- Days/times that performed best
- Subject line length correlation
- Emoji vs no-emoji
- Personalization impact

Give me 3-5 optimizations to test in the next 30 days.
```

### EL-17: A/B test design
```
I want to A/B test: [HYPOTHESIS, e.g., "shorter subject lines convert better"]

Design the test:
- Variant A: [CONTROL]
- Variant B: [TREATMENT]
- What to measure (open rate? click rate? conversion?)
- Split size (50/50 or 90/10 send)
- How long to run
- Statistical significance threshold (rule of thumb: need 500+ per variant before calling it)

Return a clear test plan I can execute in MailerLite.
```

## Email rules

### Never buy a list
Real listservs hurt deliverability within a week. Your open rates tank, everyone gets caught in spam filters. Just don't.

### One topic per email
Every newsletter should have ONE main point. Bullet lists of 5 random things = less effective than one good deep idea.

### Reply-to matters
Your "reply-to" address should be one where replies actually reach you. Noreply@ addresses tell readers you don't care what they think.

### Segmentation is power
A list of 1,000 engaged subscribers in a specific niche is worth 10× more than 10,000 random subscribers. Prioritize quality.

### Deliverability is a long game
Clean list + engaged subs + good content + proper domain authentication = emails land in primary inbox. The opposite = spam folder.
