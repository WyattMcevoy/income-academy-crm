# Monthly Client Report Format

Structure for the monthly deliverable package to each client. Consistency matters — clients start expecting the format and appreciate it.

---

## Delivery

- **When**: no later than the 15th of the following month (close by 12th, deliver by 15th)
- **How**: email with PDF attachments + links to live reports in QBO/Xero
- **To whom**: client's designated contact (usually owner/CEO). If there's a separate controller or CFO, CC them.

---

## Email template

**Subject**: [CLIENT BUSINESS NAME] — [MONTH YEAR] Financial Reports

**Body**:

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

Attached are your [MONTH YEAR] financial reports. Highlights below.

## This month at a glance

Revenue: $[X] (vs. $[PRIOR MONTH] = [UP/DOWN BY %])
Expenses: $[X] (vs. $[PRIOR MONTH])
Net income: $[X]
Cash position (end of month): $[X]

## One-paragraph summary

[2-4 sentences explaining what happened this month in plain English.
Include WHY, not just WHAT. Reference specific drivers: "a larger
project invoice paid out", "seasonal slowdown", "the new retainer
client started", etc.]

## Things I want to flag

[List 2-4 things the client should know — items that aren't automatic
in the P&L but matter to them. Examples:
- "One invoice is at 45 days past due: Acme Co., $3,200. May want to
  follow up."
- "You drew $4,500 from owner's account this month, up from $3,000
  average. Worth flagging as we plan for Q3 estimated payments."
- "There's a $2,100 transaction I couldn't categorize from [DATE].
  It's from Home Depot — was this a store purchase or a project
  material? Let me know so I can reclassify if needed."]

## Attached reports

- Profit & Loss (standard)
- Balance Sheet
- Cash Flow
- A/R Aging Summary
- Any custom report you've requested

## Anything you want different?

Let me know if you'd like a specific angle or additional report next
month. Or if there's something you don't look at and would rather
skip, happy to simplify.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]

[YOUR BUSINESS NAME]
[YOUR EMAIL]
```

---

## Report checklist (before hitting send)

Before you email, verify:

- [ ] All bank and credit card accounts reconciled
- [ ] No uncategorized transactions (either categorized or flagged in the email for client clarification)
- [ ] Balance Sheet balances (assets = liabilities + equity)
- [ ] Opening balances match prior month's closing
- [ ] Any unusual transactions flagged in the "things I want to flag" section
- [ ] PDF exports look readable (no weird page breaks, numbers are formatted)
- [ ] Summary numbers in email match the attached reports (don't let yourself quote a wrong revenue number)

---

## Quarterly bonus: a half-page trend view

At the end of each quarter (months 3, 6, 9, 12), include a simple trend view:

```
## Q[N] Trends

Revenue last 3 months: $[M1], $[M2], $[M3] (trend: up/flat/down)
Net income last 3 months: $[M1], $[M2], $[M3]
Average monthly cash position: $[X]

Notable quarterly observations:
- [insight based on the quarter's data]
- [insight that would help their planning]
```

This is a small value-add that costs you 15 minutes per quarter and makes you look strategic. Clients remember this.

---

## Annual deliverable: year-end letter (January)

In January, write a 1-page year-end summary letter for the prior year. Cover:

- Total revenue / expenses / net income for the year
- Biggest categories of spending
- Cash flow summary (starting cash → ending cash)
- Major changes vs. prior year
- Anything they should discuss with their CPA at tax prep time (large one-time items, new loan activity, etc.)

Send this early in January so it's useful for their tax prep. Your CPA relationship improves when the CPA receives a year-end letter FROM the bookkeeper TO the client (you can CC them with permission).
