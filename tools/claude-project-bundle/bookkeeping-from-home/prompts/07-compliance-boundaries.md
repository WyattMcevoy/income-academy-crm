# Prompts — Compliance & Boundaries

Staying on the right side of the bookkeeping / tax-prep / CPA line.

## BK-60: Identify if something crosses the line

```
My client asked me to [SPECIFIC TASK — e.g., "help them decide if
they should elect S-corp status", "prepare their 1099 forms",
"suggest a better retirement plan strategy", "advise on whether to
hire an employee vs. contractor"].

Evaluate:
1. Is this within the scope of bookkeeping, OR does it require CPA /
   EA / attorney / financial advisor credentials?
2. What's the legal risk to me if I help with this without
   credentials?
3. If it's over the line, what's the closest thing I CAN help with
   that's legit?
4. Who should they actually talk to?

Be specific and err on the side of caution. A misstep here can end
my business and create liability for the client.
```

## BK-61: State-specific licensing research prompt

```
I'm building my bookkeeping business in [STATE]. What are the
specific rules I need to follow?

Research and summarize:
1. Does [STATE] require a license for bookkeeping services? (as of
   [CURRENT DATE])
2. Are there specific rules about operating from home? (home
   occupation permits, zoning)
3. Sales tax — do I need to charge sales tax on bookkeeping
   services in this state? (most states don't, but some do)
4. Business registration requirements (LLC vs. sole prop decisions,
   annual filing requirements)
5. Any specific signage, disclosure, or advertising rules for
   financial-adjacent services

Flag what I should verify with an attorney for my state (because
you can't give legal advice — the prompts here are starting points
for my own research).
```

## BK-62: E&O insurance shopping

```
I want to buy Errors & Omissions (E&O) insurance for my bookkeeping
business. My situation:
- [# ACTIVE CLIENTS]
- [$X MONTHLY REVENUE]
- [INDUSTRIES SERVED]
- [STATE OF OPERATION]

Walk me through:
1. What coverage amount is appropriate for my volume (typical range
   is $100K-$1M aggregate)
2. Specific exclusions to watch for (some policies exclude "financial
   advice", some exclude tax-related work I don't do, etc.)
3. Specific inclusions I should confirm (cyber liability, subpoena
   coverage, defense costs)
4. Reputable carriers for bookkeeper E&O (typical names: Hiscox, CPH
   & Associates, BookkeepingInsurance.com)
5. Typical cost range for my profile ($300-$800/year)
6. Whether I also need General Liability, Cyber, or other policies

Questions I should ask each carrier for apples-to-apples comparison.
```

## BK-63: When a client asks about their own taxes

```
My client just asked me a tax question — [SPECIFIC QUESTION, e.g.,
"should I make a quarterly estimated payment this month?" or "can I
deduct this business trip?"]

Write me a response that:
- Declines to give tax advice (I'm not a CPA/EA)
- Acknowledges I see the question and why it matters
- Offers what I CAN provide (the numbers they'd need to make the
  decision with their CPA — e.g., "I can pull your YTD net income
  so your CPA can calculate the estimated payment")
- Directs them to talk to their CPA / recommend they engage one if
  they don't have one

Tone: helpful, clear on scope, not condescending. "I don't give tax
advice, but I can hand you the right numbers to bring to your CPA"
is the play.
```

## BK-64: FTC-safe marketing for my bookkeeping practice

```
I'm writing marketing copy for my bookkeeping services. Help me
avoid FTC compliance issues.

Draft language I want to use:
[PASTE YOUR DRAFT MARKETING COPY]

Review it for:
1. Earnings claims (my own or implied for my clients) — any numbers
   I can't substantiate?
2. Testimonials — am I using any that might be considered misleading
   or unsubstantiated?
3. "Guaranteed" language — anything I'm guaranteeing that I shouldn't?
4. Professional designation implications (claims that imply CPA/EA
   qualifications I don't have)
5. Service scope misrepresentation — any copy that implies tax
   preparation or advice?

Rewrite problem language with compliant alternatives.

Tone: still confident and compelling, but FTC-safe. Boring compliance
doesn't have to mean boring copy.
```
