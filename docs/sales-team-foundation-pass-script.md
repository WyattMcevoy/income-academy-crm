# Foundation Pass Sales Team Script

Internal reference for the phone team handling inbound leads from the Foundation Pass website + the eBay Done-With-You applications.

**Purpose**: consistent voice across the team, faster ramp-up for new hires, more Foundation Pass buyers converted, more qualified eBay DWY applicants progressed to close.

---

## The 3 lead types you'll encounter

### 1. Foundation Pass prospect (pre-purchase)
- Source: chat widget, email inquiry, contact form
- State: haven't bought the $47 + $19/mo Foundation Pass yet, have questions
- Goal: answer their question honestly, get them to buy (or decide not to — both are fine)

### 2. Active Foundation Pass member (post-purchase, pre-eBay-apply)
- Source: existing CRM record
- State: paid the $47, inside their $19/mo membership, learning the courses
- Goal: help them succeed in the course they picked, plant the eBay DWY seed when appropriate

### 3. eBay Done-With-You applicant (high-intent, phone-close)
- Source: submitted the `/apply-ebay` form
- State: sourcing $1,500+/mo inventory, ready to graduate to our warehouse program
- Goal: qualify hard, close fit applicants, politely decline wrong fit

## Opening scripts

### Foundation Pass prospect — inbound chat or call

> "Hi, this is [Name] from Income Academy. You reached out with a question about the Foundation Pass — thanks for giving us a chance to answer it honestly. What are you trying to figure out?"

Key framing:
- Not salesy
- "Honestly" signals we're different from the courses they've been burned by
- Open-ended question — let them reveal what matters

### Foundation Pass member — proactive check-in call

> "Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from Income Academy. Not selling you anything — just checking in. You joined [X weeks] ago — how's it going? Any of the courses clicking for you?"

Key framing:
- "Not selling you anything" upfront disarms
- Specific time anchor ("X weeks ago") shows you know them
- Open-ended — let them drive

### eBay DWY applicant — scheduled consultation

> "Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from Income Academy. Thanks for submitting your application — I've read through it. Before I tell you about the program, I want to make sure I understand where you're at. Walk me through what you're sourcing in a typical week."

Key framing:
- Acknowledge their application was read (not dismissed)
- "Before I tell you about the program" = earn the right to pitch
- Qualifying-first framing sets the tone

## Handling common objections

### "I've been burned by courses before."
> "Me too, and half the people in your situation say that before they buy from us. Here's how we're different: $47 today, $19/mo starting after a 7-day free trial, 7-day money-back guarantee on the $47, and you can cancel the membership in one click. If we're not right for you, you walk away without paying anything after that first week. That's about as low-risk as we can make it."

### "I'm not good with technology."
> "What can you do now? Email? Gmail? Online banking? If you can do those three things, you can use our courses. The AI Side Income course specifically has a Module 1 that assumes zero technical background. And the AI Writing Assistant is literally a chatbox — you type questions, it answers. Nothing to install."

### "I don't have time."
> "Totally fair. Here's what our typical member does: 20 minutes a day. That's it. Not 2 hours. Not 'evenings and weekends.' Twenty minutes with your morning coffee or before dinner, 5-6 days a week. In three months you finish a course. Most people feel like they don't have time because they're picturing a time-intensive course — ours isn't."

### "How much can I really earn?"
> (Be careful here — FTC Biz Opp Rule territory) "Honest answer is: it depends entirely on you. The AI Side Income course teaches services you can charge $300-800/month per client for — with 3 clients, that's $1,000-2,500/mo. The Bookkeeping course similarly: 3-5 clients at $300-500/mo each. Affiliate Marketing is slow — typical year 1 is $0-500/mo, real money takes 12-24 months. Estate Sale Sourcing depends on your local market — $300-1,500/mo part-time year 1 is typical. These are ranges, not promises. Many earn less. Some earn more. The only guarantee is we don't guarantee anything."

### "Why a bundle of 4 courses and not just one?"
> "Because most people don't know which one will click for them until they try all four. That's the whole point. You pay once, you get four options, you pick the one that fits YOU, and you go deep. If you knew for sure you wanted bookkeeping, you could buy Bookkeeper Launch for $3,000. But most people aren't sure, and our $47 bundle lets you test before you commit."

### "Is your stuff all AI-generated?"
> "Fair question. The courses are written by humans (no generic AI blog slop). We use AI as a research assistant for things like keyword analysis and trend spotting — same way a good writer uses Google. But the voice, the decisions about what to include, the honest takes — those are human. The written landing pages and the course bodies are reviewed by a human before anything ships."

### "I'm 68 / 70 / 72 — am I too old?"
> "Our course is literally built for 55-75. The reason you're asking is probably because every other 'online income' course is marketed at 25-year-olds and assumes you grew up with smartphones. Ours doesn't. Our members are your age, and honestly, older members tend to succeed MORE because they're patient and they don't chase shortcuts."

## eBay Done-With-You qualification

Only progress to the DWY pitch if they hit these criteria on the qualifying call:

| Criterion | Pass | Red flag |
|---|---|---|
| Current sourcing | Consistently $1,500+/mo | Under $500/mo, inconsistent |
| Ability to ship inventory to warehouse | Yes, willing | "I'd rather keep in my garage" |
| Time horizon | 12+ months commitment | Wants fast results |
| Cash reserves | Can float $500-1000 in inventory | Needs DWY to "save them" financially |
| Attitude | Patient, coachable, honest | Argues on every answer, over-promises |

**Decline script** (for wrong-fit applicants):
> "Based on what you've shared, I don't think the Done-With-You program is the right fit right now. The reason is [specific reason — be honest]. What I'd recommend is [specific alternative — finish the Estate Sale Sourcing course, build more sourcing consistency first, try the solo reselling path for 6 months, etc.]. We can talk again when you've hit [specific milestone]."

Don't push. A reluctant DWY client is worse than no client.

**Advance script** (for fit applicants):
> "Here's how Done-With-You works: [explain the warehouse + photography + listing + shipping setup]. The investment is [price range — don't quote exact unless you're ready to close]. A typical member in this program does [realistic income range from verified members, with earnings disclaimer]. Before I tell you the exact price, do you want to see a few case studies of current DWY members, and then we'll talk about whether this is a fit for you specifically?"

Stall on price until they've seen evidence + commitment signals.

## Always-compliant language

### Use these
- "Typical range is $X to $Y per month, based on our experience with current members"
- "Results vary — we make no guarantees"
- "Earning depends on effort, market, timing, and factors outside our control"
- "Most members in [Course X] who work [Y hours/week] earn [Z range]"

### Never use these
- "Passive income"
- "Push button profits"
- "Make money while you sleep"
- "X per month guaranteed"
- "Everyone earns at least $X"
- "Six-figure potential"

### When in doubt
Err toward understating. The worst outcome is FTC scrutiny; understating is always safer than overstating.

---

## Decline conversations gracefully

Some prospects won't buy. Some applicants won't be DWY fits. Both are normal. How you handle "no" determines whether they recommend you.

Use:
> "No problem — Foundation Pass isn't right for everyone, and I'd rather you walk away than buy something that's wrong for you. If you have friends or family in your age group who are thinking about the same path, send them our way. And if anything changes, we're here."

Then actually let them go. Don't follow up aggressively.

---

## Escalation + handoffs

- Technical question (can't log in, missing course access): hand to support@incomeacademy.biz, promise resolution within 1 business day
- Refund request: honor immediately if within 7 days. No "why are you leaving" gauntlet. Ask ONE optional question: "Is there one thing we could have done differently?" — for product insight.
- Legal question (is X allowed in my state, should I file taxes as Y): "We can't advise — please talk to a CPA or attorney. Happy to recommend sources if useful."
- Wants to talk to "the founder": pass to [YOUR NAME]. Brief them first via Slack/internal channel.

## Weekly calibration (team habit)

End of each week, review together:
- Biggest objection heard most often → refine the response above
- One success story (what worked in a close)
- One loss analysis (what we could have done better)
- Any compliance concerns (someone asked for earnings promises, etc.)

Update this script quarterly. Keep voice consistent. Stay honest. Long game wins.
