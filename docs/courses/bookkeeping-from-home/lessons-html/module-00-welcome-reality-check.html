The line you don't cross

This is the most important paragraph of the entire course. Read it twice.

You are a BOOKKEEPER, not an ACCOUNTANT, not a CPA, not a TAX PREPARER, not a FINANCIAL ADVISOR.

What this means in practice:

You DO:

  • Categorize transactions (this expense is "office supplies")
  • Reconcile accounts (the bank statement matches the books)
  • Generate reports (here's last month's P&L)
  • Send invoices and chase payments
  • Maintain accurate books

You DO NOT:

  • File tax returns
  • Give tax advice ("Is this deductible?" -> "That's a question for your CPA")
  • Make investment recommendations
  • Sign anything as the financial responsible party

This isn't optional. Crossing this line is illegal in many states. And even where it's legal, it's bad business - you can be sued for incorrect tax advice.

Stay in your lane. Bookkeeping is a great business. Trying to upsell into tax/finance gets you in trouble.

A word on what's realistic

Setting you up for success, not surprises.

  • Months 1 to 2: Setup. Software certifications. Maybe 1 client to start. Income: $0 to $400.

  • Months 3 to 4: First steady clients. Building experience. Income: $500 to $1,500/month.

  • Months 5 to 6: 2-3 active clients on retainer. Income: $1,500 to $3,000/month.

  • Months 7 to 12: 4-6 clients on retainer. Income: $3,000 to $5,000/month.

  • Year 2+: Established practice. Income: $4,000 to $8,000/month is realistic for part-time work.


The slow part is the first 3 months when you're learning + landing first clients. After that, this is one of the most consistent income streams available.

A word on AI

We're going to use AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude) throughout this course. They speed up many parts of bookkeeping:

But the AI doesn't do the work for you. Bookkeeping requires judgment - knowing what's actually "office supplies" vs. "meals and entertainment." The AI is your assistant, not your replacement.

If you got stuck

"I'm worried I don't know enough about accounting"

You don't need to be an accountant. You need to know enough to do basic bookkeeping accurately, with software handling the math.

Module 1 explains the line clearly. Most clients need basic bookkeeping (clean records, categorized transactions, monthly reports). They handle the rest with their CPA.

"I'm intimidated by software"

QuickBooks and Xero are designed for small business owners - meaning they're designed to be simple. You'll be fine.

Both have free training materials. Both have certification programs (basic certification: 4 to 6 hours of self-paced video).
