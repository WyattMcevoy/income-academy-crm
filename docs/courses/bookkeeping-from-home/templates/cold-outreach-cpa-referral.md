# CPA Referral Outreach Sequence

A 3-email sequence to build a bookkeeping referral relationship with CPAs. Most bookkeeper businesses get 50-80% of their clients from CPA referrals. This is the channel that matters most.

**Goal**: become a CPA firm's go-to for small-business bookkeeping referrals. They focus on tax + advisory, you handle the monthly books, clients are better served, everyone wins.

Send email 1 cold. If no response, wait 10-14 days and send email 2. If still no response, wait 14 days and send email 3. After email 3, move on — they may remember you later.

---

## Email 1 — Initial outreach

**Subject**: Bookkeeping referral partner — [YOUR CITY]

**Body**:

```
Hi [CPA FIRST NAME],

I'm [YOUR NAME] — a bookkeeper based in [YOUR CITY], serving
[SPECIFIC NICHE — e.g., "small construction contractors" or
"solopreneur service businesses"].

I noticed your practice serves [SPECIFIC CLIENT TYPE], which is
exactly where I focus. My thought: I could be useful as a referral
partner for the inevitable question "do you know a good bookkeeper
who'll actually keep my books clean through tax season?"

What I do that saves you time during tax prep:
- Monthly reconciliations (your clients come to you with books
  ready to prepare, not a shoebox)
- Clear chart of accounts (no surprises when you dig in)
- Monthly summary notes (you read 3 sentences, not 30 transactions)

What I don't do — intentionally:
- Tax prep or advice (your territory, not mine)
- Legal guidance
- Financial advisory

If you've got a client who'd benefit from this, I'd love to be
someone you trust to refer. And if there's a fit for 15 minutes
to learn about your practice, I'm available [SUGGESTED TIMES —
e.g., "Tuesday or Thursday afternoons next week"].

Either way — thanks for your time.

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR BUSINESS NAME]
[YOUR PHONE]
[YOUR EMAIL]
[LINK TO YOUR WEBSITE if you have one]
```

---

## Email 2 — Gentle follow-up (10-14 days later)

**Subject**: Quick bump — bookkeeping referral partner

**Body**:

```
Hi [CPA FIRST NAME],

Following up briefly. I know CPAs get pitched a lot, so this is a
quick ping and no pressure.

I'm still keen to be on your radar as a bookkeeping referral
partner. If you want a 5-minute call rather than 15, I can do that
too — whatever's easiest.

In case it's useful, here are two things most CPAs I work with
appreciate:

1. Monthly "heads up" emails to the CPA when I see anything unusual
   in a client's books (large unclassified transactions, unusual
   cash movement, new loans I notice). Saves you from being
   surprised in April.

2. Tax-ready trial balances and exports at year-end, in the format
   your prep software prefers (ProSystem fx, Lacerte, etc. — happy
   to match whatever you use).

If I sound like a fit for your referral list, reply with a thumbs
up and I'll follow up to coordinate.

Thanks,
[YOUR NAME]
```

---

## Email 3 — Final touch (14 days after email 2)

**Subject**: One more note — [YOUR NAME]

**Body**:

```
Hi [CPA FIRST NAME],

Last note from me on this — I won't pile up your inbox.

If you've got a bookkeeping referral need in the future, I'm at
[YOUR PHONE]. I'll also keep an ear out for CPA referrals I can
send your way from my network — if you let me know your ideal
client profile, I'll try to match.

Either way, best of luck with the practice. I respect what your
role takes.

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR BUSINESS NAME]
[YOUR EMAIL]
```

---

## Follow-up tactics beyond the 3-email sequence

If email 3 gets no response:

1. **LinkedIn connection request** with a short personal note ("sent you an email a few weeks ago — would welcome a connection either way")
2. **Attend one event** their firm's partners are known to attend (local Chamber of Commerce, industry meetup)
3. **Drop off a small thank-you** at their office for nothing — a handwritten card after tax season saying "I imagine that was brutal — hope you get to take a break"
4. **Revisit in 6 months** with a seasonal reason (new tax year, new fiscal year for clients)

Most CPA relationships take 3-6 touches to start, not 1. Don't burn through your list on one cold email.

---

## Once you get a CPA relationship going

**First client they refer**:
- Treat it like it's your only client for the first 3 months
- Monthly "FYI" email to the CPA with anything they should know
- Deliver tax-ready files before they're asked
- Thank the CPA explicitly, not just the client

**Thank the CPA meaningfully (not gift card spam)**:
- Send a handwritten card at year-end
- Send them a referral back when you find a business needing tax work
- Post about them on LinkedIn (with their permission) as a referral partner
- NEVER pay a referral kickback — it's unethical for CPAs in most states

CPA relationships compound. One CPA who trusts you sends 3-5 clients/year. 3 good CPA relationships = a full book without ever doing outbound marketing.
