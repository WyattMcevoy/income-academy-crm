# Services Menu Worksheet — Bookkeeping From Home, Module 2

**Goal**: by the end of this worksheet you have a written services menu you can hand a prospect on a discovery call. No vague "I do bookkeeping" — specific deliverables, specific cadence, specific prices.

**Time required**: 45–60 minutes. This document becomes the backbone of your engagement letter (Module 6) and your outreach pitch (Module 4).

---

## Part 1 — Pick your services

For each row, mark Yes/No. If you don't know what something is, that's a signal NOT to offer it yet — you can add later.

| Service | What it actually is | Offer it? |
|---|---|---|
| **Monthly close** | Reconcile bank/credit cards, categorize transactions, deliver monthly P&L + balance sheet | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| **Transaction categorization only** | Cheap retainer — you categorize, client reviews | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| **Cleanup / catchup** | One-time project to fix 6–24 months of messy books | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| **AR / AP management** | Send invoices, chase payments, pay bills | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| **Sales tax filing** | File monthly/quarterly sales tax in 1–3 states (NOT income tax) | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| **1099 prep at year-end** | Issue 1099s to client's contractors (you do NOT file their taxes) | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| **QBO setup** | One-time service: set up chart of accounts, connect bank, configure | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| **Custom reports** | Beyond standard P&L — class tracking, project profitability, etc. | ☐ Yes ☐ No |

Pick 3–5 services for your initial menu. **More is not better.** Specialization signals professionalism.

## Part 3 — Pricing reality check

The 2026 market data from Module 0:

| Service type | Reasonable range |
|---|---|
| Hourly virtual bookkeeping | $25–50/hour |
| Monthly retainer (single small biz, standard volume) | $300–800/mo |
| Cleanup project (12 months messy books) | $1,500–4,500 flat |
| QBO setup (one-time) | $300–800 |
| Sales tax filing | $50–100 per state per filing |

**Your first client will probably underpay.** Plan for it. Charge them as agreed, deliver excellent work, and raise rates for client 2. Don't shoot for top-of-range on day one.

**Self-check questions:**

1. Do my prices match my actual experience level?
   - If you have prior bookkeeping/accounting experience: middle of range
   - If you're brand-new + QBO certified: low-mid of range
   - If you're brand-new + not yet certified: low end, and be honest with prospects about it

2. Do I have a written reason for each price?
   - "I'm charging $400/mo for monthly close because I expect ~6 hours/month at $65/hour effective."
   - "I'm charging $2,500 for cleanup because I expect 35 hours of work at ~$70/hour."

If you can't justify a price, your client will negotiate you down.

## Part 4 — The No-Fly Zone

Write down everything you will NOT do. This is just as important as what you WILL do.

**Standard no-fly list (recommended):**

- ❌ I will not prepare or file income tax returns (Form 1040, 1065, 1120, etc.)
- ❌ I will not provide tax advice or tax planning
- ❌ I will not represent clients before the IRS or state tax authorities
- ❌ I will not do payroll for businesses with >5 employees or >1 state (refer to Gusto / dedicated payroll service)
- ❌ I will not work with clients whose books are >24 months behind without a paid cleanup engagement first
- ❌ I will not give business advice / strategy / valuation work (refer to CPA/CFO)

- Investment / financial planning advice → refer to a CFP

**How to engage:** All work begins with a signed engagement letter and first month's payment.
