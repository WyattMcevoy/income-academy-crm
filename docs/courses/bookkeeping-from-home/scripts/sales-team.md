# Bookkeeping From Home — Sales Team Script

**Internal doc for the phone team** when a Bookkeeping course member reaches out or when discussing the course with a prospective buyer.

---

## Who you'll be talking to

### Type 1: Prospect considering the Foundation Pass, asking about bookkeeping specifically

Most common scenario. They saw the bundle, the bookkeeping course caught their eye, and they want to understand if this path fits them.

### Type 2: Current Foundation Pass member working through Bookkeeping

Need help applying the course to their specific situation. Answer honestly using the course material. No upsell push.

### Type 3: Inbound from someone who already runs a bookkeeping business

Rarer. They're looking for a better system / community / AI tools. Honest answer: "Our course is for people building to 3-5 clients, not scaling to an agency. You may outgrow it fast." Refer to Bookkeeper Launch if appropriate.

---

## Key qualifying questions for Type 1 prospects

Ask in a warm conversational way, not interrogation:

1. "What's your background — have you worked in accounting, finance, admin before, or is this brand new?"
2. "What kind of pace are you looking for — a relaxed 3-5 clients and 15 hours a week, or bigger?"
3. "Do you have some comfort with numbers and spreadsheets, or does that sound stressful?"
4. "What made you specifically curious about bookkeeping vs. the other courses in the bundle?"

### Signals: great fit
- Past career in finance, accounting, administrative work, office management
- Already comfortable with QuickBooks or Xero from a job or family business
- Wants small-scale practice, not full-time career replacement
- Patient, detail-oriented personality
- Comfortable with regular monthly responsibility

### Signals: wrong fit
- Hates spreadsheets, finds numbers stressful
- Wants passive income (bookkeeping is active service work)
- Needs income THIS month (30-60 days is realistic for first client; client acquisition takes time)
- Already has a full-time job and can't commit to 2 focused weeks per month of bookkeeping work
- Looking to do tax preparation (this course doesn't teach that)

## Common questions from prospects

### "I don't have a bookkeeping background. Is this for me?"

> "It depends on where you're starting. If you've done any of these — managed books at a past job, volunteered as treasurer for a group, done your own family/business finances carefully, worked in admin — you have enough to start. If the word 'reconciliation' doesn't mean anything to you, take Intuit's free bookkeeping basics course first (one weekend), then come to ours to learn how to turn that skill into a business."

### "Do I need to be a CPA?"

> "No. Bookkeeping doesn't require licensure in any of the 50 states (as of 2026). CPA is a separate, much longer, much more regulated profession. Module 1 of our course covers exactly where the line is between bookkeeping (what you'll do) and accounting/tax prep (what requires credentials). Don't cross the line."

### "How much can I realistically earn?"

> "Honest answer: 3 clients at $400/month = $1,200/month recurring. 5 clients at $500/month = $2,500/month. Those are typical ranges based on industry pay data for virtual bookkeepers at $25-$50/hour. Some members earn less (only want 2 clients). A few earn more (take 6-8 clients). We don't guarantee any specific outcome."

### "How long until my first client?"

> "Realistic target is 30 days if you follow Module 4's outreach plan — 10 CPA outreaches plus 10 local network contacts per week. Some members land their first client in week 2. Some take 60-90 days. Hands-off marketing won't work — bookkeeping clients come from relationships, especially CPA referrals."

### "Why should I buy your $47 course instead of Bookkeeper Launch at $3,000?"

> "Fair question. Bookkeeper Launch is a great course — it teaches you to build a full bookkeeping career, hire staff, scale to an agency. That costs $3,000 because it's that comprehensive. Our course is different: it teaches you to build a 3-5 client practice for retirement income, not a career change. Different goal, different scope, different price. If you want to scale to 30+ clients, buy Bookkeeper Launch. If you want 3-5 clients and 2 focused weeks of work per month for $1,500-$2,500/mo in supplemental income, we're the right fit at $47 + $19/mo."

### "Can I do this from any state?"

> "As of 2026, no US state requires a bookkeeping license. Some states (LA, NV, WV) have had narrow discussions about it historically — Module 1 of the course covers how to verify your specific state. In 95% of cases, you can start bookkeeping services from your home in any US state without a license."

### "What if I realize I hate it?"

> "7-day money-back guarantee on the $47, and you can cancel the $19/mo membership in one click from your account page. Total cost if you try it for a week and bail: $0. We make it easy to leave because we don't want reluctant members."

## Mentioning other courses in the bundle

The prospect is asking about bookkeeping, but might be a better fit elsewhere. Mention alternatives WHEN fit is unclear:

- **"I'm not great with numbers but I like writing"** → Suggest AI Side Income or Affiliate Marketing courses
- **"I like hands-on physical work more than desk work"** → Suggest Estate Sale Sourcing
- **"I want multiple small businesses over time"** → Bookkeeping is a fit, but also mention affiliate marketing as a long-term complement

### Framing

> "The Foundation Pass gives you all 4 courses for the same $47 + $19/mo. You can try bookkeeping, and if it doesn't click, you've still paid for 3 other paths. That's the whole point of the bundle — reduce the risk of picking wrong."

---

## When to NOT push toward enrollment

- Prospect is clearly in financial distress ("I need income THIS week")
  → Honest: "This isn't fast income. First client in 30 days is realistic only if you hustle, and first $1,200/mo takes longer. If you need income this week, bookkeeping is the wrong answer. Try [temp agency / door-dash / immediate-paying gig work]."

- Prospect hates numbers / spreadsheets
  → Honest: "Bookkeeping is 80% spreadsheets and 20% conversation. If that sounds painful, one of our other courses is probably a better fit."

- Prospect wants passive income
  → Honest: "Bookkeeping is active service work — client comes to you each month, you do the reconciliation, you deliver reports. Not passive. If passive is your goal, affiliate marketing in the bundle is closer (not truly passive either, but more compound-asset).

## Escalation

- Specific tax questions from a member: "Happy to point you to our Module 1 which covers the line, but your specific situation needs a CPA. Here's why."
- Technical QBO / Xero issue: route to support@incomeacademy.biz
- Refund request within 7 days: honor immediately, one-sentence "thanks for giving it a try"
- Wants to speak to the founder: escalate to [YOUR NAME] with a 2-line summary

---

## What to never say

- "You'll definitely have 5 clients in 90 days" (FTC territory)
- "Everyone in our course succeeds" (not true; some quit, some don't do the work)
- "This replaces your career" (it's supplemental for most)
- "Bookkeeping is recession-proof" (no business is fully recession-proof)

Stay honest. This audience has been burned by pitches. Honesty is the competitive advantage.
