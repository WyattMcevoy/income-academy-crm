# Bookkeeping From Home — Wins Ladder

Bookkeeping has a shorter ramp than affiliate marketing (first client can land in 30–60 days for diligent students), but the in-between phase — when you're set up but haven't landed a client — is where most members cancel.

This ladder names every step so you can see progress before the first invoice gets paid.

Members who can name their current milestone retain at higher rates because they measure success by **what they did**, not just **what they got paid**.

---

## How to use this

- Each Foundation Pass member starts at **Stage 1 — Foundation Wins** in month 1.
- At the end of each module, mark off the named milestone.
- The portal displays your current stage ("Month 2: Client-Ready Bookkeeper").
- Email automations celebrate each stage advancement.

---

## Stage 1 — Foundation Wins (Week 1–2)

**Identity:** "I'm a Bookkeeping Apprentice."

**Milestones to claim:**

- [ ] **You know the line between bookkeeping, accounting, and tax prep** — and you can explain it to a 10-year-old (Module 1)
- [ ] **You picked your software stack** — QBO, Xero, or FreshBooks. One. Not "I'll decide later." (Module 2)
- [ ] **You have a free trial or learner account in your chosen software**
- [ ] **You completed at least one tutorial reconciliation** in your software
- [ ] **You read the FTC + state licensure section of Module 1** and know what you legally cannot do

**Why this stage matters:** Most people who say "I want to be a bookkeeper" never pick a software. You did. That alone puts you ahead of the wishful-thinking crowd.

**Email celebration trigger:** Module 2 complete + member confirms software chosen via portal form.

---

## Stage 2 — Service Definition Wins (Week 3–4)

**Identity:** "I'm a Client-Ready Bookkeeper."

**Milestones to claim:**

- [ ] **Your service menu is drafted.** Specific services, specific deliverables, specific cadence. (See [services-menu-worksheet.md](templates/services-menu-worksheet.md))
- [ ] **You priced each service.** Even if you change later — you have numbers on paper.
- [ ] **You wrote your "no-fly zone"** — what you will NOT do (no tax prep, no payroll above X complexity, no rescuing 5 years of unreconciled books from a new client)
- [ ] **You drafted your engagement letter template** based on the course template (with the disclaimers in place)
- [ ] **You have a way to invoice** (QBO invoicing, Wave, or whatever — you tested sending yourself a $1 test invoice)

**Why this stage matters:** Most "aspiring bookkeepers" cannot answer "what do you charge?" with a straight number. You can. That's already more professional than 80% of the field.

**Email celebration trigger:** Member uploads/confirms completion of services menu worksheet.

---

## Stage 3 — Outreach Wins (Week 5–8)

**Identity:** "I'm a Bookkeeping Networker."

**Milestones to claim:**

- [ ] **First 10 outreach contacts identified** (Module 4 — list of local small businesses, CPAs, accountants who might refer)
- [ ] **First outreach email sent** (personalized, NOT a mail-merge blast)
- [ ] **First reply received** (any reply — "no thanks" still counts, you're now a real outreach operator)
- [ ] **First discovery call booked**
- [ ] **One referral relationship established** (CPA, accountant, business advisor — someone who would send you a name)

**Why this stage matters:** Outreach is where most aspiring bookkeepers freeze. If you sent the 10 emails and held the discovery call, you have proven you can do the part of this business most people can't.

**Email celebration trigger:** Member marks "first discovery call held" in portal.

---

## Stage 4 — First Client Wins (Week 8–12)

**Identity:** "I'm a Bookkeeper with a Client."

**Milestones to claim:**

- [ ] **First engagement letter signed** (paid retainer client OR paid one-time cleanup)
- [ ] **First invoice paid**
- [ ] **First month-end close completed** for that client
- [ ] **You documented your process** so the second client onboarding is faster
- [ ] **You decided whether you want to keep them** (good client? Bad fit? Both happen.)

**Why this stage matters:** You crossed from "learning bookkeeping" to "running a bookkeeping practice." The next stage isn't a different course — it's repetition + reputation.

**Email celebration trigger:** Member self-reports "first paid client engagement."

---

## Stage 5 — Stable Practice Wins (Month 4–6)

**Identity:** "I'm a Working Bookkeeper."

**Milestones to claim:**

- [ ] **2–3 active retainer clients** (or equivalent in one-time work)
- [ ] **Your monthly closing process is documented** and repeatable (not improvised each month)
- [ ] **You raised one client's rate** (or set higher rates for a new client than your first)
- [ ] **You said NO to at least one bad-fit client** (this is the milestone, not a failure — it means you have standards)
- [ ] **You can predict next month's income** within 20%

**Why this stage matters:** Most bookkeeping practices die at the first bad client because the operator doesn't have systems to handle them. You have systems. You have standards. You have a real practice.

**Email celebration trigger:** Member reports 2+ active clients for 60 consecutive days.

---

## Stage 6 — Sustainable Practice (Month 6+)

**Identity:** "I'm running a bookkeeping practice."

**Milestones (orientation only — individual results vary):**

- [ ] You can predictably bring on a new client when you have capacity
- [ ] You fired your first wrong-fit client (or transitioned them out cleanly)
- [ ] You upgraded your software / tools based on what your practice actually needs
- [ ] You started referring out work that's outside your scope (tax prep, advisory)

**No income claims attached.** Bookkeeping income at month 12 depends on hours worked, client mix, geography, niche, pricing power, and dozens of factors outside the course. Some members run 3-client practices for steady part-time income. Some build full-time replacements. Both are valid outcomes — neither is promised.

---

## What to tell members who feel stuck

Three honest truths:

1. **Stage 3 (outreach) is where 60% of would-be bookkeepers stop.** If you're stuck here, you're at the hardest gate — and getting through it is the difference between "people who took a course" and "people who built a business."
2. **The first client is the hardest. The fifth is exponentially easier.** Reputation compounds — but only if you stay in the game past client 1.
3. **You will undercharge your first client.** Everyone does. Don't beat yourself up. Charge them as agreed, deliver excellent work, raise rates on client 2.

## Implementation notes for the team

- Map each milestone to a GHL workflow trigger
- "Stage advancement" emails should be 3 lines: celebrate, name what they did, point at the next milestone
- Stage 3 (outreach) has the highest cancel risk — schedule a personal check-in email at Day 30 for bookkeeping-track members specifically
- Coaching call topics anchor on stage, not on income
- No income representations in any celebration emails — celebrate the action
