## FAQ

**Do I need a bookkeeping background?**
Light exposure helps. If you've managed a small business's books, volunteered as a treasurer, done bookkeeping in a past job, or even managed your own family finances rigorously — you have enough. If you've never seen QuickBooks or Xero, take Intuit Academy's free course first (one weekend), then come back to this.

**Do I need to become a CPA?**
No. Bookkeeping doesn't require licensure in any of the 50 states (as of 2026 — course covers how to verify for your state). CPA is a separate, much longer, much more regulated profession.

**Can I do tax returns for my bookkeeping clients?**
Not for pay without a PTIN + EA or CPA credential. Module 1 covers this in detail. For tax prep, refer your clients to a CPA — it's actually a great way to build referral relationships the other direction.

**How much can I realistically earn?**
3 clients at $400/mo = $1,200/mo recurring. 5 clients at $500/mo = $2,500/mo. These are typical ranges based on industry pay data. Some members earn less (they only want 2 clients). A small percentage earn more (they take 6-8 clients). We don't guarantee income.

**How long until I have my first client?**
Realistic target is 30 days if you follow Module 4's outreach plan. Some members land their first client in week 2. Some take 60-90 days. If you aren't willing to do 10 CPA/network outreaches per week, it'll take longer.

**What software do I need to buy?**
QBO ProAdvisor is free. Xero Advisor cert is free. Your clients pay for their own QBO/Xero subscriptions — not you. Only real cost: optional E&O insurance ($300-$600/year once you hit 3+ clients).

**What if the client's books are a disaster?**
That's a "QBO cleanup project" — covered in Module 3. These are one-time engagements, $500-$2,000 flat, and they're often your BEST entry point because the client is motivated and already knows they need help.

**What about state licensing?**
As of 2026, no US state requires a bookkeeping license for general bookkeeping services. Module 1 covers how to verify for your specific state and the narrow areas (LA, NV, WV) that have had licensing discussions historically.

**What if I decide I want to scale to an agency later?**
Great — you'll have a working practice + client base + systems to build from. At that point, Bookkeeper Launch or similar programs become the right next investment. Most of our members don't go this route — but the door's open.
