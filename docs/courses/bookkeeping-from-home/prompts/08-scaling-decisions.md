# Prompts — Scaling Decisions

Growing (or deliberately NOT growing) your bookkeeping practice.

## BK-70: Should I take on a 4th / 5th / 6th client?

```
I currently have [N] clients, generating $[REVENUE]/month total at
roughly [HOURS]/week of work. A new prospect wants to engage me for
$[PROPOSED RATE]/month. Their engagement would be [EST. HOURS]/week
of work.

Decision help:
1. Am I at capacity already, or do I have room?
2. Is this prospect at my target rate-per-hour, below it, or above?
3. What's the opportunity cost — what would I do with that time if
   not this client (rest, marketing for better clients, other paid
   work)?
4. Is this the right "profile" of client for me? [e.g., if I
   specialize in contractors and this is a retail e-commerce client,
   stepping outside niche has costs]
5. Can I raise my rate on existing clients if I'm at capacity?

Give me a take it / decline it / counter-offer-at-higher-rate
recommendation.
```

## BK-71: Fire a bad client

```
I have a client who [DESCRIBE THE PROBLEM — e.g., "is constantly
late with data", "asks questions outside scope daily", "pays invoices
30+ days late", "is rude in communication", "has become unprofitable
after we expanded scope without re-pricing"].

The relationship isn't working. I've tried [WHAT I'VE TRIED — if
anything]. I want to end the engagement cleanly.

Write me:
1. An email informing them I'm terminating the engagement effective
   [DATE — typically 30-60 days out to give them time to find
   someone]
2. Offer to hand off cleanly — export QBO file, refer to another
   bookkeeper, close out the month cleanly
3. Final invoice for work completed through termination date
4. A brief, non-accusatory reason (I don't need to vent in the email
   — a short "our scope has outgrown what I can deliver" is fine)

Tone: professional, final. This is a business decision, not an
argument. Don't invite debate.
```

## BK-72: Should I scale to an agency?

```
I have [N] clients. My spouse / life situation wants me to either
stabilize at my current level (part-time retirement income) OR grow
to a real agency.

Current state:
- Revenue: $[X]/month
- Profit: $[Y]/month (after software, insurance, etc.)
- Hours: [Z]/week
- Satisfaction: [HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW]

Help me think through both paths:

Path A (stabilize at 3-5 clients):
- What changes to optimize this level?
- How to raise rates vs. taking new clients
- What systems/processes to formalize so I don't feel stressed
- The "income ceiling" at this level in my market

Path B (grow to agency):
- When to hire first employee / contractor
- What roles to hire first (junior bookkeeper? admin? sales?)
- How to transition from "doer" to "operator"
- What income ceiling is realistic (10-20 clients, multi-bookkeeper
  team)
- Risks I'm signing up for (management, overhead, HR)

Neither is right. I want to make an honest choice. Which is more
aligned with what I actually want?
```

## BK-73: Raise prices across the board

```
I've been at my current pricing for [DURATION] and market rates
have moved. I want to raise prices on ALL existing clients (not
just new ones).

Current state:
- [N] clients at various rates
- Average: $[X]/month
- Last raised prices: [DATE or NEVER]

Help me:
1. Decide the new rate structure (flat % increase? Tiered by client
   complexity? Pricing-to-value?)
2. Decide the rollout (all clients same date? Staggered based on
   contract anniversary?)
3. Draft the announcement email template (I'll customize per client)
4. Prepare for responses:
   - Clients who accept silently (most)
   - Clients who push back (script for each)
   - Clients who leave (acceptable — they're below target rate
     anyway)
5. Financial projection: if [X%] leave, am I still net positive?

Also: this is a good time to consider changing engagement structure
(e.g., moving from hourly to flat monthly if I haven't already).
```

## BK-74: Exit plan — selling or closing the practice

```
I'm considering [SELLING / CLOSING / TRANSITIONING] my bookkeeping
practice in [TIMEFRAME — 1 year / 3 years / 5 years].

Current practice:
- [N] clients
- $[X]/year revenue
- [OWNERSHIP structure — sole prop, LLC, S-corp]

Help me think through:
1. If selling: what's my practice worth? Typical bookkeeping practice
   sells for [X times annual revenue]. How do I maximize value
   (client stickiness, documented processes, clean books ironically)?
2. If closing: how to close cleanly — notify clients, refer to
   trusted peers, settle final invoices, deactivate subscriptions,
   tax implications of closing
3. If transitioning (e.g., to a junior bookkeeper who takes over):
   how to structure the handoff legally + emotionally

Timeline for each scenario: what to start doing 1 year before,
6 months before, 3 months before.

Tax / legal specifics: flag what I need to discuss with a CPA /
attorney — you can give general frameworks but not specific advice.
```
