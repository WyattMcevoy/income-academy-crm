## Days 31-60: Onboarding + Refinement

### Onboarding the first client

When your first prospect signs:

**Week 1:** Send engagement letter. Get signed. Set up QB access.

**Week 2:** Run a quick books review. Identify catch-up needs (if any).

**Week 3:** First month's bookkeeping. Run reports. Send first summary email.

**Week 4:** Client feedback. Adjust process.

### Continuing outreach

DON'T stop outreach because you have 1 client. **You need 3 to be sustainable.**

Continue:
- 5 emails per week
- Monthly Chamber/BNI meetings
- Weekly LinkedIn activity (post once, comment 3-5 times)

### End of Day 60

- 1 to 2 active clients
- $500 to $1,500/month income
- Pipeline of 5 to 10 prospects in conversation

## When things go sideways

### "Day 30 and no clients yet"

Diagnosis questions:

- Have you sent at least 25 outreach emails? If less, send more.
- Have you completed your ProAdvisor profile (with photo + 3 reviews)?
- Are you having actual prospect calls? If not, your emails aren't compelling.

90% of "no clients" is solved with more outreach.

### "Day 60 and stuck at 1 client"

Two things to check:

1. Did your first client become demanding/scope-creepy? Revisit engagement letter.
2. Is your pricing working? Are you saying yes to bad-fit clients?

### "Day 90 and not at $2,000/month yet"

Honest evaluation:

- Did you do 90 days of consistent work, or did you have weeks off?
- Is your niche too narrow?
- Are your prices too low?

The course works when you work it. **3 clients in 90 days is achievable.** If you're not there, it's usually about input, not skill.

## Common pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Crossing the legal line

Most common: a client asks "is this deductible?" and you answer.

**Always: "That's a CPA question."** Refer.

### Pitfall 2: Underpricing

Beginners price at $200/month for full bookkeeping. **You'll burn out.**

Stick to your floor: $400+/month for monthly retainer with reasonable volume.

### Pitfall 3: Skipping engagement letters

You'll be tempted to "just start" with a friend or family member.

**Don't.** This is how good relationships go bad.

### Pitfall 4: Working 24/7

Set your communication policy (Module 7) and STICK to it.

### Pitfall 5: Trying to do tax prep

Stay in your lane. **You're a bookkeeper.**
