# Module 4: Landing Your First 3 Clients

**Time:** about 60 minutes
**What you'll do:** Learn the 4 places bookkeeping clients come from, and send your first 5 outreach messages.

---

## Welcome back

Bookkeeping clients come from different places than other freelance services. Cold-pitching small business owners doesn't work as well for bookkeeping (people don't realize they need it until they're behind).

Instead, bookkeeping clients come through:

1. CPA referrals (the goldmine)
2. ProAdvisor / Find-an-Accountant directory
3. Local small business communities
4. Existing professional networks

By the end of this module, you'll have sent at least 5 outreach messages.

---

## Source 1: CPA Referrals (the goldmine)

### Why it works

CPAs constantly meet clients with messy books. They WANT a bookkeeper to refer to - because clean books make their tax-prep job easier.

**One CPA relationship can send you 5 to 15 clients per year.**

### How to build it

**Step 1: Find 5 to 10 local CPAs**

Google "[your town] small business CPA." Note their names, firm size, and any specialty.

**Step 2: Email each one**

Use this template:

```
Subject: Quick question, [first name]

Hi [first name],

I'm a QuickBooks-certified bookkeeper who specializes in [your
niche, e.g., "small service businesses with under 10 employees"]
in the [your area] area.

I'm reaching out because I imagine you sometimes get clients whose
books are a mess, and you'd love a reliable bookkeeper to refer
them to.

I'd love to grab a 15-minute virtual coffee to introduce myself.
If we're a fit, I can be a resource for your messy clients - and
if I run into tax questions I always punt them back to a CPA, so
I'd love to know who you are.

Worst case, you walk away knowing one more bookkeeper. Best case,
we both pick up some referrals over time.

Either way, hope your week's going well.

[Your name]
[Your phone]
[Your QB ProAdvisor profile link]
```

### What to expect

Out of 10 CPA emails:

- 5 will reply
- 3 will take the meeting
- 1 to 2 will become a steady referral source within 12 months

That 1 referral source might send you 5 to 15 clients in a year.

### How to maintain the relationship

After each meeting, send a follow-up email. Then:

- Send them an email every 90 days with a brief update on your practice
- Always promptly refer their clients back to them when tax questions come up
- Send a thank-you note if they refer you a client (handwritten works)

This is a long-game relationship. **Treat CPAs as senior partners, not as customers.**

## Source 2: ProAdvisor / Find-an-Accountant Directories

### Why it works

When small business owners search "QuickBooks bookkeeper near me," they often end up in Intuit's Find-A-ProAdvisor directory (or Xero's equivalent).

**Get listed.**

### How

Once you complete your QuickBooks ProAdvisor certification:

1. Log into your ProAdvisor account
2. Click "Profile" or "Find-A-ProAdvisor settings"
3. Fill out your profile completely:
   - Photo (real, smiling)
   - Bio (2 to 3 sentences about your specialty)
   - Services offered (the 2 to 3 you picked in Module 3)
   - Industries served (be specific)
   - Service area (your city + 50 miles)
   - Pricing range (don't leave blank)

4. Get your first 3 reviews

The first 3 reviews are critical. Ask family members or beta clients to leave honest reviews.

### What to expect

Once your profile is up and you have 3+ reviews:

- 1 to 5 leads per month (free, from Intuit)
- Conversion rate to client: 20-30%

This means: **you might get 1 paying client per month from this alone.** Free.

## Source 3: Local Small Business Communities

### Where small business owners hang out

- **Local Chamber of Commerce.** Many have monthly mixers.
- **BNI groups** (Business Networking International). Specifically built for referrals.
- **Local Facebook groups** for business owners.
- **Small business meetups** on meetup.com.

### How to engage

**Step 1: Pick 1 to 2 to actually attend**

Don't try to do all of them. Pick the ones with active, engaged members.

**Step 2: Show up monthly for 3 months before pitching**

Get to know people. Ask about their businesses. **Don't pitch yourself in your first conversation.**

**Step 3: Mention what you do, when asked**

After 2-3 meetings, when someone asks "what do you do?" - tell them in one sentence (your description from Module 3).

**Step 4: Follow up individually**

When you meet someone whose business sounds like it could need bookkeeping help, follow up:

```
Hey [name], great to meet you at [event]. You mentioned you've been
struggling to keep up with the books on your business. I help with
exactly that. Want to grab a coffee and see if I can help?
```

### What to expect

3 to 6 months of consistent attendance = 1 to 2 clients per month.

Slower than CPA referrals. But you build community along the way.

## Source 4: Existing Professional Networks

### Who's in your network

- Former coworkers who now run businesses
- Family members who own businesses
- Friends with side businesses
- Local realtors, attorneys, contractors you know personally

### The "I'm trying something new" email

Send to 10 to 20 people in your network this week:

```
Subject: Update: I'm starting a bookkeeping practice

Hi [name],

Hope you're doing well!

Wanted to share something I'm starting up. I'm now a QuickBooks-
certified bookkeeper, and I'm building a small practice helping
local small businesses keep their books clean.

I'm not asking you to hire me. I just thought you might know someone
who's been frustrated with their bookkeeping - or maybe a small
business owner who's overwhelmed and doesn't have a bookkeeper yet.

If anyone comes to mind, I'd appreciate the introduction. I'm trying
to fill my first 3 client spots in the next 60 days.

Either way, would love to catch up. Coffee soon?

[Your name]
```

### What to expect

Out of 20 emails: 2 to 5 referrals, 1 to 2 paying clients in first 60 days.

Plus your network now knows what you do. **They'll keep referring for years.**

## The first conversation with a prospect

When a prospect (referral or otherwise) wants to talk:

### Set up a 30-minute call

Don't try to land them in 5 minutes. Bookkeeping requires understanding their business.

### The script

**Open:** "Tell me about your business. How long have you been operating? How big is the team?"

(Listen 5 minutes.)

**Diagnose:** "What's the current bookkeeping situation? Are you doing it yourself? Do you have someone helping? What's working and what isn't?"

(Listen 5 to 10 minutes.)

**Identify the pain:** "What's the worst part of the current setup?"

(Listen 2 to 3 minutes.)

**Quote (broadly):** "Based on what you've described, I'd quote somewhere in the [low end] to [high end] range per month. I can give you a specific quote after I see the books."

**Close:** "Would you be open to giving me view-only access to your QuickBooks for 30 minutes so I can quote you specifically? I can do that as soon as today."

### Pricing on the call

Don't quote a specific number until you've seen the books. Volume matters more than business size.

Use ranges: "$300 to $600/month for monthly bookkeeping" - then narrow after seeing transaction volume.

### What to do after the call

Within 24 hours, send a written proposal (Module 5 covers this).

## If you got stuck

### "I'm too nervous to email CPAs"

CPAs love bookkeepers. **They WANT to hear from you.**

The worst they can do is not reply. Send the email. Move on. Email another one.

### "My ProAdvisor profile isn't getting any leads"

Two possibilities:

1. Profile incomplete (no photo, vague bio, no reviews)
2. Service area too small (expand to 50+ miles)

Get your first 3 reviews from anyone you know. The directory rewards reviewed profiles.

### "I had a prospect call and didn't know what to charge"

That's normal. Use the range: "$300 to $600 per month - I'll give you a specific quote after I see the books."

Then look at the books. Figure out the volume. Quote on the higher end if there's complexity, lower if it's simple.
