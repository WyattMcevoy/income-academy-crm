"Bookkeeping" sounds like one service. It's actually 5 different services that small businesses buy.

## Service 1: Monthly bookkeeping

### What it is

The flagship service. You handle ALL the bookkeeping for the client every month:

- Categorize all transactions
- Reconcile bank and credit card accounts
- Generate monthly P&L and Balance Sheet
- Send a monthly summary to the client
- Flag anything unusual

### What it's worth

- **Monthly retainer:** $300 to $1,200/month per client (varies by transaction volume)
- Average: $500/month for a small business with 100-300 transactions/month

### Time per client

- 4 to 8 hours per month per client
- Plus 30 minutes of communication

### Why it's the foundation service

This is what 70% of your clients will buy. Everything else builds on this.

---

## Service 2: Catch-up bookkeeping

### What it is

A client comes to you and says "I haven't done my books in 8 months." You catch them up.

- Categorize 8+ months of backlogged transactions
- Reconcile back to start of fiscal year
- Generate clean reports for tax time
- Hand off to monthly bookkeeping (or release to client)

### What it's worth

- **Project-based:** $500 to $5,000 depending on backlog size
- Average: $1,500 to $2,000 for 6 months of catch-up

### Time per project

- 10 to 40 hours total
- Done over 1 to 3 weeks

### Why it's powerful

- One-time projects pay well
- Often converts to monthly bookkeeping after
- Most common entry point for new clients (they only call when they're behind!)

---

## Service 3: Accounts receivable / payable management

### What it is

You handle the "who owes who" side of the business:

- Send invoices to client's customers
- Follow up on unpaid invoices
- Process bills your client receives
- Track 30/60/90-day aging

### What it's worth

- **Hourly:** $25 to $40/hour
- **Monthly retainer:** $200 to $600/month

### Time per client

- 5 to 15 hours per month, depending on transaction volume

### Who buys this

Service businesses with frequent invoicing. Contractors, consultants, agencies.

---

## Service 4: Payroll processing

### What it is

You run payroll for businesses with 1 to 20 employees:

- Process payroll (often via Gusto, ADP, or QuickBooks Payroll)
- Track time/hours
- Manage benefits deductions
- File required quarterly payroll taxes

### What it's worth

- **Per pay period:** $50 to $200 per pay run
- **Monthly retainer:** $200 to $800/month
- Often added to monthly bookkeeping for $200/month extra

### Important note

Payroll has legal complexity. **Get the payroll certification** before offering this (QB Payroll Certification, Gusto Partner Program, etc.).

If you mess up payroll, the IRS comes after the EMPLOYER and the EMPLOYER comes after YOU. **Take this seriously.**

### Time per client

- 1 to 3 hours per pay period

---

## Service 5: 1099 / W-9 management

### What it is

You manage contractor relationships at year-end:

- Collect W-9s from contractors throughout the year
- Generate 1099s in January
- Send 1099s to contractors
- File 1099s with the IRS

### What it's worth

- **Per 1099:** $5 to $25
- **Annual project:** $200 to $2,000 depending on volume

### Time per project

- 5 to 30 hours total in December/January

### Who buys this

Any business with contractors. Common for marketing agencies, construction, consulting.

## How to pick your 2-3 services

### Decision framework

Ask yourself:

**1. Which services match my software certification?**

- QuickBooks ProAdvisor: Services 1, 2, 3, 4
- Xero certification: Services 1, 2, 3
- FreshBooks: Services 1, 3 (limited payroll)

**2. Which services do I find tolerable?**

- Hate phone calls? Skip Service 3 (AR/AP requires lots of follow-up calls)
- Hate detail/legal complexity? Skip Service 4 (payroll has legal stakes)
- Comfortable with basics only? Stick to Services 1 and 2

**3. Which fit my time constraints?**

- 10 hours/week max? Services 1 + 5 (cyclical)
- 20 hours/week available? Services 1 + 2 + 4

### Recommended starter combinations

**Beginner package (most students):**
- Monthly bookkeeping + catch-up bookkeeping (Services 1 + 2)
- Total potential income: $2,000 to $5,000/month for 4-6 clients

**Service business package:**
- Monthly bookkeeping + AR/AP + 1099s (Services 1 + 3 + 5)
- Total potential income: $3,000 to $6,000/month for 4-5 clients

**Full-service package (more advanced):**
- Monthly + payroll (Services 1 + 4)
- Total potential income: $5,000 to $10,000/month for 4-6 clients
- Requires payroll certification first

## How to describe yourself

Once you've picked your services, you need a one-sentence description.

### Bad descriptions (don't use)

- "I do bookkeeping" (too vague)
- "I'm an accountant" (you're not - if you're a CPA, fine; if not, illegal)
- "I help small businesses with their finances" (sounds advisory - dangerous)

### Good descriptions

- "I provide monthly bookkeeping for small service businesses using QuickBooks Online."
- "I specialize in catch-up bookkeeping for small businesses who've fallen behind."
- "I handle bookkeeping and payroll for small businesses with 1 to 10 employees."

Specific. Bounded. Legal.

## If you got stuck

### "I want to do all 5"

Don't, at least not yet. **Master 2 to 3 first.** You can add more in year 2.

Trying to do everything makes you a jack of all trades and master of none. Specialists earn more.

### "I picked services that need software I'm not certified in"

Get certified first. **Don't sell something you can't deliver.**

### "What if a client wants a service I don't offer?"

Refer them to another bookkeeper. Or learn that service. **Don't fake it.**
