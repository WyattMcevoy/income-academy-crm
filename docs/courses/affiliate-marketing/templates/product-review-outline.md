# Product Review Outline Template

Proven structure for reviews that rank in Google and convert readers into buyers.

---

## Meta information (fill first)

- **Product**: [NAME]
- **Target keyword**: "[PRODUCT] review" or similar
- **Secondary keywords**: [2-3]
- **Target word count**: 1,500-3,000 words
- **My genuine experience**: [HAVE I USED IT? FOR HOW LONG? MY HONEST OPINION?]

If you haven't used the product and can't get access to test it, STOP. Either get hands-on, or write a different article.

---

## Article structure

### 1. Title (above the fold)
**Format**: "[PRODUCT] Review: [BENEFIT/ANGLE] ([YEAR] Update)"

Examples:
- "Ooni Koda 16 Review: Is This Pizza Oven Worth $599? (2026)"
- "Notion Review: Why I Switched From Evernote and Never Looked Back"

### 2. FTC Disclosure (right under title)

```
Disclosure: This post contains affiliate links. If you purchase through one of these links, I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. I personally used this product for [TIMEFRAME] before writing this review. I [received it free from company / bought it myself / received a discount]. Details at the footer.
```

### 3. Opening hook (100-200 words)

Lead with a specific moment from your experience. Not "Looking for the best X? Look no further."

Example:
> "Three months into using the [PRODUCT], I was standing in my kitchen at 6 AM wondering if I'd made a $500 mistake. [SPECIFIC DETAIL]. That moment changed my mind about this product — and here's what you need to know before you buy one."

### 4. TL;DR box (above the first H2)

Scannable summary for readers who don't want to read the whole thing:

```
🎯 **Who this is for**: [SPECIFIC AUDIENCE]
✅ **Best use case**: [SPECIFIC SCENARIO]
❌ **Don't buy if**: [HONEST ANTI-RECOMMENDATION]
💰 **Price**: $[X] (as of [DATE])
⭐ **My rating**: [RATING]/5
🔗 **Where to buy**: [AFFILIATE LINK]
```

### 5. What is [PRODUCT]? (150-300 words)

Factual description. Don't pad. Include:
- What it does
- Who made it
- Key specifications
- Price range
- Where it fits in the market (entry-level? premium?)

### 6. Who should buy this? (200-400 words)

3 specific scenarios where this product shines:

**Scenario 1**: [SPECIFIC USER/USE CASE]
- Why it fits
- Real-world example

**Scenario 2**: [SECOND]
**Scenario 3**: [THIRD]

### 7. My experience with it (300-600 words)

This is the section that separates your review from every other AI-generated review. Include:
- How I came to use it
- First impressions (setup, unboxing, onboarding)
- What surprised me (positive or negative)
- Specific moments that shaped my opinion
- One small detail only an owner would know
- How my use evolved over time

### 8. Pros (200-400 words)

5-7 specific pros. Each anchored in experience:

- **[Pro 1 headline]**: [Specific detail + example from my use]
- **[Pro 2 headline]**: [Specific detail + example from my use]
- (etc.)

Avoid: "high quality," "great value," "easy to use." These are lazy and meaningless.

Include: specific measurements, time savings, specific features that worked for you.

### 9. Cons (150-300 words)

3-5 HONEST cons. Every product has flaws. Naming them:
- Builds trust 10× more than pretending it's perfect
- Helps readers decide if the flaws matter to them
- Reduces refund/return rates

Examples of good cons:
- "Battery life is shorter than advertised — I got 4 hours, not the claimed 6"
- "Customer support was slow to respond to my first question (48 hours)"
- "The app interface feels dated compared to newer competitors"

Examples of weak cons (avoid):
- "Could be cheaper" (every product could)
- "Packaging could be better"
- "Not for everyone"

### 10. Comparison: [PRODUCT] vs alternatives (400-700 words)

Compare to 2-3 real competitors. Include a comparison table.

For each alternative:
- Who prefers it and why
- Where [PRODUCT] wins
- Where alternative wins
- Price comparison

### 11. Is [PRODUCT] worth it? (200-400 words)

The decision section. Be clear:
- For [SPECIFIC USER], yes, because [REASON]
- For [SPECIFIC USER], no, because [REASON]
- The "if only one thing" question: if you care MOST about [X], pick this. If you care MOST about [Y], pick [ALTERNATIVE].

### 12. How I'd use [PRODUCT] to maximize value (200-400 words)

Tips that prove you've actually used it:
- Setup tips I wish I knew
- Settings or features most people miss
- Combinations with other products
- When NOT to use certain features

### 13. FAQ (6-8 questions, 300-500 words)

Address questions real buyers will have. Include:
- At least 2 "searchable" questions that might rank independently
- At least 1 question about warranty, returns, or durability
- At least 1 "is it worth the money" question

### 14. Final verdict + recommendation (150-300 words)

End strong:
- Restate who this is for
- Restate who it isn't for
- My bottom-line rating with reasoning
- Clear call-to-action (check current price, read alternatives, etc.)

### 15. Affiliate link + final disclosure

```
Ready to buy? [CHECK CURRENT PRICE ON AMAZON →]

(Affiliate disclosure: I earn a small commission on purchases through this link. It doesn't change your price. I only recommend products I've used and would recommend to family.)
```

## Writing rules for this review

### Every claim needs evidence
- "It's fast" → "The 500-page document processed in 7 seconds"
- "Long battery" → "Lasted 8.5 hours in my test (manufacturer claims 10)"
- "High quality" → describe specific details you'd notice as a user

### Use contrast to show specificity
- "Unlike the [CHEAPER MODEL], this one [SPECIFIC DIFFERENCE]"
- "My previous [PREVIOUS PRODUCT] couldn't [X]; this one does it in [Y]"

### Include sensory details where relevant
- Smell (for food/kitchen products)
- Sound (for appliances, speakers)
- Feel (for wearables, tools)
- Sight (for anything visual)

### Photos matter
- Include at least 3-5 photos: product in use, size comparison, close-up of a key feature, setup
- These are signals to Google that you've actually used it

### Word count discipline
- Don't pad to hit 3,000 words. Cut what doesn't add value.
- Don't stop at 500 words if there's more to say. Depth wins post-HCU.
- Target 1,500-3,000 words for most reviews; exceptional products can go 4,000+
