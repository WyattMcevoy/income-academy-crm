# "Best X for Y" Roundup Post Template

Structure for roundup posts that rank for commercial intent keywords. The bread-and-butter of affiliate content.

---

## Meta

- **Target keyword**: "Best [PRODUCT TYPE] for [AUDIENCE/USE]"
- **Example**: "Best Pressure Washers for Older Homeowners"
- **Target word count**: 2,000-4,000 words
- **Number of products to feature**: 5-10 (7 is ideal)

---

## Structure

### 1. Title
- Front-load the primary keyword
- Include the year for freshness (e.g., "Best X for Y in 2026")
- Optional: add a specific angle ("without breaking the bank" or "tested for 3 months")

### 2. Disclosure (top of article)
```
Disclosure: This post contains affiliate links. If you buy through these links, I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. I personally tested [N] of these products; the rest are included based on research and reader feedback. Commissions do not influence my rankings.
```

### 3. Opening (150-300 words)

Structure:
- Acknowledge the reader's situation specifically
- Signal you understand their actual need (not just "you want a [PRODUCT]")
- State how you chose the products on this list (transparent criteria)
- Tease the winner (builds reading intent)

Example:
> "Picking a [PRODUCT] as a [AUDIENCE] isn't the same as picking one as a [OTHER AUDIENCE]. You probably care more about [FACTOR A] than you do about [FACTOR B]. I've spent [TIMEFRAME] with [N] of these machines and researched the rest against three criteria that matter specifically to [AUDIENCE]. Here's what actually holds up."

### 4. At-a-glance picks (table or cards)

Quick scan for impatient readers:

```
🏆 Best overall: [PRODUCT A]
💰 Best budget: [PRODUCT B]
🎯 Best for beginners: [PRODUCT C]
🏅 Best for power users: [PRODUCT D]
🌿 Best eco-friendly: [PRODUCT E]
(etc.)
```

Link each to its detailed section below.

### 5. How I chose these products (300-400 words)

Transparency section. Explain:
- Your criteria (specific attributes you prioritized)
- How you tested or researched
- Who you considered but excluded, and why
- Your biases (affiliate relationships, past experience)

This section is what separates a serious roundup from AI fluff.

### 6. Detailed reviews (200-400 words per product × 5-10 products)

For each product:

**[CATEGORY BADGE] — [PRODUCT NAME]**

*Who it's for*: [SPECIFIC AUDIENCE]

**What it is**: 1-2 sentences factual description.

**What's great**: 3 specific strengths anchored in use case. Avoid "great quality."
- Specific point 1
- Specific point 2
- Specific point 3

**The downside**: 1-2 honest cons. Every product has them.

**Specs that matter**: [KEY SPECS — price, weight, dimensions, power, etc.]

**Price**: $[X]

**Best for**: [SPECIFIC SCENARIO]

**Not great for**: [HONEST ALTERNATIVE SCENARIO]

**[AFFILIATE LINK — Check Current Price →]**

---

Repeat for each product. Maintain consistent structure so readers can compare.

### 7. Comparison table (full)

Include a wide table comparing all products on:
- Price
- Top feature
- Best use case
- Rating (1-5)
- Weight/size (if relevant)
- Warranty
- Key differentiator

Tables are scannable + SEO-friendly + great UX.

### 8. Buying guide (500-800 words)

This elevates your roundup above most competitors. Cover:

**What to consider when buying a [PRODUCT]:**
- Priority 1: [FACTOR] — why it matters, what to look for
- Priority 2: [FACTOR] — explained
- Priority 3: [FACTOR] — explained
- Common mistakes first-time buyers make

**Budget considerations**:
- What $X gets you
- What $2X gets you
- What $3X+ gets you
- When to splurge, when to save

**Red flags to avoid**:
- Common marketing tricks
- What "too cheap" usually means
- Warranty and support gotchas

### 9. FAQ (6-8 questions)

- How do I choose between [PRODUCT A] and [PRODUCT B]?
- Is [PRODUCT] worth the extra money?
- How long should a [PRODUCT] last?
- What's the best [PRODUCT] for [SPECIFIC COMMON SCENARIO]?
- Do I need [ACCESSORY]?
- How do I maintain [PRODUCT]?
- Are there any [PRODUCT] I should avoid?

### 10. Final recommendation (200-300 words)

Close with clarity:
- "If you're [SPECIFIC PERSON], I'd get [X] because [REASON]"
- "If you're [ANOTHER PERSON], [Y] is better for you"
- "If budget is your #1 concern, [Z]"

Don't leave readers to figure it out alone. Give them a clear path.

### 11. Article metadata

- Last updated: [DATE]
- Update cadence: every 6-12 months
- Reader feedback email: [YOUR EMAIL]

## Writing principles for roundups

### Rank honestly, not by commission
The biggest ranking factor should be fit for the reader. If [LOW COMMISSION PRODUCT] is genuinely best for your audience, rank it first anyway. Readers detect when rankings are commission-driven, and it destroys trust.

### Include products you DON'T recommend
A dedicated section on "Products to avoid" at the end can boost trust enormously. Name specific competitors that are oversold/underperforming.

### Update annually (minimum)
Roundups age quickly. Prices, model numbers, and best picks change. Plan to revisit each roundup every 6-12 months. Google also rewards freshness in these commercial-intent searches.

### Photos matter — real photos
Especially for physical products, include at least one photo of EACH product in use. Stock images signal low effort. Real photos signal authenticity.

### Balance depth with scan-ability
Readers who want speed should get the top picks in 30 seconds. Readers who want depth should get full analysis. Structure serves both.

---

## Common mistakes

### Mistake 1: Too many products
7 products covered well beats 20 covered shallowly. Don't inflate the list.

### Mistake 2: Same structure per product without variation
Readers get numb. Use variety: sometimes lead with pros, sometimes with "who it's for," sometimes with a quote.

### Mistake 3: Ranking the most expensive as "best overall"
Sometimes the best overall is mid-priced. Trust your judgment over the temptation to push the highest commission.

### Mistake 4: Hedging everything
"This is good for some people, in some ways, depending on your needs." Say it clearly or don't include it.

### Mistake 5: No real buying guide
The buying guide section is where you show expertise. Rushing through it = just another affiliate list.

## Post-publish promotion

- Email list: send to relevant segment within 1 week
- Social: share with the "at a glance" grid as the hook
- Newsletter swap: consider partnering with another niche site
- Update in related existing articles: link to this roundup from relevant posts

---

## Success benchmarks

For a well-targeted, well-written roundup:
- Month 1-3: indexing + early traffic trickle
- Month 3-6: page 2 rankings, 100-500 monthly visits
- Month 6-12: page 1 rankings for some keywords, 500-2,000 monthly visits
- Month 12+: compounding traffic, becoming "evergreen money page" if tended

If after 6 months you're at zero traffic, the keyword was likely too competitive. After 12 months at under 100 visits/month, consider merging or redirecting to a better-performing roundup.
