# Niche Statement Worksheet — Affiliate Marketing, Module 3

**Goal**: by the end of this worksheet you have ONE sentence describing your niche that you can paste into your site footer, social bio, and outreach emails.

**Time required**: 20–30 minutes. Don't rush. This statement gets used everywhere.

---

## Part 1 — The 3-blank formula

Fill in the blanks. First draft is allowed to be bad — you'll refine in Part 2.

> I help **[audience]** **[do specific thing]** with **[your angle / point of view]**.

**Examples (real-pattern, not real people):**
- "I help over-50 first-time hikers pick gear that lasts decades, not seasons."
- "I help new homeschool parents choose curriculum without spending $2K on courses."
- "I help working parents cook healthy weeknight dinners in under 25 minutes."
- "I help Etsy sellers ship cheaper without losing their minds at the post office."

### Your turn

> I help ______________________________
>
> ______________________________ (do what specifically)
>
> with ______________________________ (your angle).

## Part 2 — Stress-test it

For each test below, write your honest answer. If your statement fails 2+ of these tests, go back to Part 1 and rewrite.

### Test 1 — The Specific Person test

> Can you picture ONE specific real person who is your audience? Not a demographic — a person.

Write 3 sentences about that person. Their age, their job, what they were doing 30 minutes before they found your content, what they're afraid of.

If you can't write this — your audience is too broad. Narrow.

### Test 2 — The "Why You" test

> Why should this person trust YOU on this topic?

Write the honest answer in 2 sentences.

If your answer is "I'm just starting to learn it myself" — that's actually fine, but your angle then becomes "I'm doing it in public; come learn alongside me." Don't pretend to be an expert when you're a learner. Lean into the learner angle — it's a real and trustable position.

### Test 3 — The Search Volume test

> Do other people actively look for solutions to this?

- Go to Google. Search the problem in your niche statement (e.g., "best hiking gear for older adults," "homeschool curriculum guide").
- Count: are there 5+ pages of content already? **Good.** That means the market exists.
- Zero competition usually means zero demand. Some competition is healthy.

### Test 4 — The Amazon Inventory test

> Are there products in this niche that pay affiliate commissions?

- Search Amazon for 3 products your audience would buy.
- Check that those products have decent reviews and aren't out of stock.
- If your niche is "rare antique typewriter restoration," Amazon inventory will be thin and Amazon Associates won't be your primary path. That's not a failure — just an early signal you'll need other affiliate networks (Module 5: Beyond Amazon).

### Test 5 — The Income-Honesty test

> Is your niche statement making any income claim or "get rich" promise?

Re-read your statement. If it contains the words: *make money, earn, income, passive, automatic, guaranteed* — rewrite. Your audience comes for solutions to their problems, not promises about your income.

- [ ] Your site footer or About page
- [ ] Your social media bio (Twitter/X, Instagram, LinkedIn — wherever you'll be active)
- [ ] Your email signature
- [ ] The first line of your outreach emails
- [ ] The top of your content strategy doc

---

## What "done" looks like

You should be able to read your niche statement out loud, and a stranger should be able to tell you:
- Who your audience is
- What problem you solve
- Why your angle is different

If they can't, the statement is still too vague. Tighten.
