# Niche Evaluation Worksheet

Before committing 6-12 months to a niche, score it against these criteria. If the total is below 15, keep looking.

---

## How to use

1. Pick 3-5 candidate niches
2. Score each on the 5 criteria below (1-5 scale)
3. Add notes for why you scored each as you did
4. Compare totals
5. Commit to the highest-scoring niche for at least 6 months

## Sanity check questions

Before committing, answer honestly:

1. **Could I write 50+ articles about this without getting bored?**
2. **Would I read this content if someone else published it?**
3. **Do I know at least 3 specific products I'd recommend and why?**
4. **Can I name one sub-angle that nobody else is covering well?**
5. **Is there a real person (or type of person) I'd build this for — specifically?**

If you answered "no" to 2 or more, this niche probably isn't right for you.

## Common traps

### The "huge market" trap
"Fitness has a lot of searches!" → and 10,000 competitors, all stronger. Bigger markets are harder. Pick a narrower slice.

### The "high commission" trap
"Credit cards pay $200 per acquisition!" → true, and Bankrate spends millions on that space. Can't compete.

### The "my hobby" trap
"I love woodworking!" → Good. Now: who are your readers? Other hobbyists (hard to monetize) or beginners looking for tools (yes, monetizable)?

### The "trend" trap
"AI agents are hot!" → In 18 months when your content ranks, is this still relevant? If it's a fad, skip.

### The "friend's niche" trap
"My friend did well in X niche!" → Their journey isn't yours. Their skills, timing, and audience may be different. Score YOUR fit honestly.
