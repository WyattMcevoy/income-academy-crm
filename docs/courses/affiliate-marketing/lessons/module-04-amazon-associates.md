## Welcome back

Amazon Associates is where most affiliate marketers get their first commission.

It's not the highest-paying program (3% to 5% on most categories). But it's the easiest to get approved for, and **everyone trusts Amazon** - so conversion rates are high.

## Setting up Amazon Associates

This is a 20-minute process. Block uninterrupted time.

### Step 1: Go to the signup page

Open your browser and go to:

```
affiliate-program.amazon.com
```

Click the big yellow **"Sign up"** button at the top right.

### Step 2: Sign in with your Amazon account

Use the SAME Amazon account you use for shopping. If you have one. If not, create one - it's free.

### Step 3: Enter your account information

Amazon will ask for:

- **Your name and address.** Use your real legal name and address.
- **Phone number.** Real one. They might call to verify.
- **Tax info.** Most people select "I am a U.S. person" and provide your Social Security Number (Amazon uses this for tax forms - they never share or store it improperly).

This makes some people nervous. **It's a normal IRS requirement for any U.S. business income.** Amazon is one of the safest companies in the world to share this with.

### Step 4: Enter your website information

This is the part that confuses people. Amazon wants to know where you'll publish their links.

If you don't have a website yet (you probably don't), you have options:

**Option A:** Use your existing social media. Type in:
- A Facebook profile URL
- A LinkedIn profile URL
- A YouTube channel URL
- A Pinterest URL

Any one of these is enough to get approved.

**Option B:** If you don't have any of those active either, the simplest path is:
- Go to medium.com
- Sign up (free, takes 2 minutes)
- Write one short article in your niche (300 words is enough)
- Use your Medium profile URL as your website

### Step 5: Pick a Store ID

Amazon will ask for a "preferred Associates Store ID." This is your tracking ID.

**Make it short and related to your niche.** Examples:
- `gardenninja-20`
- `coffeeworld-20`
- `seniortravel-20`

The "-20" is automatic and means U.S. program.

### Step 6: Describe your site

Amazon asks: "What does your site/channel/social media do?"

Write 2 to 3 sentences honestly:

```
I run a [website / YouTube channel / Facebook page] focused on [your
niche]. My audience is mostly [type of audience]. I plan to recommend
[type of products] when they fit naturally into my content.
```

**Don't try to make it sound bigger than it is.** Amazon prefers honest small accounts over inflated ones.

### Step 7: How do you drive traffic?

Pick the options that fit. For most beginners:

- Search engines (SEO) - even if you don't yet, this is your goal
- Social media - if you'll share to Facebook etc.
- Email marketing - if you'll do a newsletter

### Step 8: Submit

Click submit. **You're approved temporarily** - you can use your links right away. Amazon has 180 days to fully verify you. To stay approved, you must make 3 sales within those 180 days.

Don't sweat the 180-day rule. Just publish content and the sales will happen.

## Creating your first 3 affiliate links

Now the fun part.

### Step 1: Go to the SiteStripe

Once approved, every Amazon product page has a "SiteStripe" at the top. It's a thin gray bar with three buttons:

- **Text** (gives you a basic link)
- **Image** (gives you a product image you can embed)
- **Text + Image** (gives you a combined block)

**Use "Text" for now.** Image links are flagged more often.

### Step 2: Pick 3 products in your niche

Open Amazon. Search your niche.

Pick 3 products that are:

- $20 or more (better commission)
- Have 100+ reviews (real buying activity)
- Genuinely good products YOU would recommend

### Step 3: Get the link

On each product page, click "Text" in the SiteStripe. A pop-up shows your affiliate link.

It looks something like:

```
https://amzn.to/3zABCDE
```

That's it. **That's your affiliate link.**

### Step 4: Save the links

Copy each into your Google Doc. Label them:

```
Cookie sheet I love (KitchenAid):
https://amzn.to/3zABCDE

Espresso machine for beginners (Breville):
https://amzn.to/3zXYZ12

Recommended cookbook for new bakers:
https://amzn.to/3zPQR45
```

You'll use these in your first article or video.

## Important: FTC disclosure

This is non-negotiable. **U.S. law requires you to disclose affiliate relationships.**

Every piece of content where you use affiliate links must say something like:

```
"Disclosure: Some links in this post are affiliate links. If you
buy something I recommend, I earn a small commission at no extra
cost to you. This helps support my work, and I only recommend
products I genuinely believe in."
```

Put this **at the top of your article**, BEFORE the first affiliate link.

Module 8 covers FTC compliance in detail. For now: just include the disclosure.

## Your first content: a "best of" article

Let's get one piece of content live before Module 5.

### The format

Title structure: "Best [thing] for [specific audience]"

Examples:
- "Best Cookie Sheets for Holiday Baking"
- "Best Beginner Espresso Machines Under $400"
- "Best Walking Shoes for Bad Knees"

### The structure

```
[FTC disclosure at the top]

[Intro paragraph - 100 words about why this topic matters]

# 1. [Top Pick]

[100 words about why it's the top pick]
[Affiliate link]

# 2. [Runner-up]

[100 words about why this is the runner-up]
[Affiliate link]

# 3. [Budget Pick]

[100 words about why this works on a budget]
[Affiliate link]

[Conclusion - 100 words]
```

Total length: 500 to 700 words. **Write it. Publish it.**

If you don't have a website yet, publish it on Medium for now. You'll move to a real site in Module 6.

## If you got stuck

### "Amazon rejected my application"

The most common reason: your site/channel "doesn't show original content yet." Fix:

- Add 2 to 3 real posts/videos before reapplying
- Reapply after 30 days

### "I don't have any product recommendations yet"

Pick products you've actually used. If you have NO experience in your niche, your niche is wrong. Go back to Module 3.

### "I don't know how to publish on Medium"

- Go to medium.com
- Click "Sign in" then "Get Started"
- Use Google or email to sign up
- Click your profile picture (top right) -> "New Story"
- Type your article
- Click "Publish"

That's it. Free. Takes 5 minutes.

### "Amazon's commission seems really low"

It is. Amazon is for getting started and for products people only buy on Amazon. Module 5 covers higher-commission programs.
