Module 5: Beyond Amazon - Higher-Commission Programs

Time: about 45 minutes What you'll do: Find and apply to 2 to 3 affiliate programs that pay 5x to 50x what Amazon pays.

Welcome back

Amazon is great for getting your first commission. But it's not where the real money is.

Most successful affiliate marketers earn 80% of their income from non-Amazon programs. Many programs pay 20% to 50% commissions, and some pay recurring (monthly).

This module shows you how to find them, apply, and avoid the shady ones.

Why higher commissions matter

Quick math.

A $100 product on Amazon at 4% commission = $4 to you.

The same $100 product on a direct affiliate program at 30% commission = $30 to you.

Same effort. Same reader. 7.5x more income.

If you're going to spend 6 to 12 months building an audience anyway, why not point them at programs that pay you fairly?

The 4 types of higher-commission programs

1. Affiliate networks

Big platforms that host hundreds of programs. You sign up once, then apply to individual merchants within the network.

The major networks for our age group:

  • ShareASale (sharesale.com) - lots of small/mid-size brands, easy approval
  • Impact (impact.com) - bigger brands like Wayfair, Office Depot
  • CJ Affiliate (cj.com) - even bigger brands like Lowe's, Best Buy
  • Awin (awin.com) - mix of brands, growing fast in U.S.

Sign up for at least 1 network. ShareASale is the friendliest for beginners.

2. Direct merchant programs

Some companies run their own programs - not through a network. Examples:

  • Bluehost (web hosting) - $65 per signup
  • ConvertKit (email marketing) - 30% recurring
  • Kit / ConvertKit / Beehiiv (creator tools) - varies, often recurring
  • Skillshare - $7 per signup (low but easy to convert)

To find direct programs, Google "[brand name] affiliate program" - if they have one, it'll be on the first page of results.

3. SaaS (software) programs

These are the goldmine. Subscription products pay you EVERY MONTH the customer keeps paying.

Look for software in your niche:

  • Email marketing tools (ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign, Mailerlite)
  • Web hosting (Bluehost, SiteGround, Kinsta)
  • Course platforms (Teachable, Thinkific)
  • Niche software (specific to your topic)

Recurring commissions of 20% to 50% are common.

4. Information products

Courses, ebooks, memberships. These often pay 30% to 50% commissions.

Most are found on:

  • ClickBank (clickbank.com) - mixed quality, careful here
  • Direct from creators - email them and ask
  • Kajabi/Teachable course creators - many run affiliate programs

Vet these carefully. Some are scammy.

How to find programs in YOUR niche

Open a Google Doc. Title it "Affiliate Program Research."

Step 1: List 10 brands you'd recommend

Just brain-dump. Brands you trust, products you've bought, software you use.

Example list (for a "beginner woodworking" niche):

  • DeWalt
  • Bosch
  • Lee Valley
  • Festool
  • Rockler
  • Wood Magazine
  • Fine Woodworking
  • Highland Woodworking
  • Wood Whisperer Guild (online courses)
  • Stumpy Nubs (YouTuber's merch)

Step 2: Google "[brand] affiliate program" for each

For each brand on your list, type into Google:

[brand name] affiliate program

Look for results that say things like:

  • "Affiliate program"
  • "Refer a friend"
  • "Partner program"
  • "Become an affiliate"

If they have one, you'll find it on the first page of results.

Step 3: Note the commission and cookie window

In your Google Doc, for each program, write:

Brand: [name]
Commission: [% or $ amount]
Cookie window: [days]
Type: [direct / network]
Notes: [anything specific]

Step 4: Apply to the top 3

Pick the 3 programs with:

  • Highest commission
  • Longest cookie window
  • Best fit for your niche

Apply to those.

How to apply

Each program is slightly different. Here's the general flow.

Step 1: Click "Apply" or "Sign up"

Usually on the program page.

Step 2: Fill out their form

Most ask for:

  • Name and address (legal info)
  • Tax info (same as Amazon)
  • Website URL or audience platform
  • Description of how you'd promote them

Step 3: Wait for approval

Most programs respond within 3 to 7 days. Some take longer.

If you're rejected, reapply in 60 days with more content on your site/channel.

Red flags - programs to avoid

Some programs are predatory. Watch for these warning signs.

Sign-up fees

If they want YOU to pay to JOIN, walk away. Real programs are free.

Promises of "guaranteed" income

Real programs say "earn up to X." Scammy programs say "guaranteed $5,000/month." That's a lie.

Pyramid schemes / MLM

If the comp plan involves recruiting other affiliates and taking a cut of their commissions - it's an MLM. Different game, different problems. Stay out unless you really know what you're doing.

Vague websites with stock photos

If the merchant's own website looks fake, the program is probably fake.

"Forced" recurring billing on customers

If the product auto-renews without clear consent and customers complain about being charged, your reputation will take the hit. Avoid.

If you got stuck

"There aren't any high-commission programs in my niche"

Two possibilities:

  1. You haven't searched broadly enough. Try Google searches for "[topic adjacent to your niche] affiliate."
  2. Your niche is too narrow. Module 3 might need revisiting.

"I got rejected by a program"

Normal. Reapply in 60 days with more content live.

In the meantime, focus on programs that approved you. You can always come back.

"I don't know how to evaluate if a program is legit"

Search for the program name + "review" or "complaints." If real affiliates are talking about it (positive or negative), it's real. If you can find nothing online, be cautious.

"I'm overwhelmed by all these networks"

Don't worry about all of them. ShareASale alone is enough to start. Sign up, browse their merchant catalog, apply to 5 to 10 in your niche. That's plenty.
