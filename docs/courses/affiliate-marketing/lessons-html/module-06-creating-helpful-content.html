Welcome back

Most affiliate content is bad.

It's bad because most affiliates write content for Google (or YouTube's algorithm) - not for actual humans. The result: keyword-stuffed nonsense that nobody finishes reading.

The cure: write for one specific human, not for an algorithm.

This module teaches you the framework that produces content people read all the way through, share with friends, and click your links from.

The 3 article types that earn

For content sites and blogs, three article structures produce 90% of affiliate income.

Type 1: "Best [thing] for [audience]"

The classic. Examples:

  • Best Cookie Sheets for Holiday Baking
  • Best Beginner Espresso Machines Under $400
  • Best Walking Shoes for Bad Knees

How it works

You compare 3 to 7 products in a category. Each has affiliate links. You give honest pros and cons.

Why people read it

They're shopping for something specific. They want a curated recommendation.

Why it earns

Readers are in "buying mode" by the time they get to the bottom. They click affiliate links because they need to choose.

Type 2: "[Specific problem] - here's what worked for me"

The personal story. Examples:

  • Lower Back Pain at 60 - The 3 Things That Finally Worked
  • I Tried 5 Espresso Machines Before I Found "The One"
  • How I Stopped My Sleep Apnea Without a CPAP Machine

How it works

You tell a personal story. You mention the products you tried. You include affiliate links.

Why people read it

Authenticity. They don't trust commercial reviews. They DO trust someone who tried things and reported results.

Why it earns

Trust converts. A reader who finishes a 2,000-word personal story is much more likely to click a link than a reader who skims a "best of" list.

Type 3: "How to [task] - step by step"

The tutorial. Examples:

  • How to Set Up Your First Vegetable Garden in a Small Space
  • How to Brew Cafe-Quality Coffee at Home
  • How to Train Your Senior Dog (Without Treats)

How it works

Step-by-step instructions. Each step might require a tool. Tools have affiliate links.

Why people read it

They want to learn. Tutorials get the most search traffic in many niches.

Why it earns

Readers who follow your tutorial need the tools. Your links convert because they're already convinced they need the tool.

A simple article template (for "best of" posts)

Use this structure for any "best [thing] for [audience]" article.

Title

Best [Thing] for [Audience] - [Year if relevant]

Examples:

  • Best Cookie Sheets for Holiday Baking 2026
  • Best Beginner Espresso Machines for Small Kitchens

Intro (200 words)

[Hook - relate to the reader's problem]

[Why this matters]

[What you tested and why your recommendations are trustworthy]

[Quick TL;DR mention of the top pick - so impatient readers don't bounce]

TL;DR section (50 words)

**Top pick: [Product name]**
**Best on a budget: [Product name]**
**Best for [specific use]: [Product name]**

The reviews (3 to 7 products, 250-400 words each)

For each product:

## [Product Name]

[1-sentence summary - "best for [use case]"]

[Why it stands out - 2 to 3 sentences]

[Honest pros and cons - 3 to 5 bullets each]

[Who it's for - 1 sentence]

[Affiliate link - "Check current price on Amazon"]

Conclusion (100 words)

[Restate top pick]

[Acknowledge other good options]

[Soft call to action - "Whichever you choose, [practical tip]"]

FTC disclosure

**Disclosure:** Some links above are affiliate links. If you buy
through them, I earn a small commission at no extra cost to you.
This helps support my work, and I only recommend products I'd buy
myself.

Total length: 1,500 to 2,500 words. Sounds like a lot. With AI helping, this is a 2 to 3 hour project.

Using AI to draft the article

Here's the workflow that actually works.

Step 1: Pick the topic and 5 products

Before opening AI, pick the topic and the 5 products you'll review. Use products YOU have actually used, or that you've researched extensively.

Step 2: Use Claude for the outline

Open Claude. Use this prompt:

You're a writer for [audience], creating a "best of" review article.

Topic: Best [thing] for [audience]
Products I'm reviewing: [list 5]

Create an outline with:
- Hook intro (200 words)
- TL;DR section
- Each of the 5 products as its own section, with the structure:
  - 1-sentence summary
  - Why it stands out
  - Pros (4 bullets)
  - Cons (3 bullets)
  - Who it's for
- Conclusion

For each product, make a guess about the audience pain point that
product solves best, so I can verify before writing.

Read the outline. Adjust as needed.

Step 3: Use Claude for the first draft

Same conversation:

Write the full article based on the outline. Audience: [audience].
Tone: [warm/expert/neighborly/etc.]. Length: 2,000 words.

Make the writing feel like a friend giving advice, not like a Google
search result. Use specific examples where you can.

You'll get a draft. Read it carefully.

Step 4: Edit ruthlessly

This is where you earn it. Common edits:

  • Cut filler. AI loves to say "in today's world" - cut it.
  • Replace vague phrases with specific ones. "Many people" -> "Mom of 3 in Ohio."
  • Add personal touches. A small story, a specific date, a real anecdote.
  • Spot-check facts. Anything specific (sizes, prices, features) - verify on the actual product page.
  • Read aloud. If it sounds robotic, rewrite that sentence.

This pass takes 60 to 90 minutes. It's why your article will rank better than the AI-spam articles you're competing with.

Step 5: Add affiliate links and disclosure

Where you mention each product, add the link. Add the disclosure at the very top.

Step 6: Publish

Hit publish on your site (Module 7 covers this) or Medium for now.

SEO basics (just enough)

You don't need to be an SEO expert. You need to know 3 things.

1. Pick a search-driven title

Title should match what people actually Google. Use Google's autocomplete:

  • Type the first half of your title into Google
  • See the suggestions
  • Pick the suggestion closest to your topic

Example: type "best cookie sheets for" - Google suggests "for baking," "for dishwashers," etc. Pick the one that fits.

2. Write your title and intro for the search query

Your title and first 100 words should clearly answer "yes, this article is about [their search query]."

If they searched "best cookie sheets for thin cookies" and your intro is about your grandma's life story - they bounce.

3. Use the search query naturally throughout

Don't keyword-stuff. But mention the search query naturally 3 to 5 times in the article. Especially in:

  • The title
  • The first paragraph
  • At least one subheading
  • The conclusion

That's enough.

If you got stuck

"I tried writing and it sounds robotic"

Three fixes:

  1. Read it aloud. Where you stumble, rewrite.
  2. Add 1 personal anecdote per article.
  3. Cut 30% of the words. Most AI drafts are 30% too long.

"Google isn't ranking my article"

It takes 6 weeks to 6 months for new articles to rank. Patience. In the meantime, share the article in Facebook groups, Reddit, and Pinterest to get early traffic.

"I'm afraid my article isn't good enough to publish"

Publish anyway. Your first article won't rank. Your fifth one might. Your twentieth one definitely will. The only way to get there is through the bad early ones.
