Module 2: The 3 Honest Paths

Time: about 45 minutes What you'll do: Compare the 3 ways to do affiliate marketing, pick ONE, and commit to it for at least 6 months.

Welcome back

There are three real ways to do affiliate marketing in 2026. Only three.

Anyone selling you a "secret 4th way" is selling you trouble. The three real paths:

  1. Content site (a blog with helpful articles)
  2. YouTube channel (videos reviewing or teaching)
  3. Email newsletter (regular emails to a subscriber list)

Each works. Each has trade-offs. Your job in this module is to pick exactly one.

The students who try all three at once almost always fail at all three. The students who pick one and stick with it for 6 months almost always succeed.

Path 1: Content Site (Blog)

What it is

A website with helpful articles in a specific niche. Examples:

  • A site about home espresso machines
  • A site about gardening for beginners
  • A site about dog training
  • A site reviewing kitchen tools

You write articles that answer questions people search for, and you include affiliate links to the products you mention.

How money flows

  • Person Googles "best espresso machine under $500"
  • Your article ranks in Google's top 10
  • They click and read your article
  • They click your affiliate link to Amazon (or wherever)
  • They buy
  • You earn a commission

Strengths

  • Compounds over time. A good article keeps earning for years.
  • Works while you sleep. Google sends traffic 24/7.
  • No face on camera. You can be invisible.
  • Easiest to scale with AI. Most of the writing can be AI-assisted.

Weaknesses

  • Slow. 6 to 12 months before meaningful traffic.
  • Google has gotten harder. Post-2024 algorithm changes hurt small sites.
  • Boring stretches. You'll write 30+ articles before you see results.

Time to first $: 6 to 12 months

Time to $1,000/month: 12 to 24 months

Best fit for

Patient people who like writing or research. Organized thinkers. People who'd rather not be on camera.

Path 2: YouTube Channel

What it is

You make videos in a specific niche. You review products, teach how to do something, or document a journey. You include affiliate links in the description.

How money flows

  • Person searches YouTube for "how to grow tomatoes from seed"
  • Your video shows up
  • They watch and trust you
  • They click an Amazon link in your description for a tomato seed starter kit
  • They buy
  • You earn a commission

YouTube also pays you ad revenue once you cross 1,000 subscribers + 4,000 watch hours.

Strengths

  • Visual trust. People trust faces faster than words.
  • YouTube algorithm is friendlier to niche channels than Google is to niche blogs.
  • Two income streams (affiliate + ad revenue).

Weaknesses

  • Camera nerves. You have to be on camera (or use AI avatars - we cover this in Module 6).
  • Production learning curve. Editing software, lighting, audio.
  • Consistency pressure. Slow weeks hurt your channel growth.

Time to first $: 3 to 9 months

Time to $1,000/month: 9 to 18 months

Best fit for

People who don't mind being on camera. Hands-on demonstrators. People who'd rather talk than write.

Path 3: Email Newsletter

What it is

You build an email list of subscribers in a specific niche. You email them regularly with helpful content and occasional product recommendations.

How money flows

  • Person signs up for your newsletter (often via a free guide you offer)
  • You email them weekly with helpful content
  • Once a month or so, you recommend a product with an affiliate link
  • They click and buy
  • You earn a commission

Strengths

  • You own your audience. No algorithm risk - you have their email.
  • High conversion rates. Subscribers who've read 20 of your emails trust you.
  • Manageable at any size. A 500-person list can pay $500 to $1,500/month.

Weaknesses

  • Starting from zero subscribers. You have to build the list (this is the slow part).
  • Writing pressure. Weekly emails compound, but you have to keep writing.
  • Deliverability challenges. Some emails go to spam. You have to learn this.

Time to first $: 6 to 18 months

Time to $1,000/month: 12 to 24 months

Best fit for

Good writers who like building relationships. People with niche expertise they can teach.

How to pick the one for YOU

Answer these 5 questions honestly. Then look at the scoring at the bottom.

1. Are you comfortable being on camera?

  • Yes, no problem -> +1 point for YouTube
  • I'd rather not -> +1 point for Blog or Newsletter
  • Definitely not -> +2 points for Blog or Newsletter

2. Do you prefer writing or talking?

  • Writing -> +1 point for Blog or Newsletter
  • Talking -> +1 point for YouTube
  • Either is fine -> 0 points

3. How patient are you with slow growth?

  • Very patient (12+ months without income is fine) -> any path works
  • Moderately patient (6+ months is fine) -> +1 point for YouTube
  • I want some signs of life within 3 months -> +2 points for YouTube

4. Do you have an existing audience anywhere (Facebook group, professional contacts)?

  • Yes, I could email them -> +2 points for Newsletter
  • No -> +1 point for Blog

5. Are you a researcher who likes diving deep into topics?

  • Yes -> +1 point for Blog
  • Sometimes -> 0 points
  • I prefer surface-level -> +1 point for YouTube

Scoring

Add up your points. The path with the most points is your starting point.

If two paths tie, pick the one you find more fun. Fun trumps optimization at this stage.

What about doing more than one?

Don't. Not for the first 6 months.

Here's why: each path requires you to learn its specific platform, audience, and workflow. Splitting attention across two means you're mediocre at both. Mediocre doesn't earn money in any of these paths.

After 6 months, if your first path is going well, you can layer in a second one (often: blog + newsletter together is a powerful combo). Module 8 covers when and how.

For now: pick one. Commit to 6 months. Don't second-guess.

If you got stuck

"I can't decide between blog and newsletter"

Default to blog. It teaches you the most foundational skills (SEO, content writing, niche selection). Once your blog is going, layering in a newsletter is much easier than the reverse.

"I'm scared of being on camera but I think YouTube is best for me"

Two options:

  1. Try a "faceless YouTube" approach (slideshow videos with voiceover). This works for some niches.
  2. Just try ONE video. Most camera fear melts after the first attempt.

If neither feels right, default to blog. You can layer in YouTube later.

"I have an audience already - 5,000 Facebook friends"

Then newsletter is your fastest path. Module 7 covers how to convert your existing audience into a list.

"I'm so excited to start - can I just begin?"

Yes! But finish the course first. Module 3 (niche selection) is the most important module - getting the niche wrong wastes 6 months of work. Don't skip ahead.
