Part 1: Measuring what matters

You can't improve what you don't track. But most beginners track too much.

Here are the only 4 numbers that matter in your first year.

Number 1: Articles or videos published

Track this weekly. Goal: 1 to 2 per week, every week.

If you're not hitting this, nothing else matters. More content beats better tools.

Number 2: Total monthly traffic

Track this in:

  • Google Analytics (free, install on your blog)
  • YouTube analytics (built-in)
  • Your email list size

Goal: traffic should grow month over month. Even if slowly.

Number 3: Affiliate clicks

Each affiliate program has a dashboard showing how many clicks your links got.

Track total clicks per month. Goal: clicks growing in proportion to traffic.

Number 4: Affiliate revenue

Total commissions paid + pending, per month.

This is the bottom line. Track it. But expect it to be near zero for the first 6 months.

Part 2: FTC compliance (the law)

The Federal Trade Commission requires disclosure on all affiliate content. This isn't optional.

The good news: it's easy to comply.

What the law requires

If you receive ANY benefit (money, free product, anything) for recommending a product, you must clearly disclose this to your audience BEFORE they see the recommendation.

The exact disclosure to use

For blog posts, at the top:

**Disclosure:** Some links in this post are affiliate links. If you
buy through them, I earn a small commission at no extra cost to you.
This helps support my work. I only recommend products I genuinely
believe in.

For YouTube videos, in the description AND verbally early in the video:

This video contains affiliate links. If you buy through them, I
earn a small commission at no extra cost to you.

For email newsletters, at the top of any email containing affiliate links:

This email contains affiliate links. I earn a small commission if
you buy through them.

Common compliance mistakes to avoid

  1. "Disclosure" only at the bottom of the post. It must be BEFORE the affiliate links.
  2. Vague language. "I get a tiny something" is not enough. "Affiliate link" or "I earn a commission" is required.
  3. No verbal mention on YouTube. The description alone isn't enough - you must SAY it on camera.
  4. Hiding behind tiny font. Disclosures must be conspicuous.

Penalties for non-compliance

  • Affiliate program bans (instant)
  • FTC fines (up to $40,000+ per violation)
  • Lawsuits (rare but real)

Just include the disclosure. Every. Single. Time.

Part 3: Your 90-day action plan

Print this. Tape it where you'll see it daily.

Days 1 to 30: Foundation

Week 1:

  • Confirm niche (Module 3)
  • Pick path: blog, YouTube, or newsletter (Module 2)
  • Set up Amazon Associates (Module 4)
  • Sign up for ShareASale (Module 5)

Week 2:

  • Publish first piece of content (article or video)
  • Set up basic analytics
  • Apply to 2-3 higher-commission programs (Module 5)

Week 3:

  • Publish second piece of content
  • If blog: start Pinterest account
  • If YouTube: post first video
  • If newsletter: set up MailerLite

Week 4:

  • Publish third piece of content
  • Run end-of-month review (template above)
  • Income expectation: $0 to $20 (this is normal)

Days 31 to 60: Volume

Goal: publish 8 to 12 more pieces of content. Build traffic momentum.

  • 1 to 2 articles or videos per week
  • 5 to 10 Pinterest pins per week (if applicable)
  • 1 to 2 helpful Facebook group comments daily (if applicable)
  • Weekly email to your list (when you have one)

End of day 60: 10 to 15 total pieces of content. Income: $0 to $100.

Days 61 to 90: Refinement

Goal: identify what's working and double down.

  • Look at top 3 articles by traffic - write 3 more on similar topics
  • Check Pinterest analytics - what pin styles get saves? Make more
  • Promote winning content harder (more group shares, more pins)
  • Apply to 1 to 2 more affiliate programs

End of day 90: 15 to 20 total pieces of content. Income: $50 to $500.

Days 91+: Compounding

By month 3, you should see the curve starting to bend. Search traffic ticks up. Pinterest is noticeable. Your list is growing.

Don't quit at month 3. Month 4 to 6 is where the curve really bends.

What to expect at each milestone

$50/month (typical: month 3-6)

The first real income. Modest. Don't celebrate yet - it's a sign things are working, not a sign of arrival.

$500/month (typical: month 6-12)

Real momentum. You should have 30+ pieces of content live, decent traffic, and a few articles consistently earning.

This is the proof your model works for your niche.

$1,500/month (typical: month 12-18)

Significant income. You're past "trying this" - this is real.

This is when most students start considering second income channels (e.g., adding YouTube on top of blog).

$3,000+/month (typical: year 2+)

The compounding takes over. Your top articles earn while you sleep. Your email list converts well. New content adds incremental revenue.

This is sustainable. Maintain the routine, don't burn out, keep showing up.
