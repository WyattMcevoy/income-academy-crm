# Services Agreement — Short Form

⚠️ **Template only. Not legal advice.** Have an attorney review before your first real use. State laws vary. Complex engagements need a full contract.

For small engagements (under $5,000 one-time or $2,000/month retainer), this short form is usually appropriate. For anything bigger, use a proper drafted agreement.

---

```
SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Agreement is between:

[YOUR LEGAL NAME / BUSINESS NAME] ("Contractor")
Address: [YOUR MAILING ADDRESS]
Email: [YOUR EMAIL]

and

[CLIENT LEGAL NAME / BUSINESS NAME] ("Client")
Address: [CLIENT ADDRESS]
Email: [CLIENT EMAIL]

Effective date: [DATE]

---

1. SCOPE OF SERVICES

Contractor will provide the following services to Client:

[LIST SPECIFIC DELIVERABLES — be concrete. Example:
- Weekly 500-word blog post, 4 per month
- Monthly editorial calendar planning call (30 minutes)
- Quarterly strategy review (60 minutes)]

Work NOT included in this agreement:
[LIST WHAT'S OUT OF SCOPE — prevents scope creep. Example:
- Social media posting or management
- Graphic design or image creation
- Paid advertising or ad copy
- SEO audits]

2. COMPENSATION

Project fee / monthly retainer: $[AMOUNT]

Payment terms:
[CHOOSE ONE:
- Monthly, on the [X]th of each month, via [METHOD]
- 50% on signing, 50% on delivery, via [METHOD]
- Milestone-based: $[X] on [MILESTONE 1], $[X] on [MILESTONE 2]]

Late payment: Invoices unpaid after [30] days will incur a [1.5%] monthly late fee.

Expenses: Client will reimburse pre-approved expenses over $[50] with receipt.

3. TERM AND TERMINATION

Term: [Month-to-month / 6 months / Through project completion]

Termination for convenience: Either party may terminate with [14] days' written notice.

Termination for cause: Either party may terminate immediately for material breach that isn't cured within [10] days of written notice.

On termination: Contractor will deliver all completed work and pass through any work-in-progress. Client will pay all earned fees and pre-approved expenses.

4. OWNERSHIP

Upon full payment, all final deliverables become Client's property, subject to:
- Contractor retains the right to use non-confidential aspects in portfolios and case studies
- Contractor retains pre-existing intellectual property (templates, frameworks, tools)
- Any third-party licensed content (stock images, fonts, etc.) remains subject to its original license

5. CONFIDENTIALITY

Both parties will treat non-public information shared during this engagement as confidential. This survives termination.

6. REPRESENTATIONS

Contractor represents that work will be original and will not infringe third-party rights (excluding reasonable use of AI tools, publicly licensed content, and references provided by Client).

Contractor uses AI tools (such as large language models) as part of the work process. Final deliverables reflect Contractor's professional judgment and editing.

7. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

Contractor's total liability under this Agreement is limited to the fees paid by Client in the [3] months preceding the claim.

Neither party is liable for indirect, consequential, or punitive damages.

8. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR

Contractor is an independent contractor. No employment, partnership, or joint venture is created.

9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Any dispute will first be discussed in good faith between the parties. If unresolved, disputes will be resolved by [binding arbitration / mediation / small claims court] in [STATE / COUNTY].

Governing law: [YOUR STATE]

10. ENTIRE AGREEMENT

This Agreement is the complete understanding between the parties. Any modifications must be in writing and signed by both parties.

---

SIGNED:

CONTRACTOR:
Name: _______________________
Signature: ___________________
Date: _______________________

CLIENT:
Name: _______________________
Signature: ___________________
Date: _______________________
```

## How to use

1. Copy the template into a Google Doc or your e-signature tool (HelloSign, SignWell, DocuSign)
2. Replace every `[BRACKETED]` field
3. Review the scope section carefully — this is where 90% of problems come from
4. Send to client for signature before starting work

## Common modifications

**For services work that may involve AI heavily:** keep clause 6 as-is — it covers AI tool use transparently.

**For monthly retainers:** add a clause about the minimum term (e.g., "Initial term is 3 months, month-to-month thereafter").

**For high-trust repeat clients:** you can skip the formal signed contract and use a simple email agreement referencing this document. Most freelancers over $500/month still use a signed contract — it's the cheap insurance that prevents expensive problems.

**For clients who push back:** "My [accountant / insurance / whatever] requires a signed contract for every engagement" is a professional answer. Most legitimate clients expect this.

## What this template does NOT cover

- Non-compete or non-solicit clauses (usually unnecessary for freelance; if client insists, talk to an attorney)
- Employment-related topics (this is specifically contractor, not employee)
- International clients — different jurisdictions, different requirements
- Work involving regulated industries (medical, financial, legal content)
- Equity or revenue-share arrangements
- Client-owned trademarks, licensing, or heavy IP transfer

For any of these, use a proper drafted contract.
