The 90-day arc

Big picture, here's what 90 days looks like for most successful students:

Days 1 to 30: Setup + Outreach

  • Service picked
  • AI tools mastered
  • 50 to 75 outreach attempts (Upwork bids, cold emails, network messages, etc.)
  • 1 to 3 prospect conversations
  • Maybe your first client (some land in week 2, most land in week 4 to 6)

Days 31 to 60: First clients + Refinement

  • 1 to 3 paying clients
  • First $500 to $1,500 earned
  • Workflow getting smoother
  • Pricing adjusted based on real data

Days 61 to 90: Steady income

  • 2 to 4 paying clients
  • $1,500 to $3,000 monthly run rate
  • 1 client repeat-purchasing or referring others
  • You're past "trying this" and into "this is working"

If you hit those milestones, keep going. If you don't, the diagnosis is almost always: not enough outreach. The fix is always: more outreach.

Your Week 1 plan

Week 1 is setup. Don't try to land clients yet. Build the foundation.

Monday

  • Confirm your service pick (review Module 3 if needed)
  • Write your one-paragraph "elevator pitch" for that service
  • Save it in a Google Doc called "My Business"

Tuesday

  • Set up Upwork account (if Upwork is your channel)
  • OR pick your local outreach niche (if cold email is your channel)
  • OR identify 3 to 5 Facebook groups (if community is your channel)

Wednesday

  • Set up your invoicing tool (Wave is free)
  • Set up a simple Google Sheet to track prospects: columns for Name, Email, Date Contacted, Status, Notes

Thursday

  • Write your first 3 outreach emails (don't send yet)
  • Have a friend (or the support team) read them

Friday

  • Send your first 3 emails
  • Mark "sent" in your tracker
  • Set a reminder to follow up in 5 days

Saturday and Sunday

  • Off. Don't work weekends.

Your Week 2 plan

Week 2 is volume. The goal is to send your first batch of outreach.

Daily routine (Mon-Fri)

  • 30 minutes: outreach (send 4 to 5 messages or apply to 2 to 3 Upwork jobs)
  • 30 minutes: practice using AI on a sample project (no client yet - just keep your skills sharp)
  • 15 minutes: check email, respond to any prospect replies

By end of week 2, you should have sent 25 to 30 outreach attempts.

Your Week 3 plan

Week 3 is conversations. You'll start getting replies. Some will turn into discovery calls.

Daily routine

  • 30 minutes: continued outreach
  • 1 to 2 discovery calls (15 min each) when prospects reply
  • 30 minutes: send proposals to anyone who's a real fit

By end of week 3, you should have had 2 to 4 discovery calls.

Your Week 4 plan

Week 4: first project. You should be working on (or about to start) your first paid project by now.

Daily routine

  • Deliver the work for your first client
  • Continue outreach (don't stop just because you have one client)
  • Use Module 5 workflow rigorously

By end of week 4: first client paid, or extremely close to it.

Weeks 5 to 8: Build momentum

By now you should have at least 1 paying client. Goal for this stretch: get to 2 to 3.

Routine

  • 30 to 45 minutes/day: client delivery work
  • 15 to 30 minutes/day: outreach (keep filling the funnel)
  • Weekly: ask current clients for referrals

Resist the urge to stop outreach because you have one client. You'll regret it the moment that client wraps up. Always be filling the funnel.

Weeks 9 to 12: Steady state

You should have:

  • 2 to 4 paying clients
  • $1,500+ monthly run rate
  • A workflow that runs almost on autopilot

Time to start thinking about the next phase.

Decisions to make in week 12

  1. Are you happy with the service you picked? If yes, double down. If not, switch to a different one for your next 90 days.
  2. Are your rates right? If you're slammed and saying yes to everyone, raise rates 20% on new clients.
  3. Are you ready for the eBay Reselling Done-With-You graduation tier? That's a much bigger income stream and a different relationship with us. (Apply via the link in your dashboard.)

When things go sideways

Three common situations and what to do:

"Day 30 and I haven't landed a client yet"

Most likely cause: not enough outreach.

Diagnosis questions:

  • How many outreach attempts have you sent? (Answer should be 50+. If less, that's the problem.)
  • Are your emails getting opened? (If you have a tracking tool, check open rates - aim for 30%+)
  • Are you getting replies? (If yes but no closes, the issue is your proposal. Review Module 5.)
  • Are you getting discovery calls but no closes? (Listen carefully on the calls. Often a tone issue.)

90% of "no clients yet" is solved by sending more outreach.

"Day 60 and my first client just left"

Normal. Some clients are short-term projects. Some find someone cheaper. Some realize they don't actually want the work.

Don't take it personally. Refill the funnel within a week.

"Day 90 and I'm completely overwhelmed"

You took on too many clients too fast. Time to:

  • Raise rates 20% (you can probably afford to lose a few)
  • Tighten boundaries (Module 7 review)
  • Stop taking new clients for 30 days

Then re-evaluate.
