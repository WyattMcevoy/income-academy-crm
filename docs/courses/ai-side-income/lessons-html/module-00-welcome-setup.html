Tool 1: ChatGPT

ChatGPT is the most popular AI chat tool. It's made by a company called OpenAI. We'll use it for many of the side hustles in Module 3, especially writing tasks.

Step-by-step signup

1. Open your web browser.

That's the program you use to get on the internet - Safari, Chrome, Firefox, or Edge. If you're not sure, look at the bottom of your screen for an icon. Safari is a blue compass. Chrome is a colored circle (red, yellow, green, blue). Click on it.

2. Find the address bar.

That's the long white box at the top of the browser window. It's where you'd normally type a website name.

3. Click once inside the address bar.

Any text in there should highlight. If it doesn't, press Cmd+A on a Mac (or Ctrl+A on Windows) to select everything, then press Delete.

4. Type the following exactly, then press the Enter key:

chat.openai.com

The Enter key is the big key on the right side of your keyboard.

5. Click "Sign up."

You'll see a page titled "Welcome to ChatGPT" with two big buttons: "Log in" and "Sign up." Click the blue "Sign up" button.

6. Choose how to sign up.

You'll see options:

  • Continue with Google
  • Continue with Microsoft
  • Continue with Apple
  • Or enter your email address

The fastest way: if you have a Gmail address (like yourname@gmail.com), click "Continue with Google." It'll show you a list of Gmail accounts you're already logged into in your browser. Click the one you want to use.

If you don't have Gmail, click "Continue with email." It'll ask for an email address and a password. Use an email you actually check.

7. Verify your email (only if you signed up with email, not Google).

Open a new tab in your browser (Cmd+T or Ctrl+T). Go to your email. Find a message from OpenAI - subject line will say something like "Verify your email." Click the big button inside that says "Verify email address." You'll be redirected back to ChatGPT, signed in.

8. Answer the welcome questions.

It'll ask for your name and birthday. Answer honestly. They'll also ask if you want to give them your phone number - you can skip this if you'd rather not.

9. You're in.

You should now see a clean page with a chat box at the bottom that says "Message ChatGPT..."

Your first conversation with ChatGPT

1. Click inside the chat box at the bottom of the page.

2. Type a question. Anything you'd ask a friend who happens to know everything. Some good starters:

  • Explain inflation to me like I'm a smart 12-year-old.
  • What's the difference between a Roth IRA and a Traditional IRA?
  • I have chicken, rice, broccoli, and a half a bottle of soy sauce. What can I make for dinner?
  • Write me a 3-paragraph thank-you note to my doctor.

3. Press Enter on your keyboard (or click the up-arrow button on the right of the chat box).

4. Watch the response appear, word by word.

This is normal - that's just how ChatGPT works. It's not broken. It's "thinking."

5. Reply. Ask a follow-up. "Can you give me an example?" or "Make it shorter." or "What about for someone in their 60s?"

6. Keep going for at least 10 minutes.

The point isn't to get useful answers - it's to feel comfortable with the interface. You're going to be using this tool to generate paid work for clients within a few weeks. Getting comfortable now is the first step.

Tool 2: Claude

Claude is the second AI chat tool we'll use. It's made by a company called Anthropic. Many users find Claude is better at writing tasks specifically. Its tone is usually a bit more thoughtful and natural-sounding. We'll use it heavily in Modules 5 through 8 when you start delivering real client work.

Step-by-step signup

1. Open a new tab in your browser.

Press Cmd+T on a Mac (or Ctrl+T on Windows). A blank new tab will open.

2. In the address bar, type:

claude.ai

3. Press Enter.

4. Click "Sign up."

You'll see a page titled "Claude" with a "Continue with Google" or "Sign up with email" option, just like ChatGPT.

5. Sign up the same way you did with ChatGPT.

If you used Google for ChatGPT, use Google for Claude too - it'll be one click. If you used email, use the same email you used for ChatGPT (so you don't have two passwords to keep track of).

6. Verify your email if you signed up with email (same process as ChatGPT - check your inbox for a verification message).

7. You're in.

You should now see a chat box that says "How can I help you today?" at the top, with a text input at the bottom.

Your first conversation with Claude

1. Type a question. Try the same question you asked ChatGPT.

For example: "Explain inflation to me like I'm a smart 12-year-old."

2. Press Enter.

3. Read the answer.

You'll notice Claude's response is slightly different - sometimes a bit more careful, sometimes a bit more conversational. Both tools are excellent. You'll learn when to reach for which one.

4. Have at least a 10-minute conversation. Same idea - get comfortable with the interface.

Now: notice what's different (5 minutes)

Quick experiment to wrap up.

1. Open both ChatGPT and Claude side by side in two browser tabs.

2. Pick a question you actually care about. Examples:

  • What's a good first online business for someone over 60 who's never done one before?
  • If I have 10 hours a week and want to make an extra $1,500 a month, what should I focus on?
  • Write me a cold outreach email to a local realtor offering to write their LinkedIn posts.

3. Paste the same question into both ChatGPT and Claude.

4. Read both answers carefully. Notice:

  • Where they agree
  • Where they give different advice
  • Which one's tone feels more like a person you'd want to work with
  • Which one's answer is more useful to you specifically

It happens to everyone, including people half your age. The whole reason this course exists is to walk you through every single thing slowly, no judgment.

If you hit a wall, you're not behind. You're just being careful, which is exactly the right energy for this work.

Here are the most common things that come up:

"I forgot my password"

Click "Log in" then click "Forgot password?" then enter your email. Check your inbox for a reset link. We've all done this. It's a 30-second fix.

"I'm getting an error message I don't understand"

Take a screenshot (Cmd+Shift+4 on Mac, Win+Shift+S on Windows) and email it to support@incomeacademy.biz with one sentence about what you were doing.

We respond within 24 hours, usually much faster. There's no question too small to ask. We'd rather you email five times this week than feel stuck and walk away.

"ChatGPT or Claude is giving me weird, broken answers"

Refresh the page (Cmd+R or Ctrl+R) and try again. AI tools occasionally have hiccups, especially during busy hours. If it's still acting strange, close the tab and come back in 5 minutes.

"It's asking me to pay for a Pro or Plus plan"

Both tools have a free tier. Don't pay yet.

The free tier is plenty for everything we'll do in this course. We'll talk about when (and if) it makes sense to upgrade in Module 5. Most students never do, and they still land paying clients.
