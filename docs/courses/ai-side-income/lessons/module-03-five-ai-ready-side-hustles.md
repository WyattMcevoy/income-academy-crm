## Service #1: Writing & Editing Assistant

### What it is

You help small businesses with their written content — blog posts, newsletters, product descriptions, web pages, sales emails — using AI to produce the first draft and your judgment to polish it.

### Who pays for this

- **Small local service businesses** (3-10 employees): plumbers, electricians, accountants, realtors, lawyers — anyone who knows they "should be blogging" but never has time
- **Solopreneurs** running consulting practices, coaching businesses, or online stores
- **Marketing agencies** that need overflow help on client work

### How much you charge

- **Per blog post**: $150 to $500 (with AI helping, you can write a 1,000-word post in about 2 hours, so $250 = ~$125/hour)
- **Per newsletter**: $200 to $400/month for a weekly send (4 emails)
- **Per product description**: $25 to $75 each, often sold in batches of 10-50

### Time per gig

- Blog post: 2-3 hours from intake to delivery
- Newsletter month: 6-10 hours total
- Product description batch: 30 minutes per 10 items once you have the workflow down

### AI tools you'll use

- **Claude** for drafting — Claude's writing is generally better quality than ChatGPT's
- **Grammarly** (free) for final polish
- **Google Docs** for delivery and client review

### Why it works for our age group

Older adults already write better than 90% of online content. We grew up reading newspapers and writing letters. **You bring legitimate writing taste**, which is the part the AI doesn't have. AI does the typing. You do the curating.

### What to call yourself

- "Writing & editing assistant"
- "Content writer for small businesses"
- "Newsletter ghostwriter"

Avoid "content marketer" — that's a more specialized title that means something different.

## Service #2: Bookkeeping Support (NOT full bookkeeping)

### What it is

You help small business owners with the **clerical** parts of their bookkeeping — categorizing receipts, drafting expense reports, generating invoices, light data entry — using AI to speed up the categorization and drafting.

**Important**: this is NOT the same as being a CPA, doing taxes, or giving financial advice. We are very specifically the **support** layer — the busywork the actual bookkeeper or CPA doesn't want to do.

### Who pays for this

- **Etsy and eBay sellers** with high transaction volume but no staff
- **Local contractors** (handymen, landscapers, painters) who hate paperwork
- **Solopreneurs** running consulting or coaching businesses

### How much you charge

- **Hourly**: $25-$40 per hour
- **Monthly retainer**: $200-$400/month for ~5-8 hours of monthly support per client
- **Per invoice / per receipt batch**: rare, but some clients prefer this

### Time per gig

- Per month per client: 5-10 hours
- A 3-client roster (achievable by Month 4) = 15-30 hours/month for $600-$1,200 income

### AI tools you'll use

- **ChatGPT** for receipt categorization (you can describe a receipt and ask it to categorize)
- **Claude** for drafting professional client memos and expense report narratives
- **Google Sheets** or **Airtable** for tracking
- **QuickBooks Online** (you'll be a "guest user" on their account)

### Compliance note (read carefully)

You are NOT a CPA. You are NOT giving tax advice. You are NOT certifying anything. You are doing **clerical support work** under the supervision of the business owner. This is legal in all 50 states and is exactly what a "bookkeeping assistant" or "data entry specialist" does in a normal company.

When clients ask things like "is this deductible?" — your answer is always: **"That's a question for your CPA. Want me to flag it for them?"**

We cover this in detail in Module 5. For now, just know: stay in your lane and you're fine.

### Why it works for our age group

Older adults are typically far more careful and detail-oriented than younger workers. **Small business owners specifically prefer hiring older adults for bookkeeping support** — they trust the carefulness. You're going to have an easier time landing clients in this niche than a 22-year-old would.

## Service #3: Social Media Ghostwriting

### What it is

You write LinkedIn posts (and sometimes Facebook posts) for busy professionals who know they should be posting but don't have the time. The professional gives you a 30-60 minute interview about their week, and you turn it into 8-12 thoughtful posts.

### Who pays for this

- **Realtors** who need consistent presence on LinkedIn and Facebook
- **Local consultants** (business, financial, life coaches)
- **Local attorneys** trying to grow their practice
- **Estate planners, financial advisors, insurance agents** — anyone who builds business through trust

### How much you charge

- **Monthly retainer**: $300 to $800 per client per month (for 8-12 posts)
- **Per post**: $30-$80 (rarely how it's structured, but useful for one-offs)

A 3-client roster (3 retainers) = $900-$2,400/month for ~10 hours/week of work.

### Time per gig

- Per client per month: ~5-8 hours
  - 1-hour voice interview with the client (recorded)
  - 2 hours processing the recording into themes
  - 3-5 hours drafting + editing 8-12 posts
  - 1 hour scheduling

### AI tools you'll use

- **Claude** for drafting (matches voice better than ChatGPT)
- **Otter.ai** or **Whisper** for transcribing the voice interview (free tier works)
- **Buffer** or **Hootsuite** for scheduling (free tier works)

### Why it works for our age group

You can have an actual conversation with a 50-year-old realtor better than a 22-year-old can. **Professionals over 40 strongly prefer hiring people their own age** for sensitive work like ghostwriting their public voice. They trust you to "get them" in a way they don't trust younger writers.

## Service #4: Customer Email Triage

### What it is

You answer the first response to customer emails for small business owners — the FAQs, the order-status questions, the easy stuff — using AI-drafted responses you edit and send. The owner only sees emails that genuinely need their input.

### Who pays for this

- **Etsy and eBay sellers** with 20-50 customer emails per day
- **Local service businesses** with steady inbound inquiry volume
- **Online course creators** with student support email
- **Small e-commerce stores** doing $20K-$200K/year

### How much you charge

- **Hourly**: $15-$25 per hour
- **Monthly retainer**: $300-$700/month for 1-2 hours/day of support
- **Per email batch**: rare, but some clients pay $1-$3 per email handled

A 2-3 client roster = $600-$2,000/month for ~10 hours/week.

### Time per gig

- 1-2 hours per day per client (clustered into a single morning or afternoon block)
- Most clients only need you 5 days a week

### AI tools you'll use

- **Claude** for drafting first-response emails (best at warm, professional tone)
- **Gmail** (or whatever the client uses) — you'll get delegated access
- **A simple Google Sheet** for tracking what you handled this week

### Why it works for our age group

Email customer service is where **patient, thoughtful people thrive.** A 22-year-old wants to type 4 emails and move on. You'll actually read what the customer asked and respond like an adult. **Clients value that more than speed.**

## Service #5: Transcription + Note Organization

### What it is

You take recorded audio (meetings, podcasts, interviews, lectures) and turn it into:

- A clean transcript
- A structured summary
- A list of action items / takeaways
- (Optional) A blog post or social media draft based on the content

### Who pays for this

- **Realtors** who record their listing-meeting walkthroughs and want notes
- **Therapists and coaches** who record session recaps with permission
- **Consultants** who record client meetings and need follow-up summaries
- **Course creators** who have hours of unedited video they want turned into written content
- **Authors and podcasters** who do interviews and need transcripts

### How much you charge

- **Per hour of audio**: $40 to $80
- **Per package** (transcript + summary + action items): $80 to $150 per audio hour
- **Monthly retainer** (for a steady client like a coach): $400-$800/month for 5-10 audio hours

### Time per gig

- 30-45 minutes per hour of audio (because AI does most of the transcription work)

### AI tools you'll use

- **Otter.ai** or **Whisper** (via the free tools at openai.com or similar) for transcription
- **Claude** for summarization, action items, and any derivative content (blog posts, etc.)
- **Google Docs** for delivery

### Why it works for our age group

Transcription is **a perfect work-from-home gig**. No client meetings required (just file delivery). You can do it on your own schedule. The AI does the heavy lifting, but **you have to actually listen and verify** — and that's where your patience and attention-to-detail beats every younger competitor.

## How to pick the one for YOU

Stop reading. Get a piece of paper. Write down your answers to these:

### 1. Which one sounds the most fun to you?

This matters more than people think. **You're going to do this work for 4-8 hours a week for the next 90 days.** If you hate it, you'll quit. If you enjoy it, you'll improve.

### 2. Which one fits your life best?

- Hate writing? Skip Services #1 and #3.
- Love writing? Lean into #1 or #3.
- Detail-obsessed and like spreadsheets? #2 or #4.
- Want maximum flexibility (no client calls)? #5.
- Want fastest path to first client? #1 (writing) — easiest to find.

### 3. Which one matches what you know?

If you ran a small business, you understand bookkeeping pain — Service #2 might be a fast fit. If you taught school, you might love structuring information — Services #1 or #5. If you sold real estate or insurance, you understand the LinkedIn ghostwriting pain — Service #3.

**Your past life matters here.** Use it.
