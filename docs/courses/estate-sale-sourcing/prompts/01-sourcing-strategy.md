# Prompts — Sourcing Strategy

Use these when planning your weekly sales route or evaluating a new market.

## ES-01: Weekly route planner for my metro

```
Act as an estate sale sourcing strategist. I live in [CITY/METRO] and
have [HOURS AVAILABLE, e.g. "Saturday 8am-1pm"] for sourcing this week.

Here are the listings I've found on estatesales.net for this weekend:
[PASTE LISTINGS — include addresses, start times, and any preview
photos or descriptions]

Rank these sales from highest to lowest priority for a reseller
focused on [YOUR CATEGORIES — e.g., "vintage tools and Pyrex"]. For
each, tell me:
- Estimated ROI potential (high/medium/low) and why
- Best arrival time
- What to look for first when I walk in
- Red flags that suggest skip-and-move-on

Then give me an optimized route (driving order) that fits in my
[HOURS AVAILABLE] window, accounting for typical sale busy times.
```

## ES-02: Discover new sales beyond estatesales.net

```
I've been using estatesales.net as my only source for estate sales in
[YOUR METRO]. I want to find sales that AREN'T on that platform —
which tend to be less competitive because fewer resellers know about
them.

What local channels should I be checking? List in order of typical
quality:
- Local Facebook groups (give me search terms)
- Newspaper classifieds (which papers serve [YOUR METRO])
- Craigslist "garage sales" section (when to check)
- Local downsizing / estate clearance services
- Nextdoor app
- Church bulletin boards
- Any other sources specific to [YOUR METRO] I should know about

For each, tell me how often to check and what red flags to look for.
```

## ES-03: Evaluate a new sourcing market

```
I've been sourcing in [CURRENT METRO] for [DURATION] and am
considering expanding to [NEW METRO / TOWN NAME], which is
[DISTANCE] from me.

For [NEW METRO], analyze:
- Demographics (older population = better estate sales typically)
- Wealth indicators (higher = higher-end items available)
- Existing competition (too many resellers = harder to compete)
- Categories likely to be abundant there (based on local history /
  demographics)
- Travel economics: is [DISTANCE] worth it for my typical per-trip
  ROI of [$X]?

Recommend whether to expand or skip, and if expand, which 1-2 sales
per month would make the trip worth it.
```

## ES-04: Thursday preview-night strategy

```
I was invited to a Thursday night preview for an estate sale company
in [METRO] that runs [FREQUENCY] sales. The preview is [NEXT DATE].
I've never attended a preview.

Explain:
- What to expect (how pricing works on preview night vs. Saturday)
- Etiquette (what I can/can't do, how to be taken seriously)
- Whether it's worth the time given preview prices are typically
  10-30% higher than Saturday
- When a preview is a MUST-attend (signals of a really good sale)
- How to build a relationship with this estate sale company so they
  invite me to future previews

Also: 3 specific questions I should ask the company rep when I'm there
that will impress them and get me on better inside lists.
```

## ES-05: Off-season / dead-zone strategy

```
It's [CURRENT SEASON — e.g., "January in Ohio"] and estate sale
listings in my area are half of what they are in peak season. I don't
want to lose 3 months of momentum.

What SHOULD I be doing during the off-season in [YOUR METRO]?
Consider:
- Inventory I've been sitting on — is this the time to list it
  strategically (January is prime eBay buyer season for self-gifting
  and resolutions)
- Estate sale companies — reach out and build relationships now
  while they're slow too
- Category research — go deep on one new category so I'm ready for
  spring
- Storage / workspace upgrades while I have time
- Any other smart off-season moves specific to my stage
  (hobbyist / part-time / serious reseller)

I'm currently at [STAGE] with [~$X inventory / month]. Tailor
recommendations to that stage.
```
