# Prompts — Pricing & Negotiation

Use these when you're at a sale haggling, or making the buy/walk decision.

## ES-20: Generate a respectful bundle offer

```
I'm at an estate sale. I've identified [NUMBER] items I'd like to buy.
Their prices are:
[LIST EACH ITEM WITH ITS PRICE]

Total as listed: $[TOTAL]

Write me a respectful bundle offer script — what I'd say to the
estate sale rep or family member to bundle these into one package
at $[YOUR TARGET PRICE]. Tone: friendly, patient, not cheap-buyer
energy.

Then: give me three variations of the script:
1. For a professional estate sale company (tight margins, known ROI)
2. For a family running their own sale (more emotional, less
   business-focused)
3. For the "last hour" of a sale when they want it all gone

Also: include a graceful walk-away line if they decline.
```

## ES-21: The "is this price firm?" opening

```
I'm at a sale and interested in [ITEM at price $PRICE]. In my
research, similar items sell for $[SOLD RANGE] on eBay, netting me
$[NET AFTER FEES] at best. I can't pay more than $[MY MAX] and have
it be profitable.

Write me the opening line to the seller — one that establishes
"asking about price flexibility" without sounding like I'm lowballing
or I don't understand value. This is a first interaction with this
seller and I want them to remember me favorably.

Then: the follow-up if they say "prices are firm today" (so I have
a move).

Then: the follow-up if they say "make me an offer" (so I have a
number ready that's neither too low nor at their ceiling).
```

## ES-22: Walk-away script

```
I've been negotiating on [ITEM] for 5 minutes and the seller won't
come down from $[THEIR PRICE] to my walk-away point of $[MY MAX].
Write me a walk-away script that:
- Acknowledges they're asking a fair price from their perspective
- Explains briefly why it doesn't work for me (without oversharing
  business details)
- Leaves the door open for a return visit at end of day if it doesn't
  sell
- Doesn't make them feel bad for not accepting

Tone: warm, not defeated, professional.
```

## ES-23: Last-hour "take it all" play

```
It's 30 minutes before the estate sale closes. There's a section
remaining that the sale runners probably don't want to haul back:
[DESCRIBE WHAT'S LEFT — e.g., "half of the garage tools, a few
kitchen items, stack of books"]. Original total prices were roughly
$[ESTIMATE].

Write me a "take it all" offer script. I'm willing to pay [$YOUR
NUMBER] to clear it — probably 25-40% of their original pricing.

Cover:
- Opening line (friendly, references the closing time)
- My offer with the specific items it covers
- How to handle "we can't go that low" (your counter)
- How to handle "do you want to also buy [harder to sell stuff]"
  (polite decline)
- Gracious close whether they accept or not
```

## ES-24: Building the relationship for next time

```
I just had a successful interaction with [estate sale company name
or family]. I paid [fair / low / high] price and left on good
terms. I want to be invited to their future sales and previews.

Write me the follow-up email / message I should send within 48 hours
to build the relationship. Include:
- A genuine thank-you (not performative)
- Something specific I appreciated (reference 1 detail so they know
  I actually remember them)
- A soft ask to stay on their list / get early notice of future sales
- My contact info and what I'm looking for long-term

Tone: like a neighbor, not a business development person.
```

## ES-25: Handling the "no negotiation" rule seller

```
I'm at a sale where the company has a "no negotiation on day 1"
policy. Everything is full price Saturday, half-price Sunday. It's
Saturday and there's a $[PRICE] item I think will sell for $[SOLD
RANGE] — profitable at full price but REALLY profitable at half.

Decision framework:
- What's my risk if I wait for Sunday half-price? (competition, item
  being sold, quality dropping if others have touched it)
- Is this item likely to be gone by Sunday in [CATEGORY]?
- Should I buy now or wait? Give me a go / wait recommendation based
  on the specifics.

And: scripting for the Sunday morning return if I decide to wait.
```
