# Prompts — Scaling Decisions

Use these when making strategic moves: specialization, reinvestment, or graduating to eBay Done-With-You.

## ES-70: Should I specialize?

```
I've been sourcing for [DURATION]. My current mix is:
- [CATEGORY 1] — [% OF SALES] of revenue, [AVG MARGIN]%
- [CATEGORY 2] — [% OF SALES] of revenue, [AVG MARGIN]%
- [CATEGORY 3] — [% OF SALES] of revenue, [AVG MARGIN]%
- Other/misc — [%]

Monthly revenue: $[AMOUNT]
Hours spent sourcing + listing: [HOURS]/week

Should I specialize (go deep on my strongest category) or stay
generalist?

Frame the decision:
- What does the data say (category with highest $ per hour?)
- What's the ceiling of each category in my metro?
- Risks of specialization (single-category risk, demand shifts)
- Risks of staying generalist (mediocre expertise, limited margin
  growth)

Recommend: specialize / stay generalist / hybrid.
```

## ES-71: Reinvestment strategy — where should next $1,000 go?

```
I have $1,000 of profit I can reinvest in the business. Options:
A) More inventory (sourcing budget — go to more sales, buy better
   stuff)
B) Better workspace (dedicated room with proper lighting, photography
   setup, shipping station)
C) Better storage (shelving, organization tools, inventory tracking
   software)
D) Learning (books, courses, conferences in my category)
E) Marketing / reach (paid placement on eBay, building an email list
   of repeat buyers, social content)
F) Tools (heat gun for label removal, photography light box, label
   printer, shipping scale)

Current state:
[YOUR CURRENT SETUP + WHAT YOU FEEL IS MISSING]

Recommend the HIGHEST-ROI reinvestment for my specific situation.
If it's a mix, give me percentages.
```

## ES-72: Am I ready for the eBay Done-With-You program?

```
The Income Academy eBay Reselling Done-With-You program is a
high-touch graduation tier where Income Academy warehouses, photographs,
lists, and ships inventory on my behalf while I focus on sourcing.

My current state:
- Monthly sourcing volume: $[AMOUNT]
- Monthly revenue: $[AMOUNT]
- Monthly profit: $[AMOUNT]
- Hours/week on sourcing: [HOURS]
- Hours/week on listing/shipping/admin: [HOURS]
- Current biggest bottleneck: [BOTTLENECK]
- Expansion goal: [WHERE I WANT TO BE IN 12 MONTHS]

Evaluate honestly:
- Am I ready for DWY based on the typical criteria ($1,500+/mo
  sourcing consistently, spending more time on post-sourcing work
  than sourcing)?
- If not, what needs to improve first?
- If yes, what should I expect from the transition?
- What questions should I ask on the consultation call?

Don't inflate my readiness. Honest go / not-yet / no.
```

## ES-73: When to fire my worst-performing category

```
I've been sourcing [CATEGORY] for [DURATION] and it's my worst
performer: [SHARE DATA — revenue %, margin %, hours].

Other data:
- My strongest category margins: [COMPARISON]
- Time spent per listing in weak category: [HOURS]
- Do I personally enjoy this category: [YES/NO/MEH]
- Inventory still on hand in this category: [$X at cost]

Should I:
- Drop the category entirely (stop sourcing, clearance the inventory)
- Keep sourcing only the BEST items in the category (strictest filter)
- Keep sourcing but change WHERE I sell (try Whatnot / FB Marketplace
  / specialty forums instead of eBay)
- Stay the course (this category just needs more time)

Give me the decision + implementation steps.
```

## ES-74: Year-end planning

```
It's [CURRENT DATE]. I want to plan the next 12 months of my reselling
business. Help me build a realistic plan.

Current state:
- Monthly revenue: $[AMOUNT]
- Monthly profit: $[AMOUNT]
- Hours/week: [HOURS]
- What I love: [ENJOYABLE PARTS]
- What I hate: [PAINFUL PARTS]
- Biggest constraint: [MONEY / TIME / SPACE / SKILL]

12-month goal:
- Revenue: $[AMOUNT]/mo
- Other outcome: [LIFESTYLE / SCALE / SPECIALIZATION GOAL]

Build me a quarterly plan:
- Q1: specific focus + 2-3 measurable goals
- Q2: specific focus + 2-3 measurable goals
- Q3: specific focus + 2-3 measurable goals
- Q4: specific focus + 2-3 measurable goals

For each quarter, include: what to START doing, what to STOP doing,
and one skill to build.
```
