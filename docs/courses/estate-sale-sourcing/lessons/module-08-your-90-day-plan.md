## The 90-day arc

### Days 1 to 30: Reconnaissance

**Goal:** visit 8 to 12 sales. Don't worry about big profit - focus on learning the local market.

- Saturday morning routine: 4 to 5 sales per day
- Buy modestly: $50 to $150 per Saturday
- Track every purchase in your Google Sheet
- List items as you go

**Expected income: -$100 to +$300 (NET)**

This sounds bad. **It's normal.** You're paying tuition.

### Days 31 to 60: Calibration

**Goal:** find what works in your local market. Lean into your best categories.

- Continue Saturday routine
- Note which categories are selling fast vs. slow
- Build relationships with 2 to 3 local sale companies
- Refine your bag (cash, supplies, route planning)

**Expected income: $400 to $1,200 (NET)**

The curve starts bending.

### Days 61 to 90: Profitability

**Goal:** consistent profitability. Hit $1,500/month.

- Same Saturday routine, now with sharper eye
- Listing 5 to 10 items per week
- Avg per-item profit: $30 to $60
- Days from purchase to sold: under 30 days

**Expected income: $1,500 to $3,000 (NET)**

Most students hit this in months 3 to 4.

## When things go sideways

### "Day 30 and I'm losing money"

Two diagnostic questions:

1. **Are you buying in your top 2-3 categories?** Or buying random "interesting" things? Random buying loses money. Discipline saves you.

2. **Are you actually listing things, or hoarding?** Items in your garage don't make money. **List within 2 weeks of purchase.**

### "Day 60 and I'm stuck around $500/month"

You're ALMOST there. Common stuck points:

- Pricing too low (bump 15% on next 10 listings, see what happens)
- Pricing too high (10%+ items still listed after 30 days, drop prices)
- Wrong categories for your area (check sold listings - what's actually selling locally?)

### "Day 90 and I want to scale"

Re-read Module 6. Run the 4-question framework. Apply for DWY if you qualify.

### "Day 90 and I want to quit"

Honest check:

- Did you give it a real shot? (12+ Saturday mornings)
- Did you hit your top categories?
- Is the issue effort, or local market?

If genuine effort + bad results -> consider another Income Academy course (AI Side Income or Affiliate Marketing might fit better).

If lazy effort + bad results -> try harder for another 30 days.

**No shame either way.** This isn't for everyone, and that's fine.

## Common pitfalls (avoid these)

### Pitfall 1: Becoming a hoarder

Over time, your garage fills with "interesting" items that don't sell. **Set a quarterly rule: anything 90+ days old gets sold or donated.**

### Pitfall 2: Driving too far

Within 6 months you might be tempted to drive 90+ minutes to "great sales." The math rarely works. **Stay within 60 minutes unless it's an obvious gold mine.**

### Pitfall 3: Overpaying for "deals"

You'll see items you "know" are valuable. **Always check eBay sold listings.** Trust your research, not your hunch.

### Pitfall 4: Storing without listing

This is THE killer. People buy 20 items, list 3, and then 17 items rot in the garage.

**If you can't list within 2 weeks, you're buying too much.**

### Pitfall 5: Burnout

Saturdays are sacred work. But weekends should also be FUN sometimes. Take a Saturday off every 4-6 weeks. **Sustainable beats intense.**
