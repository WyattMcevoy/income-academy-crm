## Welcome back

You've got items in your garage. Now we turn them into cash.

The pipeline is simple but requires discipline:

1. **Clean and prep** (30 minutes per batch)
2. **Photograph** (5 to 10 minutes per item)
3. **Research and list** (15 to 20 minutes per item)
4. **Manage the listing** (5 minutes per day)
5. **Ship and confirm** (10 minutes per sale)

By the end of this module, **you'll have a system to list 5 to 10 items per week consistently.**

## The 3 places to sell

You've got items. Where do you sell them?

### 1. eBay (the workhorse)

Best for: 80% of your inventory. Vintage items, kitchen, tools, clothing, anything shippable.

**Pros:**
- National (and international) audience
- Established trust system (buyer protection works for you and them)
- Fast payment via PayPal/Managed Payments

**Cons:**
- 13 to 15% in fees
- Shipping logistics on you

### 2. Facebook Marketplace (local)

Best for: heavy items (furniture, large decor) and quick local sales.

**Pros:**
- No fees
- Local pickup means no shipping
- Fast turnaround

**Cons:**
- Tire-kickers and time-wasters
- Lower prices than eBay (local market is smaller)
- Risky meetups (always meet in public or daytime)

### 3. Specialty platforms (advanced)

- Etsy (vintage items, crafty audience)
- Poshmark (clothing)
- Discogs (records)
- Specific collector forums

**Skip these for now.** Master eBay and Facebook first.

## Cleaning and prep

Before you photograph, every item needs basic cleanup.

### General cleaning supplies

- Microfiber cloths
- Mild dish soap
- Goo Gone (for sticker residue)
- White vinegar (for hard water spots, tarnish)
- Q-tips (for detail cleaning)

### Specific by category

**Kitchen items:** Wash with mild soap. Dry thoroughly. Remove old price stickers.

**Tools:** Wipe down with damp cloth. Light rust on metal: gently sand or use 0000 steel wool.

**Clothing:** Inspect for stains, holes. Lightly steam if wrinkled. **Don't wash unless necessary** (vintage clothing can shrink).

**Toys/games:** Wipe with damp cloth. Make sure all pieces are present (count them).

**Books:** Wipe covers with dry cloth. Check for inserts (love letters, photos, money - don't sell these).

### What NOT to do

- Don't deep-clean ANY antique or vintage item without research first (some "cleaning" destroys value)
- Don't paint, polish, or restore vintage tools (collectors want original patina)
- Don't iron vintage clothing (can damage delicate fabric)

**When in doubt, clean lightly and let buyers do their own restoration.**

## Photography

Good photos sell items. Bad photos don't.

### The 4 essential shots

For every item, take at minimum:

1. **Hero shot** - the item by itself, well-lit, shot straight-on
2. **Detail shot** - close-up of any maker mark, label, or distinguishing feature
3. **Damage shot** - any flaws, scratches, missing pieces (be honest!)
4. **Size reference** - item next to a ruler or common object

### How to take good photos

You don't need a fancy camera. Your phone is fine. But you DO need:

- **Good light.** Natural daylight near a window, OR a $30 ring light from Amazon.
- **Plain background.** A white sheet, foam board, or solid wall.
- **Steady hand.** Or use a phone tripod ($15 on Amazon).

Time per item: 5 minutes once you have the setup.

### Common mistakes

- Photos in dim light (looks low-quality)
- Photos with cluttered background (distracts from item)
- Hiding flaws (buyer leaves negative review when they see the flaw in person)
- Forgetting maker marks (buyers want to verify authenticity)

## Listing on eBay

The 30-minute walkthrough.

### Step 1: Open the eBay app or website

Click "Sell." If you don't have an account, you'll create one (free).

### Step 2: Take or upload photos

Drag your 4+ photos in. eBay accepts up to 12.

### Step 3: Title (this is critical)

Use this formula:

```
[Brand] [Item Name] [Specific Detail] [Condition] [Era/Year]
```

Examples:

- "Vintage Pyrex Butterprint 4-Quart Mixing Bowl 1960s Excellent"
- "Stanley #4 Hand Plane Smooth Bottom Type 11 Pre-1924 Restored"
- "1980s Nike Sportswear Sweatshirt Gray Tag Made in USA Size L"

**Use all 80 characters if you can.** eBay's search algorithm rewards keyword-rich titles.

### Step 4: Category

eBay auto-suggests. Usually right. Verify.

### Step 5: Item specifics

Fill out as many as possible. Brand, color, era, material, etc.

The more you fill out, the more likely your item shows in eBay searches.

### Step 6: Description

Use this template:

```
This is a [item description] in [condition] condition.

Details:
- Brand: [brand]
- Era: [era]
- Material: [material]
- Size: [size]
- Distinguishing features: [features]

Condition notes:
[List any flaws honestly. Cracks, chips, fading, missing pieces.]

Shipping:
[Will ship to [region]. Carefully packed. Combined shipping
available on multiple items.]

Returns:
[30-day returns accepted - or whatever your policy is.]
```

### Step 7: Pricing

Two options:

**Option A: Auction-style.** Lasts 7 days. Starts at $0.99 or your minimum. Best for high-demand items.

**Option B: Buy It Now.** Lasts up to 30 days. Set price at 90% of sold listings you researched. Best for most items.

**For beginners: use Buy It Now.** More predictable.

### Step 8: Shipping

- Calculated shipping (eBay calculates based on buyer's location) is usually best
- Heavy items: be honest about weight in the listing
- Set your handling time at 1 to 2 business days

### Step 9: List

Click "List." Done.

## Managing listings

Each day, spend 5 to 10 minutes:

- Check for messages (respond same day)
- Re-list any expired items (eBay does this automatically if you choose "Good Til Cancelled")
- Adjust prices on slow-moving items (drop 10% after 14 days)

---

## Shipping

When something sells:

1. eBay sends you a shipping label to print (cheaper than retail postage)
2. Pack the item carefully (bubble wrap, sturdy box)
3. Drop at USPS, UPS, or FedEx within 1 to 2 business days
4. Mark as shipped on eBay (with tracking number)

Total time per shipment: 10 to 15 minutes once you have a system.

### Shipping supplies you'll need

- Boxes (varied sizes - free from USPS for Priority Mail, or cheap on Amazon)
- Bubble wrap or packing paper
- Packing tape
- Shipping scale ($15 on Amazon)
- Permanent marker

Total starter cost: $50 to $80 for supplies. Lasts 2 to 3 months.

---

## Realistic timelines

Once you list an item:

- **Bid items:** 7 days, sell within 14 days typically
- **Buy It Now:** sells in 0 days to 60 days. Average: 14 days.
- **Slow movers:** still sitting after 30 days? Drop price 10%.

After it sells:

- Buyer pays immediately
- You ship within 2 days
- Buyer receives within 3 to 7 days
- eBay holds payment until buyer confirms (or 1 to 3 weeks for new sellers)
- Money lands in your bank account

**Total time from listing to cash: about 3 to 4 weeks for typical items.**

## If you got stuck

### "I priced something and it's not selling"

Two fixes:

1. Drop price 10% after 14 days. Repeat every 14 days.
2. Re-photograph with better lighting. Update title with more keywords.

### "I'm overwhelmed by all the eBay options"

Stick with the basics for your first 30 days:

- Buy It Now pricing only
- Calculated shipping
- 30-day return policy
- USPS Priority Mail for shipping

Add complexity (auctions, international, store subscription) only after you've sold 20+ items.

### "I'm scared of bad buyer experiences"

eBay's buyer protection works both ways. **Be honest in listings, ship promptly, respond to messages quickly** - and you'll have very few problems.

If you DO get a bad buyer: stay polite. eBay sides with reasonable sellers.
