## The 25% rule

Memorize this:

> **Buy at 25% (or less) of confirmed eBay sold price. Sell at 80% to 100% of eBay sold price. Pocket the difference minus 15% in fees.**

That math means:

- A Pyrex bowl with sold prices of $80 -> buy for $20, sell for $70, profit ~$40
- A vintage tool with sold prices of $200 -> buy for $50, sell for $180, profit ~$103
- A vintage shirt with sold prices of $120 -> buy for $30, sell for $100, profit ~$55

**If you can't get the item at 25% of sold price, walk away.**

This is hard the first month. You'll see items you love and want to pay 40% or 50%. **Don't.** The 25% rule is what makes the math work over a year.

## How to check eBay sold prices in 30 seconds

While you're at the sale, before buying:

### Step 1: Open the eBay app

If you don't have it: search "eBay" in the App Store, install it (free).

### Step 2: Search the item

Type the item name as specifically as you can:

- "vintage Pyrex Butterprint bowl"
- "Stanley #4 hand plane"
- "vintage Coach handbag"

### Step 3: Filter to "Sold Items"

In search filters: "Show Only" -> "Sold Items"

### Step 4: Note the price range

You'll see what the item ACTUALLY sold for in recent weeks. **This is your reference price.**

### Step 5: Calculate 25%

If sold price is $80, your max purchase price is $20.

Total time: 30 to 60 seconds. **Do this for any item you're considering paying over $10 for.**

## How to negotiate

Most estate sale companies allow some negotiation. Some won't budge. **You won't know until you ask.**

### The phrasing that works

```
"Would you take $X for this?"
```

That's it. Calm, polite, no theatrics.

If they say yes -> pay it.
If they say no -> ask "what's the lowest you can do?" once.
If they still say no -> "Okay, I'll pass on this one. Thanks for considering."

**Never argue. Never insult the price.** You're building a relationship - they remember rude buyers.

### When to negotiate

- Friday: light negotiation only. ($5 to $10 off on bigger items.)
- Saturday: medium negotiation. (10 to 20% off bigger items.)
- Sunday: aggressive negotiation. (Half price already, and 10% more is reasonable to ask.)

### When NOT to negotiate

- Items under $20 - just pay it or skip
- Items already marked down significantly
- When the company has a "no negotiation" sign

### The "stack and ask" technique

If you have multiple items, stack them and ask:

```
"What would be your best price for this whole stack?"
```

You'll often get 20% off when you bundle 5+ items together.

## The exit rule

This is the rule that prevents 90% of beginner losses.

> **If you can't sell this item within 60 days at 80% of sold price, you shouldn't buy it.**

When you pick up an item, ask yourself:

1. Can I list this on eBay in under 30 minutes? (Photos, description, shipping)
2. Will it sell within 60 days?
3. After fees and shipping, will I net at least 2x what I paid?

If "no" to any -> **put it back.**

This rule eliminates:

- "But it's so cool" purchases (cool doesn't pay rent)
- Items that need restoration before sale
- Items with no resale market
- Items too heavy/fragile to ship economically

## What to actually pay for common items

Quick reference. These are MAX prices for items in good condition.

### Vintage Kitchen

- Pyrex bowls (patterned): $5 to $30
- Pyrex casserole dishes: $5 to $40
- CorningWare casserole: $3 to $20
- Cast iron pans (un-rusted): $10 to $50
- Vintage Tupperware: $1 to $10

### Vintage Tools

- Stanley hand planes: $10 to $40
- Vintage chisels: $3 to $20
- Vintage measuring tools: $5 to $40
- Older Snap-On wrenches: $3 to $30

### Vintage Clothing

- Vintage band tees: $15 to $80 (research first!)
- Vintage Nike/Adidas: $10 to $80
- Vintage Levi's: $15 to $100

### Mid-Century Decor

- Brass candlesticks: $5 to $25 each
- Ceramic vases: $5 to $40
- Mid-century lamps: $20 to $80

### Vintage Toys

- Star Wars figures (loose): $1 to $20
- Vintage board games: $5 to $30
- Hot Wheels: $1 to $5 each

### Records and Books

- Common records: $0.50 to $3
- Specific genre records: $3 to $20 (research!)
- Vintage cookbooks: $2 to $10
- Vintage art books: $3 to $30

These are starting points. **Always verify with eBay sold listings before paying more than $10.**

## If you got stuck

### "I'm bad at negotiating"

That's okay - everyone is at first. **Just say "would you take $X?" and accept either answer.** That's the whole skill.

### "I keep falling in love with items and overpaying"

This is the #1 issue for beginners. Two fixes:

1. Set a hard rule: "If it's over $10, I check eBay before buying."
2. Keep a "passed on" list to remind yourself items come around again.

### "I bought something I shouldn't have"

Welcome to the club. Every reseller has a "lessons learned" pile.

Module 7 covers what to do with bad purchases. **Don't beat yourself up.** Every overpriced buy teaches you something.
