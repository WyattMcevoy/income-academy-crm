Why this works for our age group

Estate sale sourcing has a quiet advantage for adults 50+:

  • You recognize quality vintage items because you grew up around them
  • You have the patience to do the research
  • You can spot a real Pyrex piece, antique tool, or mid-century kitchen item from across a room
  • You're not embarrassed to dig through boxes (younger people skip those)

The 25-year-old Etsy reseller is shopping eBay for resellable items. You're shopping the source - estate sales - and getting items at 1/10th the price.

That's a permanent edge.

A word on what's realistic

Setting you up for success, not surprises. Here's what to actually expect:

  • Months 1 to 2: learning. You'll go to 8 to 12 sales. You'll buy some things. You'll lose money on a few. Net: -$100 to +$200.

  • Months 3 to 4: finding your eye. You'll start recognizing what sells fast. Net: $300 to $800/month profit.

  • Months 5 to 6: rhythm established. Net: $800 to $2,000/month profit, working 6 to 10 hours per week.

  • Months 7 to 12: you've got a system. Net: $2,000 to $4,000/month if you put the work in.


The first 2 months are the hardest. You'll make beginner mistakes. Everyone does. The ones who push through hit profitability around month 3.

Who this course is built for

You're going to thrive in this course if:

  • You enjoy treasure hunting and don't mind getting a little dusty

If you're still in - good. Let's get going.

The honest math

Let me show you the math that makes this work, so you understand WHY this can produce real income.

A typical Saturday

You drive to 4 estate sales (each one within a 30-minute drive). At each:

  • Sale 1: spend $30, find one good item
  • Sale 2: spend $20, find nothing special
  • Sale 3: spend $80, find 3 good items
  • Sale 4: spend $40, find 2 good items

Total spent: $170. Total items kept for resale: 6.

After research and listing

Average item resale price: $80. Total potential revenue: $480.

After eBay/PayPal fees (about 15%): $408.

Profit on this Saturday

$408 revenue - $170 sourcing cost = $238 profit for one Saturday morning.

If you do this 3 weekends per month: $238 x 3 = $714/month.

After 6 months of practice

Same Saturday, but now your "eye" is sharper. You spot better items, pay less, and know what flips fast.

Same $170 spent. Better items chosen. Average resale price: $120 instead of $80. Items kept: 8 instead of 6.

Total revenue: $960. After fees: $816. Profit: $646 per Saturday.

3 weekends per month: $1,938/month.

That's the curve. It bends in your favor as you learn.

If you got stuck

"Saturday mornings don't work for me"

Some sales happen on Fridays and Sundays too. They're rarer but exist. You can also focus on garage sales (which happen all day Saturday and Sunday) instead of estate sales.

You'll have less inventory to choose from, but the system still works.

"I don't have anywhere to store stuff"

Start small. A 4-foot section of garage shelf can hold 20 items, which is enough to test this for the first month. Most students upgrade their storage as they go.

"What if I buy something and can't sell it?"

Welcome to the learning curve. Every reseller has bought a dud. Module 6 covers what to do with slow-moving inventory (donate it, give it to family, or take it to a flea market for cash). You won't lose more than a few hundred dollars total in your first 90 days. That's tuition.
